## Study Session — Cryptographic Failures (CySA+)

Spent about 45 minutes on OWASP A02 Cryptographic Failures. The framing clicked better today: the failure isn't usually "someone broke AES." It's that the data was transmitted in plaintext because nobody configured TLS, or the certificate was self-signed and the app was silently accepting it anyway.

Had to look up the difference between **hashing** and **encryption** again — not because I didn't know it, but because the exam question phrasing trips me up. Encryption is reversible with a key. Hashing is one-way. The trap question is: "a company stores passwords using MD5 — what is the PRIMARY concern?" Answer is that MD5 is a fast hash, which means it's easy to crack with a GPU. The concern isn't that it's reversible; it's that the hash is weak.

Wrote this up properly in [[cryptographic-failures]] and linked it to the [[weak-cipher-suites]] note.
