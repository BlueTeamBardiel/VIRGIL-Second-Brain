# termination

## What it is
Like cutting off a garden hose mid-flow before disconnecting it from the tap — termination in security refers to the formal, controlled process of ending an employee's access, accounts, and organizational privileges when they leave a role. Precisely, it is the offboarding procedure that revokes credentials, recovers assets, and eliminates insider threat vectors when employment or a contract ends.

## Why it matters
A disgruntled employee at a financial firm was given two weeks' notice but retained full VPN and database access during that period; they exfiltrated 50,000 customer records before their last day. Immediate access revocation at the moment of termination — not at the end of a notice period — is the critical defensive control that prevents this insider threat scenario.

## Key facts
- **Immediate account disablement** is the priority: disable accounts *before* or *simultaneous with* notifying the employee, especially for involuntary terminations
- **Exit interviews** serve a dual purpose: gathering institutional knowledge and identifying any security violations or data taken
- **Asset recovery** must include laptops, badges, tokens, and MFA devices — physical items that could provide residual access
- **Principle of least privilege** review is triggered: all shared accounts or credentials the employee knew (service accounts, API keys) must be rotated
- Termination procedures are a core element of **personnel security policies** tested on Security+ and appear under the "Governance, Risk & Compliance" domain; improper termination is classified as an **insider threat** risk
