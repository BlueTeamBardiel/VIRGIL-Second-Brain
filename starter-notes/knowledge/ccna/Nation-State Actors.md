# Nation-State Actors

## What it is
Think of them like the special forces of hacking — instead of robbing a corner store for quick cash, they're conducting a years-long covert operation with military funding, intelligence resources, and geopolitical objectives. Nation-state actors are government-sponsored threat actors who conduct cyberattacks to achieve strategic national goals such as espionage, sabotage, influence operations, or intellectual property theft.

## Why it matters
In 2020, the SolarWinds supply chain attack — attributed to Russian SVR-linked group APT29 (Cozy Bear) — compromised ~18,000 organizations including US Treasury and Homeland Security by trojanizing a legitimate software update. Defenders must treat nation-state intrusions differently: assume long dwell times (often 200+ days undetected), sophisticated lateral movement, and the adversary's willingness to burn expensive zero-days rather than make noise.

## Key facts
- Nation-state actors are characterized by **advanced persistence, massive resources, and low financial motivation** — they want access, not ransoms
- They are often categorized as **APTs (Advanced Persistent Threats)** — groups like APT28 (Russia), APT41 (China), Lazarus Group (North Korea)
- Primary objectives include **espionage, critical infrastructure disruption, IP theft, and election interference** — not opportunistic crime
- North Korea's Lazarus Group is unique: it blends **nation-state espionage with financially motivated attacks** (e.g., $81M Bangladesh Bank heist) to fund the regime
- On Security+/CySA+, nation-state actors are classified as the **highest capability, highest sophistication** threat actor type — above hacktivists, organized crime, and script kiddies
