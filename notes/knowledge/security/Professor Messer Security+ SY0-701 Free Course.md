## What Is It? (Feynman Version)  
The course is like a public library’s audiobook collection for a specific certification: free, structured videos that walk you from basic concepts to exam readiness without the cost of commercial training.  

## Why Does It Exist?  
Before this course, newcomers faced two hurdles: expensive, often outdated paid training, and scattered self‑study resources that left gaps. The course fills that void, giving a cohesive, up‑to‑date path to the Security+ exam, reducing the barrier to entry for aspiring security professionals.  

## How It Works (Under The Hood)  
1. **Content Delivery** – 121 bite‑sized videos (average 3–7 min each) hosted on a free online platform with captions and timestamps.  
2. **Structure** – Organized into sections that mirror the exam objectives, each video follows a clear flow: definition → real‑world example → key points.  
3. **Supplemental Materials** – 96‑page PDF notes that summarize slides, a downloadable practice quiz set, and links to live study‑group replays.  
4. **Progress Tracking** – Learners can mark videos complete, review the outline, and replay pop quizzes to gauge retention.  

## What Breaks When It Goes Wrong?  
- **Outdated Content** – If exam objectives shift and the course doesn’t update, students study wrong material and may fail.  
- **Platform Downtime** – Without access to videos or notes, learners can’t review and lose momentum.  
- **Incorrect Notes** – Misinformation in the PDF leads to misconceptions, which can cause exam errors or security misconfigurations in the field.  

## Exam Angle  
- **Testing Format** – Security+ blends multiple‑choice with performance‑based questions on the same concepts.  
- **Common Traps** –  
  - Confusing *confidentiality* with *integrity* (use “C‑I‑A” mnemonic).  
  - Mixing up the order of AAA: Authentication → Authorization → Accounting.  
  - Overlooking that Zero Trust means “never trust, always verify” rather than a set of tools.  
- **Key Takeaway** – Remember the exam wants *application* of concepts, not just definition recall.  
- **Mnemonic** – “C‑I‑A, AAA, Zero Trust” – three quick checks before answering a question.
