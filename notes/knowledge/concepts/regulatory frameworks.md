# regulatory frameworks

## What it is
Think of regulatory frameworks like building codes for skyscrapers — they don't tell architects exactly how to design every room, but they enforce minimum structural standards so the whole thing doesn't collapse on people. In cybersecurity, regulatory frameworks are legally binding or industry-mandated sets of rules that organizations must follow to protect data, ensure privacy, and maintain accountability. Unlike voluntary guidelines, violations carry real penalties: fines, loss of operating licenses, or criminal liability.

## Why it matters
In 2017, Equifax exposed 147 million people's data partly because they failed to meet basic patch management standards required under their applicable compliance obligations — and were later fined $575 million by the FTC. Regulatory frameworks like GDPR and HIPAA create enforceable floors that, when ignored, expose organizations to massive financial and reputational damage. Compliance audits directly pressure organizations to implement controls they might otherwise skip.

## Key facts
- **HIPAA** governs protected health information (PHI) in the U.S.; the Security Rule specifically mandates administrative, physical, and technical safeguards
- **PCI DSS** applies to any organization processing credit card data; requires 12 core requirements including network segmentation and encryption of cardholder data at rest
- **GDPR** (EU) mandates breach notification within **72 hours** and grants individuals the "right to be forgotten"; fines can reach €20 million or 4% of global annual revenue
- **FISMA** requires U.S. federal agencies to implement NIST-based security controls and undergo annual security reviews
- Frameworks are often **mapped to controls** (e.g., PCI DSS maps to NIST 800-53), allowing organizations to satisfy multiple frameworks with overlapping control sets
