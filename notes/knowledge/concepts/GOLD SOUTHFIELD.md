# GOLD SOUTHFIELD

## What it is
Think of GOLD SOUTHFIELD as the franchise headquarters behind a criminal empire — they don't rob the banks themselves, they license the tools and infrastructure to those who do. GOLD SOUTHFIELD is a financially motivated threat actor group identified by Secureworks as the operators and developers behind the **REvil (Sodinokibi) ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS)** platform. They recruit affiliates, maintain the malware codebase, and collect a percentage cut of every ransom paid.

## Why it matters
In July 2021, GOLD SOUTHFIELD's infrastructure underpinned the **Kaseya VSA supply chain attack**, where affiliates exploited a zero-day in Kaseya's IT management software to encrypt over 1,500 businesses simultaneously. This incident demonstrated how a single RaaS operator can weaponize trusted software distribution channels to achieve mass impact — making attribution and containment exponentially harder than tracking a single threat actor.

## Key facts
- Tracked by Secureworks CTU (Counter Threat Unit); other vendors may refer to overlapping activity as **Pinchy Spider** (CrowdStrike naming)
- REvil/Sodinokibi first appeared in **April 2019**, emerging after GandCrab ransomware retired
- Operated a **"Happy Blog"** leak site on Tor to pressure victims via double extortion (encrypt + threaten to publish stolen data)
- Took approximately **60–70% of ransom proceeds**; affiliates kept the remainder — a standard RaaS revenue split model
- REvil infrastructure was disrupted by a **multi-nation law enforcement operation in January 2022**, resulting in arrests of alleged members in Russia
