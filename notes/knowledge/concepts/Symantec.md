# Symantec

## What it is
Think of Symantec as the long-standing sheriff of a sprawling digital frontier — one who's been patrolling the territory since before most users knew the internet existed. Symantec is a major cybersecurity vendor historically known for endpoint protection, threat intelligence, and enterprise security products, now operating primarily under Broadcom after its enterprise division was acquired in 2019.

## Why it matters
In 2017, Symantec's threat intelligence team identified the Dragonfly 2.0 campaign — a sophisticated nation-state attack targeting energy sector ICS/SCADA systems across the US and Europe. Their telemetry across millions of endpoints gave them the visibility to correlate indicators of compromise that individual organizations would have missed entirely, demonstrating how large security vendors function as shared early-warning systems.

## Key facts
- Symantec Endpoint Protection (SEP) combines signature-based AV, behavioral analysis, IPS, and application control in a single agent — relevant for understanding layered endpoint defense
- Symantec operates one of the largest civilian threat intelligence networks, the Global Intelligence Network (GIN), processing billions of events daily
- Norton (consumer) and Symantec Enterprise products were formally separated; Broadcom owns enterprise assets while NortonLifeLock (now Gen Digital) holds consumer products
- Symantec SSL/TLS certificates were distrusted by major browsers in 2018 after Google found CA policy violations — a landmark PKI trust incident
- Their Blue Coat acquisition (2016) added web proxy and network traffic analysis capabilities critical for DLP and SSL inspection
