# CYOD

## What it is
Like a restaurant that lets you pick from a curated menu rather than cooking anything you want at home — CYOD (Choose Your Own Device) lets employees select a personal device from a pre-approved list of corporate-vetted options. The organization maintains security control over the approved device catalog while employees gain some personal preference in their hardware.

## Why it matters
A financial firm adopting CYOD can pre-load MDM (Mobile Device Management) agents and enforce full-disk encryption on every approved device model before employees ever receive them. This prevents the nightmare scenario of an employee connecting an unvetted personal laptop loaded with outdated, unpatched software directly to the corporate network — a common lateral movement entry point attackers exploit.

## Key facts
- CYOD sits between BYOD (maximum user freedom, maximum risk) and COPE (maximum corporate control) on the device policy spectrum
- Organizations maintain a **pre-approved device list**, ensuring all hardware supports required security controls like TPM chips, BitLocker, or MDM enrollment
- Unlike BYOD, CYOD devices are typically **corporate-owned but personally chosen**, giving IT a legal basis to enforce full wipes and policy compliance
- Reduces the **attack surface variability** problem in BYOD — IT only needs to test and harden a finite set of known device models
- Common exam distractor: CYOD ≠ COPE — in COPE, the company both owns AND chooses the device; in CYOD, the employee chooses from an approved list
