# Security Posture

## What it is
Think of security posture like a castle's defensive readiness at any given moment — the height of its walls, the alertness of its guards, the depth of its moat, and how quickly it can raise the drawbridge. In cybersecurity, **security posture** is the aggregate measurement of an organization's overall cybersecurity strength, including its policies, controls, configurations, patch status, and ability to detect and respond to threats. It answers the question: "How well can we withstand and recover from an attack *right now*?"

## Why it matters
During the 2020 SolarWinds supply chain attack, organizations with strong security postures — those running network segmentation, behavioral monitoring, and least-privilege access — detected anomalous lateral movement far earlier than those with weak visibility and flat networks. The difference wasn't having *any* controls; it was the *quality and integration* of those controls, which is exactly what posture measures.

## Key facts
- Security posture is assessed through tools like **vulnerability scanners**, **Security Posture Management (CSPM/SSPM)** platforms, and **penetration tests**
- A **weak posture** often stems from unpatched systems, excessive user privileges, misconfigured firewalls, or lack of MFA — not necessarily absence of tools
- **Continuous monitoring** (a NIST RMF requirement) is how organizations maintain posture awareness over time, not just at point-in-time audits
- On the **CySA+ exam**, improving security posture is the core goal of vulnerability management, threat intelligence, and incident response workflows
- **Attack surface** is a direct input to posture: reducing exposed services, ports, and accounts directly strengthens it
