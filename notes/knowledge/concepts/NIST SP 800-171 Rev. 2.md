# NIST SP 800-171 Rev. 2

## What it is
Think of it as a building code for contractors who handle sensitive government blueprints — if you're not a federal agency but you touch federal data, you still have to meet the standard. Precisely, NIST SP 800-171 Rev. 2 is a set of 110 security requirements across 14 control families that non-federal organizations must implement to protect Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) residing in their systems. It was published in February 2020 and derives its requirements directly from NIST SP 800-53.

## Why it matters
In 2020, a defense contractor storing CUI related to aircraft schematics was breached because they lacked multi-factor authentication — a direct violation of 800-171's Identification and Authentication controls. This triggered DFARS clause 252.204-7012 penalties and loss of the DoD contract, illustrating that 800-171 compliance is a contractual requirement, not just a best practice, for anyone in the Defense Industrial Base (DIB).

## Key facts
- Contains **110 security requirements** organized into **14 families** including Access Control, Incident Response, Configuration Management, and System and Communications Protection
- Applies to **non-federal systems** handling CUI — it is NOT for federal agencies (those use NIST SP 800-53)
- Compliance is enforced via **DFARS 252.204-7012**, making it legally binding for DoD contractors
- Organizations must produce a **System Security Plan (SSP)** and a **Plan of Action & Milestones (POA&M)** to document gaps
- **CMMC (Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification)** is built on top of 800-171 requirements — Level 2 maps directly to all 110 controls
- Rev. 2 added clarifying language around **supply chain risk management** and removed the term "adequate security" to sharpen enforcement
