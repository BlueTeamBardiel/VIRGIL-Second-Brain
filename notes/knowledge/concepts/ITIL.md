# ITIL

## What it is
Think of ITIL as the recipe book a five-star restaurant uses to ensure every dish — whether cooked by a rookie or a veteran — comes out consistently and safely. ITIL (Information Technology Infrastructure Library) is a globally recognized framework of best practices for delivering, managing, and improving IT services throughout their lifecycle. It provides structured processes for everything from incident handling to change management, ensuring IT operations align with business goals.

## Why it matters
During the 2017 NotPetya attack, organizations without mature change management processes had unauthorized or unpatched systems running in production — exactly the gap ITIL's Change Management process is designed to close. A company following ITIL's Change Advisory Board (CAB) procedures would have required formal approval and security review before any system changes, reducing attack surface exposure. ITIL's Incident Management process also defines clear escalation paths, meaning security teams know precisely *who* does *what* when a breach occurs, cutting response time dramatically.

## Key facts
- ITIL v4 (current version) organizes practices around the **Service Value System (SVS)**, replacing the older lifecycle stages of ITIL v3
- **Incident Management** (restore service fast) is distinct from **Problem Management** (find root cause) — a critical distinction tested on CySA+
- ITIL's **Change Management** process requires a Change Advisory Board (CAB) to review high-risk changes, directly supporting security control over configuration changes
- **Configuration Management Database (CMDB)** is an ITIL artifact that maps all IT assets and their relationships — essential for scope definition during incident response
- ITIL aligns with **ISO/IEC 20000**, the international standard for IT service management, making it compliance-relevant
