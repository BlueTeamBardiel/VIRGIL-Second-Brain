# Red Team

## What it is
Like a theater director hiring professional hecklers to stress-test a play before opening night, a Red Team is a group of security professionals authorized to simulate real adversary tactics against an organization's defenses. They operate with full attacker mindset — using deception, persistence, and creativity — to find what defenders miss during routine testing.

## Why it matters
In 2020, the SolarWinds supply chain attack succeeded partly because defenders lacked visibility into their own build pipeline. Organizations running periodic Red Team engagements that specifically target CI/CD infrastructure and third-party trust relationships would have a much higher chance of detecting that style of lateral movement before nation-state actors exploited it.

## Key facts
- Red Teams focus on **objectives** (exfiltrate data, achieve domain admin), not just vulnerability counts — this distinguishes them from penetration testers
- Engagements operate under a **Rules of Engagement (ROE)** document that legally authorizes all simulated attack activity
- Red Teams use the **MITRE ATT&CK framework** to structure tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) to mirror real threat actors
- A **White Team** acts as referee — they know the engagement is happening and adjudicate disputes; the **Blue Team** (defenders) typically does not know in advance
- **Purple Teaming** is a collaborative variant where Red and Blue Teams share findings in real-time to accelerate defensive improvement rather than operate in opposition
