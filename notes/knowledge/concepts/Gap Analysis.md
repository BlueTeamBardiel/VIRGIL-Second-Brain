# Gap Analysis

## What it is
Think of it like a doctor comparing your current health to an ideal baseline — spotting where you're deficient before disease strikes. Gap analysis is the process of comparing an organization's current security posture against a desired target state (such as a compliance framework or industry standard) to identify what controls, policies, or processes are missing or insufficient.

## Why it matters
Before a major healthcare provider can achieve HIPAA compliance, they must run a gap analysis comparing existing access controls, encryption practices, and audit logging against HIPAA's required safeguards. Without this structured comparison, auditors discovered that one hospital had no documented procedures for revoking terminated employee credentials — a gap that had allowed a former employee to access patient records for six months after resignation.

## Key facts
- Gap analysis is a **proactive** control activity, not a reactive one — it identifies weaknesses *before* exploitation or audit failure
- Common target frameworks used as the "desired state" include **NIST CSF, ISO 27001, CIS Controls, and PCI-DSS**
- Outputs typically include a **remediation roadmap** prioritized by risk severity, cost, and regulatory requirement
- Gap analysis is distinct from a **risk assessment** — it measures *distance from a standard*, not probability/impact of a threat event
- On CySA+ exams, gap analysis frequently appears in the context of **compliance management** and the **vulnerability management lifecycle** (identify → assess → remediate → verify)
