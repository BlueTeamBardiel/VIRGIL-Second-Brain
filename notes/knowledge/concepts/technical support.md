# technical support

## What it is
Like a locksmith who fixes your door but could also copy your key — technical support is a trusted access role that can be exploited as easily as it can be helpful. Precisely, technical support refers to assistance services that help users troubleshoot, configure, or repair systems, often requiring elevated access or sensitive information to do so.

## Why it matters
Vishing (voice phishing) attacks frequently impersonate technical support personnel to socially engineer victims into surrendering credentials or granting remote access. The 2020 Twitter hack began when attackers called Twitter employees pretending to be internal IT support, convincing staff to hand over VPN credentials — ultimately compromising high-profile accounts including Obama and Musk.

## Key facts
- **Vishing via tech support impersonation** is a primary social engineering vector; attackers spoof caller ID to appear as legitimate internal IT or vendor numbers
- **Remote access tools (RATs)** like TeamViewer or AnyDesk are commonly weaponized in fake tech support scams — users are tricked into installing them, granting full system control
- **Pretexting** is the underlying technique: attackers fabricate a believable scenario (e.g., "your account shows suspicious activity") to justify their requests for access
- **Separation of duties** and **callback verification procedures** are core defenses — always verify tech support identity through an independent, pre-established channel before granting access
- The FTC recognizes **tech support fraud** as a multi-billion-dollar category; legitimate companies like Microsoft explicitly state they never make unsolicited support calls
