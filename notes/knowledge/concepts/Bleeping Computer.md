# Bleeping Computer

## What it is
Think of it as the AP wire service for the cybersecurity world — a newsroom that breaks stories on active malware campaigns before most vendors have even updated their signatures. BleepingComputer is a free online cybersecurity news publication and community forum founded in 2004 by Lawrence Abrams, specializing in malware analysis, vulnerability disclosures, and incident reporting for both technical professionals and general users.

## Why it matters
During the 2021 Kaseya VSA ransomware attack, BleepingComputer published real-time reporting — including sample ransom notes, IOCs (indicators of compromise), and victim communications with REvil — hours before official advisories from CISA or most vendors. Security analysts actively monitoring the site were able to pull early IOCs and begin threat hunting on their own networks before formal intelligence feeds caught up, demonstrating the site's role as a practical early-warning resource during active incidents.

## Key facts
- Founded in 2004 as a malware removal help forum; evolved into one of the most-cited cybersecurity news sources globally
- Regularly publishes exclusive interviews with ransomware threat actors, providing unique insight into adversary TTPs and negotiation behavior
- Maintains active technical forums where users submit malware samples for community-assisted analysis — a crowdsourced threat intelligence channel
- Frequently cited as a primary source by CISA, SANS, and major vendors when new vulnerability disclosures or ransomware variants emerge
- Not a vendor or tool — it is an open-source intelligence (OSINT) resource; analysts use it for threat awareness, not for automated detection or response
