# CMMI

## What it is
Think of CMMI like a Michelin star rating for software development kitchens — one star means you cook differently every night, three stars means every dish is documented, repeatable, and optimized. The **Capability Maturity Model Integration (CMMI)** is a process improvement framework that measures an organization's ability to develop, maintain, and secure software across five maturity levels, from chaotic ad-hoc processes (Level 1) to continuously optimizing processes (Level 5). It was developed by Carnegie Mellon's Software Engineering Institute and is widely used to benchmark organizational security and development discipline.

## Why it matters
During a vendor risk assessment, a financial institution discovered their payment processor operated at CMMI Level 1 — meaning security patches were applied inconsistently with no documented process. This directly predicted the breach that followed: without repeatable processes, a known vulnerability sat unpatched for 11 months. Organizations with CMMI Level 3 or higher typically demonstrate documented, standardized security procedures that auditors and regulators treat as evidence of due diligence.

## Key facts
- **Five levels:** 1-Initial (chaotic), 2-Managed (reactive), 3-Defined (proactive, documented), 4-Quantitatively Managed (metrics-driven), 5-Optimizing (continuous improvement)
- Level 3 is the **de facto baseline** required by many DoD contracts and FedRAMP-aligned programs
- CMMI maps closely to **NIST SP 800-53** and **ISO 27001** — higher maturity levels correlate with more complete control implementations
- CMMI assessments are conducted by **certified lead appraisers** and result in formal maturity level ratings
- In CySA+ context, CMMI informs **security program maturity** discussions alongside frameworks like NIST CSF tiers
