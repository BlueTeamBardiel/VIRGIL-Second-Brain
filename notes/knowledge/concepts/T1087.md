# T1087

## What it is
Like a burglar casing a building by checking the tenant directory in the lobby, account discovery is the act of mapping out what users and groups exist on a system before deciding who to target. Precisely, T1087 is a MITRE ATT&CK Discovery technique where adversaries enumerate local accounts, domain accounts, email addresses, or cloud identities to understand the target environment's structure.

## Why it matters
During the SolarWinds supply chain attack, threat actors used account enumeration to identify high-privilege service accounts and domain admin groups after initial access, allowing them to prioritize lateral movement toward crown-jewel systems. Defenders who monitor for bulk `net user`, `net group`, or `Get-ADUser` commands can catch this reconnaissance phase before privilege escalation occurs.

## Key facts
- Sub-techniques include **T1087.001** (Local Account), **T1087.002** (Domain Account), **T1087.003** (Email Account), and **T1087.004** (Cloud Account)
- Common tools used: `net user /domain`, `net group "Domain Admins"`, PowerShell `Get-ADUser`, and `ldapsearch` on Linux
- Attackers frequently query Active Directory via LDAP to harvest domain accounts without triggering traditional AV alerts
- Detection relies on **SIEM rules** flagging high-frequency directory queries and **User and Entity Behavior Analytics (UEBA)** for anomalous enumeration patterns
- This technique falls under the **Discovery** tactic (TA0007) and typically precedes Credential Access or Lateral Movement
