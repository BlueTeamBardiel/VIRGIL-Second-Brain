# ISO 27001 Annex A

## What it is
Think of Annex A as the master parts catalog for a car manufacturer — you don't install every part in every vehicle, but every legitimate part you *could* need is listed there. Annex A is a normative reference within the ISO/IEC 27001 standard that provides a catalog of 93 information security controls (as of the 2022 revision) organized into four themes: Organizational, People, Physical, and Technological.

## Why it matters
During a ransomware investigation at a healthcare firm, auditors discovered the attackers pivoted through an unmonitored supplier VPN connection — a gap that Annex A Control 5.19 (Information Security in Supplier Relationships) directly addresses. Organizations that perform a proper Statement of Applicability (SoA) against Annex A would have been forced to consciously accept or mitigate that supplier risk, creating a documented decision trail and likely a compensating control.

## Key facts
- The 2022 revision restructured controls from **114 controls across 14 clauses** (ISO 27001:2013) to **93 controls across 4 themes**, consolidating and modernizing the framework
- Annex A is *normative* but controls are not all mandatory — organizations must justify exclusions in the **Statement of Applicability (SoA)**
- The four themes are: **Organizational** (37), **People** (8), **Physical** (14), and **Technological** (34)
- Annex A maps directly to **ISO 27002**, which provides implementation guidance for each control
- A new control category introduced in 2022 is **Threat Intelligence (5.7)**, reflecting modern threat-informed defense practices
