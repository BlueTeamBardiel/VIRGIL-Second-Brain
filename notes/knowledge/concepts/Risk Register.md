# Risk Register

## What it is
Think of it like a ship captain's logbook for dangers — every known hazard, its likelihood, and what you're doing about it, all in one place. A risk register is a formal document that records identified risks, their likelihood and impact ratings, assigned owners, and the chosen treatment strategy (accept, mitigate, transfer, or avoid). It serves as the organization's living inventory of threats and the decisions made to manage them.

## Why it matters
During a post-incident review after a ransomware attack, investigators found the risk of unpatched internet-facing servers had been logged in the risk register months earlier — rated high likelihood, high impact — but the risk owner never acted on the mitigation plan. The register itself became legal evidence that leadership had accepted negligence rather than risk. This illustrates why registers must drive action, not just documentation.

## Key facts
- Each entry typically includes: risk ID, description, likelihood (1–5), impact (1–5), risk score (likelihood × impact), risk owner, and treatment status
- Risk treatment options are **Accept, Mitigate, Transfer, Avoid** — the register records *which was chosen and why*
- A risk register is a core output of the **risk assessment process** and feeds directly into the organization's **risk management framework**
- For CySA+/Security+: risk registers are maintained and reviewed on a recurring schedule — they are *living documents*, not one-time artifacts
- Residual risk (risk remaining after controls are applied) must be explicitly documented in the register, separate from inherent risk
