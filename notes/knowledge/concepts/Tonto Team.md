# Tonto Team

## What it is
Like a persistent neighbor who keeps trying different keys on your lock, this group never stops probing until something opens. Tonto Team (also known as CactusPete or Bronze Butler) is a Chinese nation-state advanced persistent threat (APT) group believed to be affiliated with the People's Liberation Army (PLA), active since at least 2009 and primarily targeting government, military, and critical infrastructure organizations across Asia and Eastern Europe.

## Why it matters
In 2021, Tonto Team was observed targeting Russian cybersecurity firms — including Group-IB — using spear-phishing emails with malicious RTF documents that exploited the CVE-2018-0802 Microsoft Equation Editor vulnerability. This is notable because attacking a cybersecurity company inverts the usual dynamic: the defenders become the target, potentially exposing threat intelligence about other investigations.

## Key facts
- **Primary targets:** South Korea, Japan, Russia, India, and Eastern European governments, defense contractors, and energy sectors
- **Signature tooling:** Uses the ShadowPad and Bisonal RAT malware families for command-and-control (C2) and lateral movement
- **Initial access method:** Spear-phishing with weaponized Office documents exploiting older vulnerabilities (CVE-2018-0802, CVE-2017-11882) remains a hallmark technique
- **Attribution:** Linked to PLA Unit 65017, making it a state-sponsored espionage operation focused on intelligence collection rather than financial gain
- **Defense relevance:** Their reliance on legacy Office exploits means patching cadence and email filtering are primary mitigations — reinforcing the basics matter most
