# T1598.004

## What it is
Like a con artist sending a fake bank survey to learn your account details before the actual heist, spearphishing for information doesn't deliver malware — it just asks questions. T1598.004 specifically covers adversaries using spearphishing via voice (vishing) or other service-based lures to **elicit sensitive reconnaissance data** from targets, gathering credentials, org structure, or technical details before a follow-on attack.

## Why it matters
In the 2020 Twitter Bitcoin scam, attackers called Twitter employees posing as IT support staff, convincing them to hand over VPN credentials — no malware required. The information gathered enabled account takeovers of high-profile targets including Obama and Musk. This shows how T1598.004 serves as a **precursor operation** feeding directly into initial access phases.

## Key facts
- Classified under **Reconnaissance (TA0043)**, not Initial Access — the goal is information gathering, not immediate compromise
- Distinguishes from T1566 (phishing for execution) because no malicious payload is delivered; the *response* is the prize
- Common pretexts include IT helpdesk impersonation, vendor surveys, HR onboarding calls, and executive assistant requests
- Targets are often **non-technical employees** (HR, finance, reception) who have access to org charts, employee directories, or system names
- Defensive controls include **security awareness training**, strict callback verification procedures, and flagging unsolicited information requests about internal infrastructure
