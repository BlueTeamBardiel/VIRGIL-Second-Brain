# GRU Unit 74455

## What it is
Think of it as the Russian military's equivalent of a bomb disposal unit — except instead of defusing explosives, they *plant* them inside critical infrastructure and democratic institutions. Unit 74455 is a specialized cyberwarfare division of Russia's GRU (military intelligence), formally attributed as the threat actor known as **Sandworm**, responsible for some of the most destructive nation-state cyberattacks ever recorded.

## Why it matters
In 2017, Unit 74455 deployed **NotPetya**, a wiper malware disguised as ransomware, initially targeting Ukrainian infrastructure via a poisoned software update to the MeDoc accounting application. The malware spread globally using the EternalBlue exploit, causing an estimated $10 billion in damages to companies including Maersk, Merck, and FedEx — demonstrating how supply chain attacks can turn a regional operation into a global catastrophe.

## Key facts
- **Formally indicted** by the U.S. DOJ in 2020; six GRU officers were charged for attacks including NotPetya, the 2016 French election interference, and the 2018 Winter Olympics disruption (Olympic Destroyer malware)
- **NotPetya** overwrote the Master Boot Record (MBR), making recovery impossible — it was a wiper, not true ransomware
- **Industroyer/Crashoverride** (2016) targeted Ukrainian power grid SCADA systems, causing blackouts for ~230,000 customers — attributed to Unit 74455
- Used **spearphishing** and **supply chain compromise** as primary initial access vectors
- Classified as an **Advanced Persistent Threat (APT)**, specifically tracked as **APT44** by Mandiant and **Voodoo Bear** by CrowdStrike
