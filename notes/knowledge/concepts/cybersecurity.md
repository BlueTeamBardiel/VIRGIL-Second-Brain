# cybersecurity

## What it is
Think of cybersecurity like maintaining a medieval castle: walls (firewalls), guards (monitoring), locked vaults (encryption), and drawbridges (access control) all work together — because a single unguarded postern gate collapses every other defense. Cybersecurity is the discipline of protecting digital systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access, damage, or attack through a combination of technical controls, processes, and human awareness. It operates across the CIA Triad — Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability — as its foundational framework.

## Why it matters
In 2017, the WannaCry ransomware attack exploited an unpatched Windows SMB vulnerability (EternalBlue), encrypting data across 200,000+ systems in 150 countries, including UK National Health Service hospitals forced to turn away patients. A single missing patch cascaded into a public health crisis — demonstrating that cybersecurity failures have physical, life-affecting consequences, not just financial ones.

## Key facts
- The CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability) is the foundational model for evaluating security controls on Security+/CySA+ exams
- Defense-in-depth means layering multiple independent controls so no single failure creates total compromise
- The NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) organizes security into five functions: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover
- Cybersecurity threats are categorized by actor type: nation-states, hacktivists, organized crime, insider threats, and script kiddies — each with different motivations and capabilities
- The average time to detect a breach (dwell time) was 194 days in 2023, meaning attackers often operate undetected for months
