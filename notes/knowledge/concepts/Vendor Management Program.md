# Vendor Management Program

## What it is
Think of it like a restaurant's health inspection system — you wouldn't let a supplier deliver uninspected ingredients to your kitchen just because they're cheap. A Vendor Management Program is a structured framework for evaluating, onboarding, monitoring, and offboarding third-party vendors to ensure they meet an organization's security, compliance, and operational standards. It treats every external partner as a potential attack surface that must be continuously assessed.

## Why it matters
The 2013 Target breach — 40 million credit cards stolen — began through an HVAC vendor with network access and weak credentials. A mature Vendor Management Program would have enforced least-privilege access, required the vendor to meet specific security controls, and monitored that third-party connection continuously rather than leaving it as an unguarded side door into the corporate network.

## Key facts
- **Due diligence** must occur *before* onboarding: review SOC 2 reports, conduct questionnaires, and verify compliance certifications (ISO 27001, PCI DSS, etc.)
- **Right-to-audit clauses** in contracts give organizations the legal authority to inspect vendor security controls — without this, oversight is toothless
- **Fourth-party risk** (your vendor's vendors) is a critical blind spot; a strong program maps the entire supply chain, not just direct relationships
- Vendors should be **classified by risk tier** (high/medium/low) based on data access and criticality, with review frequency scaled accordingly
- **Offboarding** must include revoking all access credentials, retrieving or destroying data, and confirming termination of connections — neglected offboarding is a common audit finding
