# JustCallMeKoko Discord

## What it is
Like a neighborhood watch group that accidentally lets a wolf join the chat, JustCallMeKoko is a Discord community associated with security researcher "Koko" (Joshua Rogers), known for vulnerability research and public disclosure of critical infrastructure weaknesses. It serves as an informal gathering point where security findings, tools, and discussions around responsible (and sometimes controversial) disclosure practices are shared.

## Why it matters
In 2022, Koko publicly disclosed vulnerabilities in Australian critical infrastructure — including water treatment and emergency services systems — after alleging that responsible disclosure attempts were ignored. This scenario illustrates the real tension in the disclosure ecosystem: when vendors fail to patch, researchers must decide between full public disclosure (maximizing pressure but increasing exploitation risk) and indefinite silence (protecting attackers, not defenders).

## Key facts
- Associated with high-profile critical infrastructure vulnerability disclosures targeting SCADA/ICS systems in Australia
- Highlights the **coordinated vulnerability disclosure (CVD)** debate: researchers, vendors, and CERTs each have competing timelines and incentives
- Koko's disclosures included water and sewage treatment systems — sectors covered under **critical infrastructure protection (CIP)** frameworks like NERC CIP and NIST SP 800-82
- The community represents the "grey zone" between ethical hackers and provocateurs — not illegal activity, but legally and ethically contested
- Relevant to CySA+ exam topics around **threat intelligence sharing**, disclosure ethics, and **ICS/OT security** risks
