# APT18

## What it is
Like a seasoned cat burglar who studies a building's blueprints for months before ever touching a lock, APT18 is a patient, methodical threat actor. APT18 (also known as Dynamite Panda or TG-0416) is a Chinese state-sponsored advanced persistent threat group known for long-term espionage campaigns targeting healthcare, defense, aerospace, and government sectors primarily in the United States.

## Why it matters
In 2014, APT18 was linked to the breach of Community Health Systems (CHS), one of the largest U.S. hospital networks, resulting in the theft of approximately 4.5 million patient records including Social Security numbers and personal health information. The attackers exploited the Heartbleed vulnerability (CVE-2014-0160) within days of its public disclosure, demonstrating the group's ability to rapidly weaponize newly published CVEs — a critical reminder that patch velocity directly determines organizational exposure windows.

## Key facts
- **Aliases:** Dynamite Panda, TG-0416; attributed to Chinese state-sponsored operations
- **Primary motivation:** Espionage — targeting intellectual property, PII, and national security data rather than financial gain
- **TTPs:** Spearphishing for initial access, use of custom malware (including Gh0st RAT), living-off-the-land techniques, and lateral movement via valid credentials
- **Rapid CVE exploitation:** Exploited Heartbleed within days of public disclosure, highlighting the danger of the patch gap
- **Target sectors:** Healthcare, defense contractors, aerospace, and government — sectors aligned with Chinese strategic intelligence priorities per MITRE ATT&CK Group G0018
