# Security Plus SY0-701

## What it is
Think of it as a driver's license exam for cybersecurity professionals — but instead of parallel parking, you're proving you can identify a phishing attack, configure a firewall, and respond to an incident. CompTIA Security+ SY0-701 is a vendor-neutral, entry-level certification exam validating foundational skills across threat management, cryptography, identity, and network security, updated in 2023 to reflect modern attack surfaces.

## Why it matters
When a hospital's IT team needs to hire someone capable of triaging a ransomware alert at 2 AM, Security+ SY0-701 serves as a baseline hiring filter — it signals the candidate understands concepts like segmentation, least privilege, and incident response without being trained on a single vendor's toolset. The 2023 revision added heavier emphasis on cloud, hybrid environments, and OT/ICS threats, reflecting real-world shifts in attack surface.

## Key facts
- **Five exam domains:** General Security Concepts (12%), Threats/Vulnerabilities/Mitigations (22%), Security Architecture (18%), Security Operations (28%), Security Program Management & Oversight (20%)
- **Exam format:** Maximum 90 questions (multiple choice + performance-based), 90-minute time limit, passing score of 750/900
- **Performance-based questions (PBQs)** simulate real tasks — configuring ACLs, analyzing logs, or identifying vulnerabilities in a network diagram
- **DoD 8570 approved:** Satisfies IAT Level II requirements, making it a mandatory credential for many U.S. government and military IT roles
- **Replaced SY0-601** in November 2023; key additions include zero trust architecture, AI/ML threat vectors, and supply chain risk management
