# TIA

## What it is
Like a security guard checking your badge *before* you enter the building rather than after you've already walked in, a Telecommunications Industry Association (TIA) standard defines the rules of the road for secure physical and network infrastructure. TIA is a standards body that publishes technical specifications governing structured cabling, data centers, and telecommunications systems used as the physical backbone of enterprise networks.

## Why it matters
When a penetration tester discovers that a corporate data center's cable runs lack proper physical separation between public and private network segments, they're likely citing a violation of TIA-942 — the data center infrastructure standard. Attackers who gain physical access to improperly designed cable infrastructure can tap unshielded runs or access patch panels to intercept traffic, making TIA compliance a first line of physical security defense.

## Key facts
- **TIA-942** defines data center tiers (Tier I–IV) covering redundancy, uptime, and physical security requirements — commonly tested in infrastructure security contexts
- **TIA-568** governs commercial building telecommunications cabling standards, specifying cable categories (Cat5e, Cat6, Cat6A) that affect eavesdropping resistance and signal integrity
- **TIA-222** sets structural standards for antenna towers and supporting structures, relevant to wireless infrastructure security
- TIA standards are often paired with **EIA (Electronic Industries Alliance)** standards — you'll frequently see them cited together as "TIA/EIA"
- Non-compliance with TIA-942 physical security zones (Entrance Room, MDA, HDA, ZDA) can expose critical infrastructure to insider threat scenarios
