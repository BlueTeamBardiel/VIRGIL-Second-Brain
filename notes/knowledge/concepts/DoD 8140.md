# DoD 8140

## What it is
Think of it like a federal licensing board for doctors, except instead of certifying surgeons, it certifies every person who touches a Department of Defense information system. DoD 8140 (formerly DoD 8570) is the Department of Defense directive that mandates baseline cybersecurity certifications for all personnel performing information assurance roles on DoD networks. It maps specific job roles to required certifications, ensuring that anyone with privileged access to military systems has demonstrated, verified competency.

## Why it matters
When Edward Snowden exfiltrated NSA documents in 2013, it exposed a critical gap: technical access without adequate vetting and role-based accountability. DoD 8140's successor framework, the Cyber Workforce Framework (DCWF), directly addresses this by tying specific workforce roles — like system administrators and vulnerability analysts — to mandatory credential requirements, making it harder for improperly qualified personnel to hold sensitive positions unchecked.

## Key facts
- **Replaced DoD 8570.01-M** — 8140 modernized and expanded the older 8570 framework, aligning with the NICE Cybersecurity Workforce Framework (NIST SP 800-181)
- **Three workforce categories**: Cyber IT, Cybersecurity, and Software Assurance — each subdivided into roles with specific certification requirements
- **Approved certifications include**: CompTIA Security+, CISSP, CEH, CySA+, CASP+, and others mapped to specific job codes
- **Mandatory for contractors too** — any contractor employee performing IA functions on DoD systems must meet 8140 requirements, not just active-duty personnel
- **Privileged Access roles (e.g., IASAE)** require higher-tier certs like CISSP or CASP+, while helpdesk-level IA roles may satisfy requirements with Security+ alone
