# T1562.006

## What it is
Like a burglar who cuts the phone line before breaking in so the alarm can't call the police, adversaries disable or tamper with security tools' ability to report events. T1562.006 specifically covers **Indicator Blocking** — the technique of preventing security products from collecting, forwarding, or alerting on telemetry that would expose malicious activity. The goal isn't to destroy the tool, just to make it blind and mute.

## Why it matters
During the 2020 SolarWinds compromise, SUNBURST malware checked for the presence of security and forensic tools and would delay or halt execution if certain processes were running — effectively blocking indicators of compromise from being generated or observed. Defenders who notice gaps in their logging timelines or missing telemetry windows should treat that silence as a loud alarm in its own right.

## Key facts
- Attackers commonly target **ETW (Event Tracing for Windows)** by patching `ntdll.dll` in memory to nullify logging calls before events reach SIEMs
- **Sysmon configuration tampering** is a frequent method — modifying or deleting rules so high-value events (process creation, network connections) are never logged
- Blocking indicators differs from T1562.001 (Disable or Modify Tools) — the tool keeps running, but its *output* is intercepted or suppressed
- Adversaries may use **IOCTL calls** or driver manipulation to prevent EDR kernel callbacks from firing
- Defenders can detect this via **log volume anomalies** — a sudden drop in expected event counts from an endpoint is a strong indicator of blocking activity
