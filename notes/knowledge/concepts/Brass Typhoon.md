# Brass Typhoon

## What it is
Like a quiet burglar who memorizes your entire house layout before stealing anything, Brass Typhoon is a Chinese state-sponsored advanced persistent threat (APT) group that prioritizes long-term reconnaissance and pre-positioning over immediate exploitation. Also tracked as VANGUARD PANDA, it is a PLA-linked cyber espionage actor known for targeting telecommunications, defense contractors, and critical infrastructure — particularly in the Indo-Pacific region.

## Why it matters
Brass Typhoon has been linked to intrusions targeting telecommunications providers to enable persistent surveillance of communications infrastructure — a capability that enables both intelligence collection and potential sabotage during geopolitical conflict. Understanding this group matters defensively because their tradecraft emphasizes living-off-the-land (LotL) techniques, making detection through signature-based tools extremely difficult without robust behavioral analytics.

## Key facts
- Attributed to the People's Liberation Army (PLA), distinguishing it from MSS-linked groups like APT40 or APT41
- Frequently leverages **living-off-the-land binaries (LOLBins)** such as PowerShell and WMI to avoid endpoint detection
- Known for targeting **telecommunications and satellite communications** infrastructure, consistent with signals intelligence (SIGINT) collection missions
- Overlaps with activity clusters associated with **Volt Typhoon** in terms of critical infrastructure targeting but represents a distinct operational unit
- Uses **valid credentials and VPN abuse** for initial access, making traditional perimeter defenses insufficient — requires zero trust architecture for mitigation
