# SANS Institute

## What it is
Think of SANS as the Navy SEAL training program for cybersecurity professionals — rigorous, practical, and respected by operators who need real skills under fire. SANS (SysAdmin, Audit, Network, Security) is a private organization founded in 1989 that provides information security research, training, and certification through hands-on, practitioner-focused courses.

## Why it matters
When a SOC analyst needs to triage a live ransomware incident, theoretical knowledge collapses under pressure — SANS training is explicitly designed around this. Organizations like the NSA and CISA formally recognize SANS certifications, and incident response playbooks across enterprise environments are frequently built on SANS-developed frameworks like the Incident Handler's Handbook. A GIAC-certified analyst is expected to perform, not just recite.

## Key facts
- SANS operates **GIAC (Global Information Assurance Certification)** — its certifying arm, offering credentials like GCIH (incident handling), GPEN (penetration testing), and GSEC (security essentials)
- The **SANS Top 20 Critical Security Controls** (now CIS Controls) were originally developed through SANS research and remain a baseline for enterprise hardening
- SANS runs **Internet Storm Center (ISC)**, a free threat intelligence service that monitors global attack trends in near real-time
- **NetWars** is SANS's competitive, gamified cyber range used for live skills assessment in CTF-style environments
- SANS courses map directly to frameworks like **MITRE ATT&CK** and **NIST CSF**, making them directly applicable to CySA+ exam scenarios and real job roles
