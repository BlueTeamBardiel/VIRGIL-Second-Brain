# SOW

## What it is
Like a restaurant order ticket that specifies exactly what the kitchen must prepare — no more, no less — a Statement of Work (SOW) is a formal document that defines the specific tasks, deliverables, timelines, and boundaries of a contracted engagement. In cybersecurity, it legally scopes what a vendor, pentester, or consultant is authorized to do.

## Why it matters
During a penetration test, a missing or vague SOW is how ethical engagements become legal nightmares. If a pentester pivots into a cloud environment not listed in the SOW, they may have technically committed unauthorized access under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA) — even if the client verbally approved it. The SOW is the legal shield protecting both parties.

## Key facts
- The SOW is a sub-document that typically lives beneath a Master Service Agreement (MSA), specifying work for one particular engagement
- In pentesting contexts, the SOW defines **scope** (IP ranges, systems, attack types), **rules of engagement**, start/stop times, and emergency contacts
- A SOW paired with a **Rules of Engagement (ROE)** document creates the full authorization framework for offensive security work
- Penetration testers should obtain a signed SOW **before** any testing begins — verbal authorization is legally insufficient
- SOWs are distinct from an NDA (confidentiality) and an MSA (general relationship terms); all three may exist simultaneously in a security engagement
