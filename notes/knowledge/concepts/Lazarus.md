# Lazarus

## What it is
Imagine a nation-state's intelligence agency built a hacking division the way a corporation builds an R&D team — with salaries, shift schedules, and long-term strategic goals. Lazarus Group is exactly that: a North Korean state-sponsored Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) actor, attributed to the DPRK's Reconnaissance General Bureau, operating since at least 2009. Unlike opportunistic cybercriminals, Lazarus conducts espionage, sabotage, and financially motivated attacks as instruments of national policy.

## Why it matters
In 2016, Lazarus orchestrated the Bangladesh Bank heist — using fraudulent SWIFT messages to steal $81 million by exploiting trusted interbank communication channels. This attack demonstrated that APT actors can target financial infrastructure not just for intelligence gathering, but to directly fund state operations, making Lazarus uniquely dangerous as both a geopolitical and criminal threat actor.

## Key facts
- **Attribution**: Linked to North Korea's Reconnaissance General Bureau (RGB); also tracked as HIDDEN COBRA by US-CERT and APT38 (the financially focused sub-group)
- **WannaCry connection**: The 2017 WannaCry ransomware attack — which disrupted the UK's NHS and 150+ countries — is formally attributed to Lazarus by the US, UK, and allies
- **TTPs**: Known for spearphishing, custom malware families (BLINDINGCAN, HOPLIGHT), supply chain attacks, and cryptocurrency theft to evade sanctions
- **Financial motivation**: Estimated to have stolen over $2 billion in cryptocurrency between 2017–2023, directly funding DPRK weapons programs
- **Sony Pictures (2014)**: Lazarus executed a destructive attack using the Destover wiper malware, combining data exfiltration with disk destruction — a hallmark dual-purpose operation
