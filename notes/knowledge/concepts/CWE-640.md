# CWE-640

## What it is
Imagine a bank that lets you reset your vault PIN by answering "What's your mother's maiden name?" — information your ex-spouse already knows. CWE-640 (Weak Password Recovery Mechanism for Forgotten Password) describes authentication reset flows that rely on security questions, predictable tokens, or easily guessable data, effectively creating a backdoor weaker than the front door. The vulnerability undermines the primary authentication mechanism by offering an alternate path with lower security guarantees.

## Why it matters
In 2016, attackers compromised Sarah Palin's Yahoo email by answering her password reset security questions (birthdate, zip code, where she met her spouse) using publicly available information — no hacking required. This illustrates how a robust password policy becomes irrelevant when the recovery path can be bypassed with a Google search. Defenders should enforce time-limited, cryptographically random reset tokens sent to a pre-verified channel (email/SMS) rather than relying on knowledge-based authentication.

## Key facts
- Password reset tokens should be generated with a CSPRNG, be single-use, and expire within 15–60 minutes
- Security questions are explicitly discouraged by NIST SP 800-63B due to their low entropy and public discoverability
- CWE-640 is a child of CWE-287 (Improper Authentication) in the CWE hierarchy
- Common vulnerable patterns include: predictable tokens (user ID + timestamp), tokens sent to unverified addresses, and tokens that never expire
- A correct implementation sends a short-lived, high-entropy token to an already-authenticated secondary channel, not a new one
