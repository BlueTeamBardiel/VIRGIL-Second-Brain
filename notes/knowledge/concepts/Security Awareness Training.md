# Security Awareness Training

## What it is
Like teaching factory workers to spot a frayed wire before it starts a fire, security awareness training conditions employees to recognize threats before they become incidents. It is a formal, ongoing program that educates users on recognizing social engineering, handling sensitive data, and following security policies. Rather than a one-time event, effective programs use repeated, role-based instruction to build lasting behavioral habits.

## Why it matters
In the 2020 Twitter hack, attackers didn't breach a firewall — they called employees, impersonated IT staff, and talked their way into admin credentials through vishing. A workforce trained to verify caller identity through a callback procedure to an official number would have broken that attack chain entirely. Human error consistently appears in over 80% of breach post-mortems, making trained users the last line of defense when technical controls fail.

## Key facts
- Security+ categorizes awareness training as an **administrative control** (not technical or physical)
- **Phishing simulations** are considered the gold standard metric for measuring program effectiveness — click rates should decrease over time
- Training must be **role-based**: finance staff needs invoice fraud scenarios; developers need secure coding awareness; executives need whaling/BEC scenarios
- **Frequency matters**: NIST SP 800-50 recommends ongoing training, not annual checkbox exercises — periodic reinforcement prevents skill decay
- **Measurable outcomes** are required for compliance frameworks (PCI DSS, HIPAA, ISO 27001) — training records must be logged and retained
