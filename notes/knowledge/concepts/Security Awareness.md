# Security Awareness

## What it is
Like teaching factory workers to recognize a gas leak before it becomes an explosion, security awareness is the ongoing process of training humans to identify and respond correctly to threats. It's the organizational program that transforms employees from attack surfaces into active defenders by building recognition of social engineering, phishing, and policy violations.

## Why it matters
In 2021, a Ubiquiti employee clicked a phishing link that gave attackers access to internal systems, resulting in a $46 million extortion attempt. A properly trained employee who recognized the hallmarks of a spear-phishing email — urgency, mismatched sender domains, credential requests — could have broken the entire attack chain before a single system was touched.

## Key facts
- Security awareness training is mandated by frameworks including HIPAA, PCI-DSS, and NIST SP 800-50, making it a compliance requirement, not just best practice
- Phishing simulations are the most measurable component: organizations track click rates before and after training to quantify behavior change
- The most effective programs use **continuous micro-training** rather than annual one-time sessions — short, frequent exposure produces lasting behavior change
- Security+ objectives specifically list awareness training as a control under the "Program Management" domain (CompTIA Objective 5.5)
- Role-based training matters: privileged users (admins, executives) require targeted training beyond general staff because they are higher-value social engineering targets
