# NIS2

## What it is
Think of NIS2 like a building code upgrade after a city's old regulations failed to prevent a string of structural collapses — broader scope, stricter enforcement, real penalties. The Network and Information Security Directive 2 (NIS2) is the European Union's updated cybersecurity legislation (effective October 2024), replacing the original 2016 NIS Directive to expand sector coverage, harmonize incident reporting requirements, and impose stricter obligations on essential and important entities.

## Why it matters
In 2021, a ransomware attack crippled Ireland's Health Service Executive (HSE), exposing gaps in healthcare cybersecurity governance across the EU. NIS2 directly addresses this by mandating healthcare organizations implement risk management measures, supply chain security controls, and report significant incidents to national authorities within 24 hours (initial warning) and 72 hours (detailed report) — creating real accountability where voluntary guidelines previously failed.

## Key facts
- **Two tiers of entities**: "Essential" (energy, transport, health, water, digital infrastructure) and "Important" (postal, waste management, food, manufacturing) — each with different supervisory intensity
- **Penalties**: Essential entities face fines up to €10 million or 2% of global annual turnover; Important entities up to €7 million or 1.4% of turnover
- **Management liability**: Senior executives can be held personally liable for cybersecurity failures — a significant departure from previous frameworks
- **10 mandatory security measures** include multi-factor authentication, supply chain security, encryption, vulnerability disclosure, and incident response planning
- **Scope expansion**: NIS2 covers medium-sized enterprises (50+ employees or €10M+ revenue) in covered sectors, compared to NIS1's narrower critical infrastructure focus
