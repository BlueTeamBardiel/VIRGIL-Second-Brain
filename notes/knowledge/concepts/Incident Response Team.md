# Incident Response Team

## What it is
Like a hospital trauma team where each surgeon, anesthesiologist, and nurse has a defined role the moment a patient crashes through the ER doors, an Incident Response Team (IRT) is a pre-designated group of specialists who execute a coordinated, structured response when a security incident is confirmed. They own the full lifecycle from detection through lessons learned, ensuring chaos doesn't dictate outcomes.

## Why it matters
During the 2020 SolarWinds supply chain breach, organizations *without* structured IRTs spent weeks just identifying who was responsible for forensic collection versus communication — burning critical containment time. Organizations with established IRTs could immediately activate playbooks, isolate affected Orion instances, and preserve forensic evidence before logs rotated out of existence.

## Key facts
- An IRT typically includes a **Team Lead, Security Analysts, Forensic Investigators, Legal/Compliance, HR, and Public Relations** — security alone is insufficient
- NIST SP 800-61 defines four IR phases: **Preparation → Detection & Analysis → Containment/Eradication/Recovery → Post-Incident Activity**
- Three organizational models exist: **dedicated team** (full-time IR), **distributed team** (part-time members with day jobs), and **coordinating team** (guides others but has no direct authority)
- The **CISO or IR Manager** typically has authority to authorize emergency containment actions, including network isolation, without waiting for change management approval
- A **lessons learned meeting** (required by NIST within two weeks post-incident) is not optional busywork — it's the primary feedback loop that improves future response
