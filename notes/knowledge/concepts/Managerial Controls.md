# Managerial Controls

## What it is
Like a pilot's pre-flight checklist and air traffic control tower — not the physical plane or its instruments, but the *oversight systems* that govern how flying happens safely — managerial controls are the administrative policies, procedures, and governance frameworks that direct how an organization manages security risk. They operate at the planning and decision-making layer, telling people *what to do* and *why*, rather than technically enforcing it.

## Why it matters
When Target suffered its 2013 breach, the technical controls (FireEye alerts) actually fired correctly — but management had no incident response policy mandating action on those alerts. The failure wasn't technical; it was managerial. A documented, enforced escalation procedure — a managerial control — would have changed the outcome.

## Key facts
- Managerial controls are also called **administrative controls** on Security+ exams — both terms appear and mean the same thing
- Examples include: **risk assessments, security policies, security awareness training programs, background checks, and change management procedures**
- They are most effective when paired with technical and operational controls — alone, they rely entirely on human compliance
- The **Security Policy** is the master managerial control; all other controls trace authority back to it
- On the CySA+ exam, managerial controls appear in the context of **governance, risk, and compliance (GRC)** frameworks like NIST RMF and ISO 27001
- They address risk through **avoidance, transference, mitigation, or acceptance** — the *decision* to act belongs to managerial controls
