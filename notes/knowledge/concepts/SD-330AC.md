# SD-330AC

## What it is
Like a Swiss Army knife that a field soldier carries instead of a full armory, the SD-330AC is a compact, portable shortwave antenna tuner/coupler designed for military and expeditionary HF radio communication. Precisely, it is a broadband antenna coupler manufactured by Antenna Specialists (later Codan) used with military HF radios — most notably the AN/PRC-104 and similar SINCGARS-adjacent systems — to match impedance across a wide frequency range without manual tuning.

## Why it matters
In signals intelligence (SIGINT) and tactical communications security contexts, understanding the hardware layer of military HF systems is essential for threat modeling. An adversary who can identify that SD-330AC couplers are in use can infer the radio family being operated, the likely frequency ranges in use (2–30 MHz HF band), and exploit predictable antenna radiation patterns to perform direction finding (DF) and geolocation of friendly forces — a real-world OPSEC failure vector.

## Key facts
- Covers the **2–30 MHz HF frequency range**, enabling long-distance skywave propagation communication
- Functions as an **automatic antenna coupler (AAC)**, matching antenna impedance to the radio's 50-ohm output without manual intervention
- Commonly paired with **military manpack HF radios** used in PACE (Primary, Alternate, Contingency, Emergency) communication planning
- Relevant to **COMSEC** because HF transmissions at these frequencies are highly susceptible to interception and direction finding by adversaries
- Understanding physical layer hardware like this is tested in **CySA+ and Security+** under the domain of network architecture and communication security fundamentals
