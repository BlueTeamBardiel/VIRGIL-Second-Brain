# Foreign Intelligence Service

## What it is
Think of a foreign intelligence service like a master chess player who operates twenty boards simultaneously — patiently, invisibly, years ahead of their opponent's awareness. Precisely defined, a Foreign Intelligence Service (FIS) is a government-sponsored organization tasked with collecting intelligence, conducting espionage, and executing covert operations against foreign nations, corporations, or individuals to advance national interests.

## Why it matters
In 2020, the SVR (Russia's Foreign Intelligence Service) orchestrated the SolarWinds supply chain attack, compromising ~18,000 organizations including U.S. federal agencies by injecting malicious code into trusted software updates. Defenders must understand FIS tradecraft because these threat actors combine nation-state resources, long dwell times (averaging 197+ days undetected), and zero-day capabilities that dwarf typical criminal actors.

## Key facts
- FIS actors are classified as **Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs)** — they prioritize stealth and long-term access over quick financial gain
- Common FIS objectives include **intellectual property theft, political espionage, critical infrastructure reconnaissance, and disinformation campaigns**
- Well-known FIS-linked APT groups: **APT29** (Russia/SVR), **APT41** (China/MSS), **Lazarus Group** (North Korea/RGB)
- FIS operations frequently exploit **trusted relationships** — supply chains, third-party vendors, and insider recruitment (human intelligence/HUMINT)
- Indicators of FIS activity include **living-off-the-land (LotL) techniques**, use of legitimate credentials, and targeting of classified or high-value intellectual assets — behaviors flagged by CySA+ detection frameworks
