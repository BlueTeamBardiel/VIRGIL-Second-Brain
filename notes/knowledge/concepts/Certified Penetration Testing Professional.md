# Certified Penetration Testing Professional

## What it is
Like a licensed locksmith who legally breaks into your house to prove your deadbolt is worthless, a C|PENT-certified professional is formally credentialed to conduct structured, authorized penetration tests across complex enterprise environments. The Certified Penetration Testing Professional (C|PENT) is an advanced certification offered by EC-Council that validates hands-on skills in real-world network penetration, exploit development, and advanced attack techniques. Unlike entry-level certifications, it requires candidates to compromise live machines in a proctored, multi-layered network range.

## Why it matters
During a red team engagement at a financial institution, a C|PENT practitioner discovers that internal segmentation between the DMZ and core banking network is bypassable via a misconfigured pivot point — something automated scanners completely missed. This credential ensures the tester has the skill to chain vulnerabilities across network segments, not just run canned tool outputs. The structured methodology prevents scope creep and legal liability while maximizing discovered attack surface.

## Key facts
- C|PENT is issued by EC-Council and requires passing a **24-hour or 48-hour live, proctored exam** on a cyber range — not a multiple-choice test
- Covers **advanced pivoting**, writing custom exploits, attacking IoT, OT/SCADA systems, and bypassing filtered networks
- Scoring **90% or above** automatically earns the **Licensed Penetration Tester (Master)** designation
- Distinguishes itself from CEH by emphasizing **practical exploitation** over conceptual knowledge
- Aligns with penetration testing phases: **reconnaissance → scanning → exploitation → post-exploitation → reporting** — directly mappable to PTES (Penetration Testing Execution Standard)
