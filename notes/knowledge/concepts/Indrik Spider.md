# Indrik Spider

## What it is
Think of a criminal franchise that started selling getaway cars, then realized robbing the banks themselves was more profitable — Indrik Spider followed exactly that arc. Indrik Spider is a sophisticated Russian-speaking cybercriminal threat actor group known for developing and operating the Dridex banking trojan and later pivoting to BitPaymer and WastedLocker ransomware operations.

## Why it matters
In 2019, Indrik Spider deployed WastedLocker ransomware against Garmin, encrypting critical systems and demanding a multi-million dollar ransom, disrupting GPS services and aviation databases globally. Defenders use this group's TTPs as a case study in how criminal organizations evolve from credential theft to high-impact ransomware extortion targeting operational technology.

## Key facts
- Attributed by CrowdCoin as operating since at least 2014, originally distributing **Dridex** via phishing emails with malicious Office macro attachments
- Pivoted to **BitPaymer ransomware** (~2017) and later **WastedLocker** (~2020), representing a shift from mass banking fraud to targeted "big game hunting"
- Uses **Evil Corp** as an alias name frequently cited in U.S. Treasury OFAC sanctions (2019), making ransom payments to them potentially illegal for U.S. entities
- Employs **SocGholish** (fake browser update framework) as an initial access vector to deliver ransomware payloads
- OFAC sanctions created a unique defensive lever: victim organizations could face legal liability for paying ransoms, incentivizing incident response over negotiation
