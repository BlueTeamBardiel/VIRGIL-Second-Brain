# User On-boarding

## What it is
Like issuing a new employee a keycard, a parking pass, and a building code all on their first day — user on-boarding is the formal process of provisioning identities, credentials, roles, and access rights when someone joins an organization. It encompasses account creation, role assignment, security training acknowledgment, and initial credential distribution within a defined workflow.

## Why it matters
Weak on-boarding processes are a direct attack surface: in 2020, the Twitter breach began partly because attackers used social engineering against newly on-boarded or insufficiently trained employees to gain internal admin tool access. A well-designed on-boarding process enforces least privilege from day one, ensures MFA enrollment before first login, and ties account activation to verified HR records — preventing ghost accounts from being created for non-existent employees.

## Key facts
- **Provisioning** during on-boarding should follow the **principle of least privilege** — accounts receive only the minimum access required for the stated role, not a copy of a senior employee's permissions.
- On-boarding is a critical phase for **identity lifecycle management**; failure to integrate with HR systems creates **orphaned accounts** when roles change.
- Security+ and CySA+ treat on-boarding as part of **Identity and Access Management (IAM)**, alongside off-boarding (deprovisioning) as equally important bookends.
- **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)** is typically implemented during on-boarding by mapping job titles to predefined permission groups rather than granting ad hoc access.
- On-boarding workflows should require signing an **Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)** and completing security awareness training *before* credentials are activated — not after.
