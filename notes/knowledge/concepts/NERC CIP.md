# NERC CIP

## What it is
Think of it as the electrical grid's version of a building code — but instead of ensuring walls won't collapse, it ensures substations and control systems won't be hijacked. NERC CIP (North American Electric Reliability Corporation Critical Infrastructure Protection) is a mandatory set of cybersecurity standards enforced by FERC that governs how utilities must protect Bulk Electric System (BES) assets. It applies to owners and operators of high-voltage transmission infrastructure across North America.

## Why it matters
In 2015, the Ukraine power grid attack (BlackEnergy malware) knocked out power for 225,000 customers by compromising SCADA systems at three distribution companies — exactly the threat NERC CIP is designed to prevent in North America. A utility compliant with NERC CIP CIP-007 (Systems Security Management) would have had patch management controls and port/service restrictions that could have reduced the attack surface exploited in that incident.

## Key facts
- **14 active CIP standards** cover areas from sabotage reporting (CIP-008) to supply chain risk management (CIP-013, added 2020)
- **BES Cyber System classification** (High/Medium/Low impact) determines the rigor of required controls — not all assets are treated equally
- **CIP-005** requires Electronic Security Perimeters (ESPs) with defined entry/exit points, similar in concept to network segmentation
- **CIP-010** mandates configuration change management and vulnerability assessments, including active scanning at least every 35 days for High impact systems
- **Non-compliance penalties** can reach **$1 million per violation per day**, making it one of the most financially enforced cybersecurity frameworks in any sector
