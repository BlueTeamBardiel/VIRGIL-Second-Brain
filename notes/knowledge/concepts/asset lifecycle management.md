# asset lifecycle management

## What it is
Like a hospital tracking a scalpel from sterilization to surgery to disposal — never losing sight of it, never letting it fall into the wrong hands — asset lifecycle management tracks every IT asset from procurement through retirement. It is the formal process of cataloging, monitoring, maintaining, and securely decommissioning hardware, software, and data assets throughout their entire operational existence.

## Why it matters
In 2020, a major healthcare provider was breached through a forgotten server that had been "decommissioned" but never wiped or removed from the network — still running an unpatched OS, still listening on open ports. Proper lifecycle management would have ensured that server was sanitized and physically removed, eliminating the attack surface entirely. This is why end-of-life and disposal phases are just as critical as procurement.

## Key facts
- **Five phases**: Procurement → Deployment → Operations/Maintenance → Decommission → Disposal — all must be documented
- **Sanitization standards**: NIST SP 800-88 defines media sanitization methods (Clear, Purge, Destroy) required before asset disposal
- **End-of-life (EOL) software** represents one of the highest-risk states — vendor patches stop, but vulnerabilities keep arriving
- **Hardware asset tracking** typically uses asset tags, RFID, or CMDB (Configuration Management Database) entries tied to each device
- **Licensing compliance** falls under lifecycle management — running unlicensed or expired software creates both legal and security exposure
