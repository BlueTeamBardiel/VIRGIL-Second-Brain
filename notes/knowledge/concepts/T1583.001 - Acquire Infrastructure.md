# T1583.001 - Acquire Infrastructure: Domains

## What it is
Like a con artist renting a storefront that *looks* like a legitimate bank branch before running a scam, adversaries register or purchase domain names to establish convincing operational infrastructure before launching attacks. This sub-technique specifically covers acquiring domains — either freshly registered, typosquatted, or expired — that will serve as command-and-control servers, phishing pages, or malware delivery points.

## Why it matters
In the 2020 SolarWinds supply chain attack, threat actors registered domains mimicking legitimate Solarwinds infrastructure months before the attack went live, using them to blend C2 traffic with normal network communication. Defenders who monitor newly registered domains resembling their organization's name can catch this staging activity during the reconnaissance window — *before* the first payload ever fires.

## Key facts
- Adversaries frequently use **typosquatting** (e.g., `micros0ft-update[.]com`) or **combo-squatting** (appending words like "-login" or "-secure") to make domains appear legitimate
- Domains registered within the **last 30 days** are statistically higher risk; many threat intel feeds flag "newly registered domains" (NRDs) as an IOC category
- Attackers may purchase **expired domains** with existing reputation scores to bypass domain-age-based security controls
- Domain acquisition happens in the **Resource Development** (TA0042) phase of the MITRE ATT&CK framework — *before* Initial Access
- WHOIS privacy services and bulletproof hosting registrars are commonly used to obscure domain ownership and complicate attribution
