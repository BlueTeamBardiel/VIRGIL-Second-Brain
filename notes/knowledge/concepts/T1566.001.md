# T1566.001

## What it is
Like a wolf wearing a sheep costume to sneak into the flock, spear phishing with attachments disguises malware inside familiar-looking files sent directly to targeted individuals. Precisely: it is a targeted phishing technique where adversaries craft emails with malicious file attachments — PDFs, Office documents, executables — tailored to a specific victim to gain initial access.

## Why it matters
In the 2020 SolarWinds supply chain attack, early-stage intrusions leveraged spear phishing attachments to compromise specific contractor accounts before pivoting deeper into networks. Defenders who monitor email gateway alerts for macro-enabled documents (`.xlsm`, `.docm`) and enforce attachment sandboxing can break the kill chain at the very first step.

## Key facts
- Attachments commonly exploit Office macros (VBA), PDF readers, or vulnerabilities in document parsers (e.g., CVE-based exploits in Word)
- Unlike broad phishing (T1566.003), spear phishing is **targeted** — attackers research the victim using OSINT to increase credibility
- MITRE classifies this under **Initial Access** (Tactic: TA0001), making it one of the most common entry vectors in real-world breaches
- Indicators include unexpected attachments, mismatched sender domains, and prompts to "Enable Macros"
- Defenses include: email filtering with sandboxing, disabling macros by policy (Group Policy/Intune), user awareness training, and DMARC/DKIM/SPF enforcement
