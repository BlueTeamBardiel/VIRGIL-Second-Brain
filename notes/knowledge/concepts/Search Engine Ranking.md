# Search Engine Ranking

## What it is
Like a town's most-trusted bulletin board where notices posted by respected community members get placed at eye level while unknown flyers get buried at the bottom — search engine ranking is the algorithmic process by which search engines like Google order results based on relevance, authority, and trustworthiness signals. Precisely, it refers to a page's position in search engine results pages (SERPs), determined by factors including backlinks, content quality, keywords, and domain reputation.

## Why it matters
Attackers exploit search engine ranking through **SEO poisoning** (also called search poisoning), where malicious actors optimize fraudulent websites to appear at the top of results for high-traffic queries like "free antivirus download" or "IRS tax form." A user searching for legitimate software clicks the top-ranked malicious result and downloads malware — the attacker weaponized trust in the ranking system itself. Defenders monitor brand-adjacent search terms and use threat intelligence feeds that flag newly registered look-alike domains climbing the rankings.

## Key facts
- **SEO poisoning** is explicitly tested on Security+ (SY0-701) as a social engineering/malware delivery vector
- Attackers use **typosquatting** combined with SEO manipulation to outrank legitimate sites for misspelled brand queries (e.g., "goggle.com")
- **Black hat SEO** techniques include keyword stuffing, link farms, and cloaking — showing search engines different content than human visitors see
- **Watering hole attacks** often begin with ranking a malicious site highly for terms the target audience (e.g., industrial engineers) regularly searches
- Google's **Safe Browsing API** is used by security tools to flag URLs identified as phishing or malware distribution points regardless of their ranking
