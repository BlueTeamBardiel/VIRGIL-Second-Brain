# Data Owner

## What it is
Like a landlord who decides who gets a key to each apartment — not the one fixing the pipes, but the one who sets the rules — a data owner is the senior individual or executive accountable for a specific dataset within an organization. They determine classification levels, access policies, and acceptable use, but typically delegate day-to-day handling to data custodians.

## Why it matters
During a ransomware investigation, incident responders need to know *whose* data was encrypted and who has authority to approve a ransom payment or restoration plan. Without a clearly designated data owner, critical decisions stall — no one has the legal accountability to authorize recovery actions or notify affected parties under breach disclosure laws like GDPR. Defined ownership means faster, cleaner response.

## Key facts
- The data owner is typically a **business executive or department manager** (e.g., CFO owns financial records), not an IT staff member
- Data owners are responsible for **assigning data classification** (e.g., confidential, public, restricted) under a formal classification policy
- They approve **access control decisions** — who gets permissions — while data custodians technically *implement* those permissions
- Under regulations like **HIPAA**, the data owner bears ultimate accountability for protecting covered information, including selecting appropriate safeguards
- Data owners are distinct from **data processors** (GDPR terminology) who handle data on behalf of the controller/owner
