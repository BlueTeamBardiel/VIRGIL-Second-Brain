# Evil Corp

## What it is
Think of a Fortune 500 company, but instead of selling software, they sell ransomware-as-a-service and steal hundreds of millions of dollars in bank transfers — with a CEO who drives Lamborghinis in Moscow. Evil Corp is a Russian cybercriminal organization responsible for developing and deploying the Dridex malware and BitPaymer/WastedLocker ransomware families, operating since approximately 2007 under the leadership of Maksim Yakubets.

## Why it matters
In 2020, Evil Corp deployed WastedLocker ransomware against Garmin, encrypting critical systems and allegedly demanding a $10 million ransom — disrupting GPS services for millions of users worldwide. This attack illustrates why OFAC sanctions against Evil Corp created a legal dilemma: paying the ransom potentially violated U.S. Treasury sanctions, forcing victims to choose between operational recovery and legal liability.

## Key facts
- Evil Corp is sanctioned by the U.S. Treasury Department's OFAC, making ransom payments to them a potential federal violation for U.S. entities
- Their flagship malware, **Dridex**, is a banking trojan spread via phishing emails with malicious Office document attachments using macros
- They pioneered the shift from banking trojan operations to high-value enterprise ransomware targeting (big game hunting)
- Maksim Yakubets was indicted in 2019 with a $5 million bounty — one of the largest ever offered for a cybercriminal
- Evil Corp continuously rebrands ransomware strains (BitPaymer → WastedLocker → Hades → Phoenix) specifically to evade OFAC sanction identification
