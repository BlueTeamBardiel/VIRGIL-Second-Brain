# Entitlement Management

## What it is
Think of it like a hotel key card system: each guest gets a card that only opens their room and the gym, not the penthouse or the kitchen. Entitlement Management is the discipline of defining, assigning, reviewing, and revoking the specific permissions and access rights that users hold across systems and applications. It ensures identities carry exactly the privileges they need — no more, no less.

## Why it matters
In the 2020 SolarWinds attack, compromised service accounts held excessive entitlements that allowed lateral movement across networks and cloud environments without triggering immediate alerts. Proper entitlement management — enforced least privilege and regular access reviews — would have contained the blast radius by restricting what those credentials could reach, even after compromise.

## Key facts
- **Least privilege principle** is the core doctrine: users receive only the minimum access required to perform their job function.
- **Access reviews (recertification campaigns)** periodically verify that entitlements are still appropriate — critical after role changes or terminations.
- **Privilege creep** occurs when users accumulate permissions over time through role changes without old rights being revoked; entitlement management directly combats this.
- **Identity Governance and Administration (IGA)** platforms (e.g., SailPoint, Saviynt) automate entitlement discovery, provisioning, and certification workflows at scale.
- Entitlement management is distinct from **authentication** — it governs what an already-verified identity is *allowed to do*, not *who they are*.
