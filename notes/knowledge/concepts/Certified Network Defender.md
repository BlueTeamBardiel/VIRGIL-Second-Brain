# Certified Network Defender

## What it is
Like a trained garrison soldier who doesn't just watch the walls but actively patrols, repairs breaches, and coordinates evacuations, a Certified Network Defender (CND) is a professional credentialed by EC-Council to protect, detect, respond to, and predict threats against organizational networks. The CND certification validates hands-on skills in network defense technologies, security controls, and incident response—going beyond passive monitoring into active defense operations.

## Why it matters
During the 2021 Colonial Pipeline ransomware attack, attackers moved laterally through the network for days before detection. A CND-trained defender, applying continuous traffic analysis and network segmentation principles, would have established east-west monitoring controls and isolation procedures to contain lateral movement—potentially cutting off attacker access before operational systems were impacted.

## Key facts
- CND is an EC-Council certification covering five defense pillars: **Protect, Detect, Respond, Predict, and Adapt**
- The exam (312-38) consists of 100 multiple-choice questions with a 4-hour time limit, requiring 70% to pass
- CND covers **19 network security domains** including firewall configuration, IDS/IPS deployment, VPN management, and cloud network defense
- Aligns with the **NICE Cybersecurity Workforce Framework** (NIST SP 800-181), making it relevant for U.S. government and DoD roles
- Unlike Security+ which is vendor-neutral and broad, CND is **network-defense specific**, emphasizing operational controls like packet analysis, log monitoring, and vulnerability scanning workflows
