# Contract Law

## What it is
Like a mutual handshake frozen in writing — binding two parties to specific promises with legal consequences for breaking them — contract law governs enforceable agreements between entities. In cybersecurity, it defines the legal backbone of every vendor relationship, outsourcing deal, and service agreement that touches your data or infrastructure.

## Why it matters
When a cloud provider suffers a breach and leaks customer PII, the first question investigators ask is: "What did the contract say?" If the SLA (Service Level Agreement) specified 99.9% uptime and encryption at rest but the vendor skipped the encryption, the victim organization can pursue breach-of-contract remedies — and regulators may look at contractual negligence when assessing liability. Contracts also define incident notification timelines, which directly affect breach disclosure compliance under laws like GDPR.

## Key facts
- **Four elements** make a contract enforceable: Offer, Acceptance, Consideration (exchange of value), and Legality — missing any one voids it
- **SLAs** are contracts defining measurable service expectations (uptime, RTO, RPO) — a Security+ exam favorite
- **NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)** is a contract that legally prohibits sharing sensitive information; violating it triggers civil liability
- **MOU (Memorandum of Understanding)** is a *non-binding* agreement — often confused with contracts; important distinction for CySA+
- **Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)** are mandatory contracts under GDPR when a controller shares personal data with a processor
