# AWS Trust Center

## What it is
Think of it as a restaurant's open kitchen — instead of hiding food prep behind closed doors, AWS puts its entire compliance and security posture on display for customers to inspect. The AWS Trust Center is a public-facing portal where Amazon documents its security controls, compliance certifications, privacy practices, and shared responsibility model. It serves as the authoritative source for AWS's security posture, giving organizations evidence to support their own compliance programs.

## Why it matters
During a vendor security assessment, a bank's compliance team needs to verify that AWS meets PCI-DSS requirements before migrating card processing workloads to EC2. Rather than waiting weeks for AWS to respond to a custom questionnaire, the compliance officer pulls AWS's PCI-DSS Attestation of Compliance directly from the Trust Center, cutting audit preparation time from months to days. This is third-party assurance made scalable.

## Key facts
- The Trust Center hosts AWS's **ISO 27001, SOC 1/2/3, PCI-DSS, FedRAMP, and HIPAA** compliance documentation — critical artifacts for regulated industries
- AWS operates under a **Shared Responsibility Model**: AWS secures the infrastructure *of* the cloud; customers secure what they put *in* the cloud — the Trust Center clarifies this boundary explicitly
- **AWS Artifact** (accessible via Trust Center) is the self-service portal for downloading compliance reports and signing NDAs to access restricted audit documents
- Trust Center content supports **third-party risk management (TPRM)** programs by providing independently audited evidence rather than self-attestations
- Documented security controls in the Trust Center align with **NIST SP 800-53** and **ISO 27002** control frameworks, making cross-framework mapping straightforward
