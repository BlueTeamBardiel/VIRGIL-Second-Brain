# Certified SOC Analyst

## What it is
Like an air traffic controller who monitors dozens of radar screens to spot dangerous flight paths before collisions happen, a Certified SOC Analyst (CSA) is a trained professional who monitors security dashboards, triages alerts, and responds to threats inside a Security Operations Center. The CSA certification, offered by EC-Council, validates Tier 1–2 SOC competencies including SIEM management, log analysis, and incident escalation procedures.

## Why it matters
During the 2020 SolarWinds supply chain attack, SOC analysts at affected organizations had to sift through massive volumes of SIEM alerts to identify anomalous outbound connections to attacker-controlled domains — a task requiring exactly the triage and correlation skills the CSA validates. Analysts who understood how to correlate Windows Event Logs with network flow data could isolate compromised hosts faster, containing lateral movement before further damage occurred.

## Key facts
- The CSA certification covers three core domains: **SOC processes**, **SIEM deployment/use**, and **incident detection and response**
- Focuses heavily on **log management and correlation** — analysts must understand Windows Event IDs (e.g., 4624 for logon, 4688 for process creation) as detection signals
- Emphasizes **threat intelligence integration** into SOC workflows, mapping alerts to the MITRE ATT&CK framework
- SOC Tier 1 analysts focus on **alert triage and monitoring**; Tier 2 handles **incident investigation**; Tier 3 performs **threat hunting** — CSA primarily targets Tiers 1–2
- Uses **runbooks and playbooks** to standardize response, reducing mean time to detect (MTTD) and mean time to respond (MTTR)
