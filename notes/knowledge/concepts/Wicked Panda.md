# Wicked Panda

## What it is
Think of a skilled locksmith who works for the government by day and robs banks by night using the same tools — Wicked Panda is a Chinese state-sponsored Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) group (also tracked as APT41) that uniquely blurs the line between nation-state espionage and financially motivated cybercrime. It is one of the rare threat actors conducting both government-directed intelligence gathering and independent criminal operations simultaneously.

## Why it matters
In 2020, the U.S. DOJ indicted five members of Wicked Panda for compromising over 100 organizations across 12 countries, including healthcare, telecommunications, and video game companies — stealing source code, customer data, and COVID-19 research. This dual-mission model means defenders must treat them as both an espionage threat (targeting IP and government secrets) and a financially motivated attacker (deploying ransomware and stealing in-game currency), requiring layered detection strategies beyond standard nation-state playbooks.

## Key facts
- **Attribution**: Tracked as APT41, Double Dragon, BARIUM, and Winnti by various vendors; linked to Chinese MSS (Ministry of State Security)
- **TTPs**: Known for supply chain attacks, exploiting public-facing applications (MITRE T1190), and using custom malware like DUSTPAN and HIGHNOON
- **Dual motivation**: Unique among APTs for combining state-sponsored espionage with personal financial gain (video game fraud, ransomware)
- **Speed**: Demonstrated capability to exploit vulnerabilities within hours of public disclosure (e.g., Citrix CVE-2019-19781)
- **Targets**: Healthcare, defense, telecoms, fintech, and gaming sectors across the US, Europe, and Asia
