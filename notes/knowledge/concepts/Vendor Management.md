# vendor management

## What it is
Like hiring a contractor to renovate your kitchen — you're responsible for what they do inside your house, even if they bring the tools. Vendor management is the formal process of assessing, contracting, monitoring, and offboarding third-party suppliers to ensure they meet your organization's security and compliance requirements throughout the relationship lifecycle.

## Why it matters
The 2013 Target breach originated through an HVAC vendor with network access credentials — attackers pivoted from the vendor's foothold to steal 40 million credit card numbers. This is the canonical example of why third-party risk isn't just a procurement checkbox: a vendor's weak security posture becomes your attack surface.

## Key facts
- **Right-to-audit clauses** in contracts give organizations the legal ability to inspect vendor security controls; without them, you're trusting promises on paper
- **Vendor Risk Assessments (VRA)** typically include questionnaires, SOC 2 Type II reports, and penetration test results to evaluate security posture before onboarding
- **Minimum security baselines** should be defined in contracts (e.g., MFA requirements, encryption standards, breach notification timelines — often 72 hours per GDPR)
- **End-of-relationship procedures** must include revoking all access, recovering data, and verifying destruction of sensitive information — offboarding is as critical as onboarding
- Security+ exam distinguishes between **vendor**, **supplier**, and **business partner** risk categories; each carries different trust levels and due diligence requirements under supply chain risk management (SCRM)
