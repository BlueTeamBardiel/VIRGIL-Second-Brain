# Cisco Catalyst Center

## What it is
Think of it as an air traffic control tower for your entire network — instead of routing planes, it routes policies, configurations, and telemetry across thousands of devices simultaneously. Cisco Catalyst Center (formerly DNA Center) is a centralized network management and intent-based networking platform that provides automation, assurance, and policy enforcement across Cisco campus and branch infrastructure from a single dashboard.

## Why it matters
During a ransomware outbreak, a SOC analyst can use Catalyst Center's device 360 and network assurance features to instantly visualize anomalous east-west traffic patterns and push segmentation policies via Software-Defined Access (SD-Access) to quarantine infected VLANs — without touching individual switches one by one. This dramatically compresses the containment window from hours to minutes.

## Key facts
- Catalyst Center uses **Intent-Based Networking (IBN)**: administrators define *what* behavior they want, and the platform translates it into device-level configurations automatically
- Integrates with **Cisco ISE** (Identity Services Engine) to enforce role-based access and micro-segmentation through Group-Based Policy (GBP/TrustSec)
- Provides **encrypted traffic analytics (ETA)** — it can detect malware within TLS-encrypted flows without decrypting them, using behavioral telemetry and machine learning
- Supports **REST APIs**, making it programmable via Python or tools like Postman — relevant for automated threat response playbooks
- Formerly called **Cisco DNA Center**; the rename to Catalyst Center occurred in 2023 — both names appear in exam and vendor documentation
