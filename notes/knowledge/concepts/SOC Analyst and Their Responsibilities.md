# SOC Analyst and Their Responsibilities

## What it is
Think of a SOC Analyst as an air traffic controller for a company's digital infrastructure — constantly watching radar screens for anomalies, coordinating responses, and preventing collisions before they become disasters. A SOC (Security Operations Center) Analyst is a cybersecurity professional who monitors, detects, investigates, and responds to security events and incidents using tools like SIEM platforms, EDR solutions, and threat intelligence feeds.

## Why it matters
During the 2020 SolarWinds supply chain attack, SOC analysts at affected organizations were responsible for correlating unusual outbound traffic patterns and lateral movement alerts that — when caught early — limited attacker dwell time. Analysts who understood behavioral baselines and escalation procedures were the difference between a contained incident and a full enterprise compromise.

## Key facts
- SOC analysts are organized in **tiers**: Tier 1 triages alerts, Tier 2 performs deeper investigation and correlation, Tier 3 handles advanced threat hunting and incident response.
- A core responsibility is **alert triage** — distinguishing true positives from false positives using SIEM tools like Splunk or Microsoft Sentinel.
- SOC analysts use the **Cyber Kill Chain** and **MITRE ATT&CK framework** to map attacker behavior and identify what stage of an intrusion is occurring.
- **Mean Time to Detect (MTTD)** and **Mean Time to Respond (MTTR)** are key KPIs used to measure SOC effectiveness.
- SOC analysts document findings in **ticketing systems** (e.g., ServiceNow, TheHive) and escalate incidents following defined **runbooks and playbooks**.
