# collaborator

## What it is
Like a locksmith hired to open a safe who secretly photographs its contents for a third party, a collaborator is an internal actor who knowingly assists an external threat — providing access, intelligence, or capabilities they couldn't otherwise obtain. Precisely: a collaborator (or insider threat accomplice) is a trusted insider who deliberately aids an outside attacker, typically motivated by financial gain, coercion, or ideology.

## Why it matters
In the 2016 Bangladesh Bank heist, insiders are believed to have provided attackers with network credentials and operational timing, enabling the theft of $81 million via fraudulent SWIFT transfers. Without that internal collaboration, the external threat actors lacked the access needed to craft believable transfer requests — making the collaborator the critical attack enabler.

## Key facts
- Collaborators are classified as **malicious insiders** under threat actor taxonomy and are distinct from negligent insiders who cause harm accidentally
- Common motivators follow the **MICE model**: Money, Ideology, Coercion, Ego
- Collaborators are especially dangerous because they bypass perimeter defenses — they already have legitimate credentials and context
- Detection relies heavily on **User and Entity Behavior Analytics (UEBA)** — spotting anomalous access patterns, unusual data transfers, or off-hours activity
- The **kill chain** implication: a collaborator can short-circuit early phases (reconnaissance, weaponization) by handing over direct access, dramatically accelerating an attack timeline
- Security clearance programs use **continuous monitoring** and **periodic reinvestigation** specifically to detect insider collaboration before damage occurs
