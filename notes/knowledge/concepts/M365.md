# M365

## What it is
Think of M365 (Microsoft 365) like a Swiss Army knife that every office worker carries — email, files, chat, video calls, and identity all folded into one subscription. Precisely, it is Microsoft's cloud-based productivity suite combining Exchange Online, SharePoint, Teams, OneDrive, and Azure Active Directory under a unified licensing and management platform.

## Why it matters
In the 2023 Storm-0558 breach, Chinese threat actors forged authentication tokens to access M365 email accounts of U.S. government officials — no password needed, just a stolen signing key. This attack demonstrated that in M365 environments, identity and token infrastructure *is* the perimeter; compromise the auth layer and you own the inbox silently.

## Key facts
- **Microsoft Secure Score** is M365's built-in security posture metric — CySA+ expects you to know it as a measurable baseline for hardening.
- **Unified Audit Log (UAL)** records sign-ins, email access, file sharing, and admin actions; requires E3/E5 licensing to retain logs beyond 90 days — a critical gap attackers exploit.
- **Conditional Access Policies** (via Azure AD/Entra ID) enforce MFA, device compliance, and location-based controls — primary defense against credential-stuffing attacks.
- **Defender for Office 365** (Plan 1/2) adds anti-phishing, Safe Links, and Safe Attachments on top of base Exchange Online Protection (EOP).
- M365 E5 includes **Microsoft Sentinel integration**, SIEM-ready log forwarding, and advanced threat analytics — E3 does not.
