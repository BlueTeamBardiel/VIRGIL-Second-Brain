# User Awareness Training

## What it is
Like teaching factory workers to recognize the smell of gas before an explosion — not to fix the pipe, but to not strike a match — user awareness training conditions non-technical employees to recognize and avoid security threats before they become incidents. It is a formal program that educates organizational users about cybersecurity risks, policies, acceptable use, and social engineering tactics through recurring instruction and simulated attacks.

## Why it matters
In the 2016 Democratic National Committee breach, spear-phishing emails tricked staffers into surrendering credentials — not because firewalls failed, but because humans clicked. A robust awareness training program that included simulated phishing campaigns and credential hygiene modules could have broken the attack chain at its weakest link: the human.

## Key facts
- **Phishing simulations** are a core component; organizations track click rates over time to measure training effectiveness and identify high-risk users
- Security+ expects you to know that awareness training addresses the **non-technical attack surface**, making it a compensating control for threats that bypass technical defenses
- **Role-based training** tailors content to job function — executives receive business email compromise (BEC) scenarios; IT staff get privilege escalation awareness
- Frequency matters: training should be **continuous or at minimum annual**, with reinforcement after real incidents or failed simulations
- Metrics like **phishing click-through rate reduction** and **incident reporting rate increase** are the measurable KPIs for program success on CySA+ exams
