# BARIUM

## What it is
Like a master locksmith who works exclusively for one landlord, BARIUM is a threat actor that operates as a contractor serving state-level interests — specifically, it's the Microsoft MSTIC designation for the Chinese state-sponsored APT group also tracked as APT41, Winnti, and Double Dragon. BARIUM blends financially motivated cybercrime with traditional espionage, making it uniquely dangerous among nation-state actors.

## Why it matters
In 2020, the U.S. Department of Justice indicted five BARIUM-affiliated members for compromising over 100 organizations across 12 countries, including software supply chains, gaming companies, and telecommunications firms. Their most notorious technique was the compromise of legitimate software update mechanisms — poisoning trusted channels like CCleaner and ASUS Live Update to deliver malware to millions of downstream users. This supply chain attack vector is precisely why software integrity verification matters.

## Key facts
- **Dual mission**: BARIUM simultaneously conducts IP theft for Chinese government objectives AND financially motivated attacks (ransomware, virtual currency theft) for personal profit — rare among state actors
- **Supply chain specialization**: Known for injecting malware into legitimate software update pipelines (CCleaner 2017 breach affected ~2.27 million users)
- **TTPs include**: Spearphishing, SQL injection, rootkits, and living-off-the-land binaries (LOLBins) to evade detection
- **Also tracked as**: APT41, Winnti Group, Barium, Double Dragon, Wicked Panda — understanding alias convergence matters for threat intelligence correlation
- **Targeting profile**: Healthcare, telecoms, technology, gaming, and managed service providers (MSPs) — any sector with valuable IP or financial systems
