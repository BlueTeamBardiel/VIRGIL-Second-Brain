# spear phishing

## What it is
If regular phishing is casting a wide net into the ocean hoping to catch anything, spear phishing is a sniper with a dossier — they've studied your LinkedIn, know your boss's name, and craft a single email designed specifically for you. It is a targeted social engineering attack where the attacker personalizes deceptive communications using reconnaissance data about a specific individual or organization to dramatically increase the likelihood of deception.

## Why it matters
In the 2016 Democratic National Committee breach, attackers sent John Podesta a spoofed Google security alert that was convincingly personalized — his aide even called it "legitimate." Podesta entered his credentials, handing over access to 50,000 emails. The lesson: even security-aware users fall when the lure matches their exact context.

## Key facts
- Spear phishing is the most common initial access vector in targeted attacks, appearing in over 90% of APT campaigns according to Symantec research
- Attackers conduct OSINT (Open Source Intelligence) using LinkedIn, corporate websites, and social media to build a target profile before crafting the message
- A **whaling** attack is spear phishing specifically targeting C-suite executives (CEOs, CFOs), often for wire fraud or credential theft
- **BEC (Business Email Compromise)** frequently begins with spear phishing, costing businesses over $50 billion globally per the FBI IC3
- Defenses include DMARC/DKIM/SPF email authentication, security awareness training with simulated phishing campaigns, and anti-phishing MFA enforcement
