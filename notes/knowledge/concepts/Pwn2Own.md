# Pwn2Own

## What it is
Think of it as the World Series of hacking — top researchers compete publicly to crack fully-patched, hardened systems for cash prizes and bragging rights. Pwn2Own is an annual competitive hacking contest organized by Zero Day Initiative (ZDI) where researchers demonstrate previously unknown zero-day exploits against popular software and hardware targets in a live, controlled environment.

## Why it matters
At Pwn2Own 2021, researchers from Devcore chained together two zero-days to fully compromise a Microsoft Exchange Server in under five minutes — a demonstration that forced Microsoft to issue emergency patches protecting millions of enterprise mail servers worldwide. This illustrates how structured adversarial research translates directly into defensive action: vendors receive technical details, issue patches, and the vulnerability is retired from active weaponization before criminal actors can weaponize it at scale.

## Key facts
- **Responsible disclosure built-in**: ZDI pays researchers, acquires the exploit details, notifies the affected vendor, and enforces a 120-day patch deadline before public disclosure
- **"Pwned" = owned**: Successfully exploiting a target earns the researcher full ownership of the device plus cash — prizes have exceeded $1 million in a single contest
- **Targets are fully patched**: All competition systems run the latest updates, meaning only true zero-days succeed — no known-CVE shortcuts allowed
- **Categories include browsers, VMs, enterprise software, cars, and ICS/SCADA systems** — reflecting real-world high-value attack surfaces
- **Master of Pwn**: The overall top scorer earns this title, creating a competitive incentive structure that attracts elite talent toward constructive research
