# NIST SP 800-171

## What it is
Think of it as the building inspection code for contractors who store government blueprints in their private warehouses — if you handle federal data, your house must meet these standards. NIST SP 800-171 is a set of 110 security requirements organized into 14 families that non-federal organizations must implement to protect Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) stored or processed on their systems. It was derived from NIST SP 800-53 and is mandatory for DoD contractors under DFARS clause 252.204-7012.

## Why it matters
In 2020, a defense contractor handling technical drawings for military equipment was breached after failing to implement multi-factor authentication — a direct SP 800-171 requirement. The attacker exfiltrated CUI related to weapons systems, triggering a DFARS violation, contract termination, and a False Claims Act lawsuit worth millions. Compliance with 800-171 isn't just good hygiene; it's legally enforceable for anyone touching DoD data.

## Key facts
- Contains **110 security requirements** across **14 families** including Access Control, Incident Response, and System & Communications Protection
- Protects **CUI** (Controlled Unclassified Information) — not classified data, but sensitive government information like export-controlled technical specs
- Compliance is enforced via **DFARS 252.204-7012**; contractors self-attest using a **System Security Plan (SSP)** and Plan of Action & Milestones (POA&M)
- The **CMMC (Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification)** framework builds directly on 800-171, adding third-party verification requirements
- Organizations score themselves using the **SPRS (Supplier Performance Risk System)**, with a maximum score of **110 points**
