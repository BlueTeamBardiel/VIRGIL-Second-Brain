# FortiSOAR

## What it is
Think of FortiSOAR as an air traffic control tower for your SOC — instead of planes, it coordinates alerts, analysts, and automated responses so nothing collides or gets lost. Precisely, FortiSOAR (Security Orchestration, Automation, and Response) is Fortinet's enterprise SOAR platform that integrates with hundreds of security tools to automate incident workflows, reduce manual triage, and enforce consistent response playbooks.

## Why it matters
During a ransomware campaign, a SOC team might receive thousands of alerts simultaneously from firewalls, EDR tools, and email gateways — far too many for humans to triage manually in time. FortiSOAR can automatically correlate those alerts into a single incident, enrich indicators with threat intelligence, isolate affected endpoints via API calls to CrowdStrike or Carbon Black, and notify analysts — all within minutes of initial detection, dramatically shrinking dwell time.

## Key facts
- FortiSOAR uses **playbooks** (visual, drag-and-drop workflows) to automate response actions like IP blocking, ticket creation, and user notifications without manual intervention
- It provides **role-based case management**, allowing analysts at different tiers (L1/L2/L3) to collaborate on incidents with full audit trails — relevant for compliance requirements like SOC 2 and HIPAA
- FortiSOAR integrates with 350+ connectors (Splunk, VirusTotal, ServiceNow, Palo Alto, etc.) enabling true end-to-end orchestration across heterogeneous environments
- **Mean Time to Respond (MTTR)** reduction is the primary KPI FortiSOAR targets — documented cases show 80%+ reduction in manual analyst effort on repetitive alert handling
- Supports **multi-tenant deployments**, making it common in MSSP (Managed Security Service Provider) environments managing multiple client environments from one console
