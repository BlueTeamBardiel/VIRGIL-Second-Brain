# APT39

## What it is
Think of APT39 as a corporate intelligence firm that moonlights as a spy ring — outwardly focused on business data, but ultimately serving a government agenda. APT39 is an Iranian state-sponsored threat group (linked to the IRGC via Rana Intelligence Computing Company) that specializes in large-scale surveillance and tracking of specific individuals rather than destructive attacks. Their primary mission is persistent monitoring of targets for intelligence collection, particularly focused on diaspora communities and perceived political dissidents.

## Why it matters
In 2019, the FBI and CISA attributed a multi-year campaign against telecommunications, travel, and hospitality companies to APT39 — sectors that are goldmines for tracking individual movement and communications. By compromising airline booking systems and telecom providers, they could reconstruct the travel history and contacts of Iranian dissidents abroad without ever touching the target's personal device directly.

## Key facts
- **Attribution**: Linked to Iran's Ministry of Intelligence and Security (MOIS); indictments unsealed in 2020 named specific Iranian nationals
- **Primary targets**: Telecommunications, travel/hospitality, and IT companies across the Middle East, Europe, and the US — chosen for their access to personal data at scale
- **TTPs**: Heavy use of spearphishing, custom malware families (SEAWEED, CACHEMONEY, POWBAT), and living-off-the-land techniques to blend into legitimate traffic
- **Goal**: PII harvesting and tracking individuals — notably dissidents, journalists, and dual nationals — distinguishing them from destructive Iranian groups like APT33
- **MITRE ATT&CK**: Appears in ATT&CK framework; notable for T1078 (Valid Accounts) and T1114 (Email Collection) as core persistence and collection techniques
