# T1110.002

## What it is
Like a locksmith who already knows your street address and just needs to try the three most common deadbolt combinations, password spraying attempts a small set of common passwords (like `Password1!`) against a *large* number of accounts. Unlike brute force, it deliberately avoids rapid repeated attempts against any single account, staying under lockout thresholds.

## Why it matters
In the 2021 SolarWinds-adjacent campaigns, NOBELIUM used password spraying against Microsoft 365 tenants to gain initial access without triggering account lockouts. Defenders who only watch for single-account lockout events will miss this attack entirely — detection requires correlating *many* accounts receiving *few* failed logins in the same time window.

## Key facts
- **Lockout evasion by design**: Spraying typically fires one attempt per account every 30–60 minutes, staying below the common "5 failures" lockout threshold
- **High-value targets**: Cloud identity providers (Azure AD, Okta, M365) are prime targets because successful authentication often directly yields email, files, and SSO access
- **Detection signature**: Look for a single source IP generating AuthN failures across dozens of different usernames within a short window — the inverse of traditional brute force patterns
- **Credential hygiene gap**: Effectiveness collapses when MFA is enforced; a correct password alone grants nothing if a second factor is required
- **Common spray list**: Passwords matching seasonal patterns (`Summer2024!`, `Company@1`) or the organization's own password policy minimum (8 chars, 1 special) are typical candidates
