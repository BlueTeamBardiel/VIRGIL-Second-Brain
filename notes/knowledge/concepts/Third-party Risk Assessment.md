# Third-party Risk Assessment

## What it is
Like inspecting the kitchen of a restaurant that caters your party — you're responsible for the food even though someone else cooked it. Third-party risk assessment is the structured process of evaluating the security posture of vendors, suppliers, and partners who have access to your systems, data, or infrastructure before and during the relationship.

## Why it matters
In 2020, SolarWinds' build pipeline was compromised, injecting malicious code into software updates delivered to ~18,000 organizations — including US federal agencies. Every affected organization had implicitly trusted SolarWinds as a vendor without adequately monitoring the integrity of what that vendor delivered into their environment.

## Key facts
- **Supply chain attacks** exploit trusted third-party relationships; organizations inherit their vendors' vulnerabilities even without direct compromise
- Key assessment methods include **questionnaires (SIG, CAIQ)**, on-site audits, penetration testing requirements, and reviewing **SOC 2 Type II reports**
- **Right-to-audit clauses** in vendor contracts grant the purchasing organization the ability to inspect security controls directly — critical for high-risk vendors
- Risk tiers matter: vendors with **privileged access or data handling responsibilities** require deeper due diligence than peripheral suppliers
- Third-party risk management aligns with frameworks like **NIST SP 800-161** (supply chain risk) and is testable under **Security+ domain: Governance, Risk, and Compliance**
