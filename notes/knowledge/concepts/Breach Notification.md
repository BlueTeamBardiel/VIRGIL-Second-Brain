# Breach Notification

## What it is
Like a hospital legally required to tell patients when their medical records were left in a taxi, breach notification is the mandatory obligation to inform affected individuals, regulators, and sometimes the public when their personal data has been compromised. It transforms a private security failure into a legally governed, time-bound disclosure process.

## Why it matters
In 2017, Equifax discovered a breach exposing 147 million people's SSNs, credit card numbers, and birthdates — but waited 41 days before public disclosure. This delay triggered FTC investigations, congressional hearings, and a $575 million settlement, demonstrating that *how* and *when* you notify is as legally consequential as the breach itself.

## Key facts
- **HIPAA Breach Notification Rule** requires covered entities to notify affected individuals within **60 days** of discovering a breach involving unsecured PHI; breaches affecting 500+ people in a state must also notify major media outlets
- **GDPR Article 33** mandates notification to the supervisory authority within **72 hours** of becoming aware of a breach — one of the tightest regulatory windows globally
- **All 50 U.S. states** have their own breach notification laws, meaning a single incident can trigger dozens of overlapping compliance obligations
- Notification must typically include: what data was exposed, how the breach occurred, what remediation is being taken, and contact information for follow-up
- **Safe harbor provisions** exist in many laws — properly encrypted data that is breached may not trigger notification requirements because the data is considered unreadable to attackers
