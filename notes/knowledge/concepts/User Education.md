# User Education

## What it is
Like teaching factory workers to recognize a gas leak by smell before the explosion happens, user education is the deliberate training of non-technical personnel to identify and respond correctly to security threats. It transforms the human layer — statistically the weakest link — into a conscious defensive control rather than a passive vulnerability.

## Why it matters
In the 2016 John Podesta email breach, a simple phishing email succeeded not because of technical failure but because a staff member lacked the judgment to verify a suspicious password-reset link. A single hour of phishing-awareness training teaching users to hover over links and verify sender domains before clicking could have prevented a breach that influenced a presidential election.

## Key facts
- **Security awareness training** is a required administrative control under frameworks like NIST SP 800-50 and is explicitly tested on Security+ as a compensating control against social engineering
- **Phishing simulations** (sending fake phishing emails to employees) are the most measurable form of user education — click rates before and after training provide quantifiable risk reduction metrics
- The **insider threat** risk is reduced through education on data handling policies, clean-desk rules, and recognizing pretexting attempts
- **Password hygiene training** — covering reuse, complexity, and MFA adoption — directly reduces credential-stuffing attack success rates
- Security+ categorizes user education under **administrative/managerial controls**, not technical controls — important for exam classification questions
