# NIST Special Publication 800-61

## What it is
Like a hospital's trauma protocol that turns chaotic emergencies into ordered, repeatable responses, NIST SP 800-61 is the federal playbook for computer security incident handling. It defines a structured lifecycle for responding to cybersecurity incidents, published by NIST as the *Computer Security Incident Handling Guide*.

## Why it matters
When the 2020 SolarWinds supply chain attack hit thousands of organizations simultaneously, responders with a defined IR process could methodically contain affected systems, preserve forensic evidence, and coordinate eradication — while unprepared organizations scrambled without clear roles or steps. SP 800-61 provides exactly that structure: who does what, in what order, and how to learn from it afterward.

## Key facts
- Defines **four phases** of the incident response lifecycle: **Preparation → Detection & Analysis → Containment, Eradication & Recovery → Post-Incident Activity**
- The **Post-Incident Activity** phase includes a *lessons-learned meeting* held within days of the incident — a commonly tested exam detail
- Recommends organizations establish a formal **CSIRT (Computer Security Incident Response Team)** with defined roles before an incident occurs
- Distinguishes between an **event** (any observable occurrence) and an **incident** (an event that violates security policy or causes harm)
- Emphasizes **evidence handling and chain of custody** during containment to preserve data for potential legal proceedings
