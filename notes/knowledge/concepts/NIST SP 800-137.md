# NIST SP 800-137

## What it is
Like a smoke detector system that doesn't just sound an alarm once but continuously monitors air quality and reports to a central fire station — NIST SP 800-137 defines the framework for **Information Security Continuous Monitoring (ISCM)**. It establishes how federal agencies should maintain ongoing awareness of their information security, vulnerabilities, and threats to support organizational risk management decisions.

## Why it matters
In 2020, the SolarWinds supply chain attack persisted undetected for nearly nine months partly because organizations lacked continuous visibility into anomalous network behavior and software integrity. A mature ISCM program per SP 800-137 — with automated monitoring of software baselines, network traffic, and configuration states — would have created earlier detection opportunities by flagging unexpected outbound communications from the Orion platform.

## Key facts
- ISCM operates across **three tiers**: Organization (Tier 1), Mission/Business Process (Tier 2), and Information System (Tier 3)
- Defines a **six-step ISCM process**: Define → Establish → Implement → Analyze/Report → Respond → Review
- Monitoring frequency is **risk-based** — higher-risk controls are assessed more frequently than lower-risk ones
- Works in tandem with **NIST SP 800-37 (RMF)** — ISCM is essentially the "Monitor" step of the Risk Management Framework made into a full discipline
- Distinguishes between **automated** (continuous, near-real-time) and **manual** monitoring, advocating for automation where feasible
- Security metrics and **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)** must be defined to measure ISCM program effectiveness
