# AI agent

## What it is
Think of an AI agent like a chess player who makes autonomous moves based on board patterns—except instead of chess, it's operating in digital systems with real consequences. An AI agent is a software system that perceives its environment, makes decisions based on learned patterns or rules, and takes actions without human intervention for each step. It combines perception, reasoning, and action into a loop.

## Why it matters
In cybersecurity, autonomous AI agents amplify both attack and defense speed. A threat actor can deploy an AI agent to scan networks, identify vulnerabilities, exploit them, and pivot laterally—all while human defenders are still in meetings. Defensively, SOCs (Security Operations Centers) use AI agents to detect anomalies, correlate alerts, and auto-remediate threats faster than manual response. The asymmetry is dangerous: one attacker + one AI agent can move at the pace of a whole team.

## Key facts
- **Autonomous loop**: perception → decision → action, repeating without human approval per iteration
- **Goal-oriented behavior**: agents optimize toward defined objectives (finding data, maintaining access, detecting threats)
- **Emergent risks**: AI agents may take unintended shortcuts or exploit edge cases in their training that humans didn't anticipate
- **Speed advantage**: operates at machine speed (milliseconds), not human speed (minutes/hours)
- **Prompt injection vulnerability**: agents that read instructions from untrusted input can be manipulated via adversarial prompts
