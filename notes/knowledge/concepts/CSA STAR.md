# CSA STAR

## What it is
Think of it as Yelp reviews for cloud providers — but instead of restaurant cleanliness, it's a publicly audited registry of how well a cloud vendor controls your data. CSA STAR (Security, Trust, Assurance, and Risk) is a Cloud Security Alliance program that allows cloud service providers to publicly document their security controls, assessed against the CSA Cloud Controls Matrix (CCM). It operates across three tiers of increasing rigor: self-assessment, third-party audit, and continuous monitoring.

## Why it matters
When a healthcare company is evaluating whether to migrate patient records to a cloud provider, they need assurance beyond a vendor's marketing brochure. A CSA STAR Level 2 certification means an independent auditor has verified controls against the CCM — giving the customer concrete evidence of compliance posture before a breach occurs, not after. This prevents scenarios where organizations unknowingly inherit a vendor's weak access controls or unencrypted storage configurations.

## Key facts
- **Three levels**: Level 1 = self-assessment (free, public); Level 2 = third-party audit (mapped to ISO 27001 or SOC 2); Level 3 = continuous monitoring (emerging standard)
- The **Cloud Controls Matrix (CCM)** is the underlying control framework — 197 controls across 17 domains — that STAR assessments map against
- STAR Level 2 assessments are published in the **STAR Registry**, a publicly searchable database of certified providers
- CSA STAR **is not a regulation** — it's a voluntary assurance program, but it satisfies due diligence requirements in many vendor risk assessments
- Level 1 self-assessments use the **Consensus Assessments Initiative Questionnaire (CAIQ)** as the documentation format
