# NIST SP 800-61

## What it is
Think of it as the emergency room triage manual for cybersecurity — it tells you exactly who does what, in what order, when the alarms go off. NIST SP 800-61 is the *Computer Security Incident Handling Guide*, a federal framework published by NIST that defines a structured, repeatable process for preparing for, detecting, containing, and recovering from security incidents.

## Why it matters
During the 2020 SolarWinds supply chain attack, organizations without a defined incident response lifecycle struggled to coordinate across IT, legal, and executive teams — wasting critical hours. SP 800-61 provides the exact playbook structure that allows organizations to move from chaotic reaction to disciplined response, including defined roles, escalation paths, and lessons-learned processes that improve future defenses.

## Key facts
- Defines **four phases** of the incident response lifecycle: **Preparation → Detection & Analysis → Containment, Eradication & Recovery → Post-Incident Activity**
- Emphasizes that **Preparation** is ongoing, not a one-time event — includes building incident response teams (CSIRTs) and establishing communication plans
- **Post-Incident Activity** (the "lessons learned" meeting) is explicitly required and should occur within **72 hours** of incident resolution
- Distinguishes between an **event** (any observable occurrence) and an **incident** (an event that violates security policy or causes harm)
- Recommends **containment strategies be chosen before an incident occurs** — not improvised in the moment — and documented in playbooks
- Widely referenced in **CySA+** and **Security+** exams as the gold-standard IR framework
