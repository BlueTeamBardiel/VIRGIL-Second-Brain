# T1566.003

## What it is
Like a con artist slipping a poisoned business card into a stack of legitimate ones at a networking event, spearphishing via service exploits trust in third-party platforms rather than email. Specifically, T1566.003 describes adversaries delivering malicious links or content through legitimate services — social media, messaging apps, collaboration tools, or job platforms — to lure targeted victims into executing malware or surrendering credentials.

## Why it matters
In Operation Dream Job, the Lazarus Group (North Korea) used LinkedIn to pose as recruiters, sending fake job offer documents to aerospace and defense engineers. The targeted employees, seeing messages arrive through a trusted professional platform rather than a suspicious email, were far more likely to open the malicious files — resulting in several confirmed intrusions at defense contractors.

## Key facts
- Platforms abused include LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Discord, Slack, WhatsApp, and GitHub — anywhere direct messaging or file sharing exists
- Bypasses email security controls (SPF, DKIM, DMARC, email gateway filters) entirely since no email is involved
- Often paired with **T1585** (Establish Accounts) — attackers build convincing fake personas before initiating contact
- The social engineering angle is enhanced because recipients view platform notifications as inherently more trustworthy than cold emails
- Detection relies on monitoring for abnormal outbound connections or file downloads triggered after social media/messaging activity, not traditional email telemetry
