# SecurityWeek

## What it is
Think of SecurityWeek as the Reuters newswire for the cybersecurity industry — a dedicated wire service where analysts, vendors, and practitioners get breaking threat intelligence before it hits mainstream media. It is a professional cybersecurity news and information portal that publishes original reporting on vulnerabilities, data breaches, threat actor campaigns, and industry trends. Founded in 2006, it operates as a primary open-source intelligence (OSINT) resource used by security teams worldwide.

## Why it matters
When the Log4Shell vulnerability (CVE-2021-44228) was disclosed in December 2021, SecurityWeek published technical breakdowns and exploitation timelines within hours, giving defenders a critical head start before mass exploitation peaked. Security Operations Center (SOC) analysts routinely monitor SecurityWeek as part of their threat intelligence feeds to identify newly published CVEs and active threat actor TTPs that may require immediate patch prioritization or detection rule updates.

## Key facts
- SecurityWeek hosts the **ICS Cyber Security Conference**, one of the premier events focused on industrial control system and OT/SCADA security threats
- It is considered a credible **OSINT source** for threat intelligence gathering — a skill tested in CySA+ objectives around threat data collection
- SecurityWeek publishes **vendor-agnostic** reporting, making it valuable for benchmarking security controls against real-world attack scenarios
- The site covers **adversary group profiles** (APT actors, ransomware gangs), relevant to the CySA+ domain of threat intelligence and the MITRE ATT&CK framework
- Security professionals use it alongside **CISA alerts**, **NVD**, and **Krebs on Security** to build a comprehensive threat awareness picture
