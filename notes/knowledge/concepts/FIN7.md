# FIN7

## What it is
Think of FIN7 like a criminal franchise that licenses its "attack-as-a-service" toolkit to operators — complete with HR departments, customer support roles, and specialized teams. FIN7 (also known as Carbanak Group or Navigator Group) is a sophisticated, financially motivated Russian-linked threat actor group that has systematically targeted the retail, hospitality, and restaurant industries since at least 2015 to steal payment card data at massive scale.

## Why it matters
FIN7 compromised over 100 U.S. companies including Chipotle, Arby's, and Red Robin by deploying custom malware through spear-phishing emails that delivered the CARBANAK and BOOSTWRITE malware families, ultimately stealing tens of millions of payment card records. Defenders studying this group learn to prioritize email gateway hardening, anomalous lateral movement detection, and point-of-sale system isolation as critical controls against financially motivated APTs.

## Key facts
- FIN7 operated a front company called "Combi Security" to recruit unwitting cybersecurity professionals as legitimate-seeming employees
- Their primary attack vector is highly targeted spear-phishing with malicious Word documents using macros or embedded scripts to drop CARBANAK malware
- CARBANAK gives attackers remote access and targets POS systems specifically to scrape payment card data from memory (RAM scraping)
- FIN7 demonstrated advanced operational security: compartmentalized teams handled intrusion, lateral movement, and monetization separately
- Three leaders were indicted by the U.S. DOJ in 2018, but the group continued operating — demonstrating the resilience of well-structured criminal organizations
