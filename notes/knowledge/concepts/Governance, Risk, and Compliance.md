# Governance, Risk, and Compliance

## What it is
Think of GRC like running a hospital: governance is the board setting policy ("surgeons must wash hands"), risk management is calculating the odds a patient gets infected if they don't, and compliance is the inspector verifying the soap dispensers are actually full. Together, GRC is the organizational framework that aligns security strategy with business objectives, quantifies threats, and enforces adherence to laws, regulations, and internal policies.

## Why it matters
In 2017, Equifax suffered a breach exposing 147 million records — not because they lacked security tools, but because a known Apache Struts vulnerability sat unpatched for 78 days due to failed internal governance processes. A functioning GRC program would have assigned ownership of that patch cycle, tracked it as an unacceptable risk, and enforced a compliance deadline before attackers exploited it.

## Key facts
- **Governance** defines *who* is accountable — roles like CISO, risk committees, and board-level oversight fall here
- **Risk management** follows the formula: Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Impact; key outputs are risk registers and residual risk acceptance
- **Compliance** maps to specific frameworks: PCI-DSS (payment card data), HIPAA (health data), SOX (financial controls), GDPR (EU privacy)
- **Risk treatment options** are four: Accept, Avoid, Transfer (e.g., cyber insurance), or Mitigate — all are testable on Security+
- A **risk register** is a living document tracking identified risks, their likelihood, impact rating, owner, and treatment status — central to both CySA+ and real-world audits
