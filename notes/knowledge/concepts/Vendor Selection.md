# Vendor Selection

## What it is
Like choosing a contractor to build your house — you wouldn't hand over the blueprints and a key to someone you haven't vetted. Vendor selection is the formal process of evaluating third-party suppliers, software providers, and service partners before granting them access to your systems, data, or infrastructure. It includes security assessments, contract reviews, and due diligence to ensure the vendor doesn't become your weakest link.

## Why it matters
The 2013 Target breach began when attackers compromised a third-party HVAC vendor, Fazio Mechanical, who had network access to Target's systems. Target's vendor selection and ongoing monitoring processes failed to enforce least-privilege access and adequate security controls on a supplier, ultimately exposing 40 million credit card records. Rigorous vendor selection would have identified and mitigated that access risk before attackers could exploit it.

## Key facts
- **Due diligence** includes reviewing SOC 2 reports, penetration test results, certifications (ISO 27001), and financial stability before signing
- **Rules of Engagement** and data handling requirements must be documented in the contract, including right-to-audit clauses
- **Supply chain risk management (SCRM)** is the broader framework; vendor selection is the entry gate — once a vendor is approved, ongoing monitoring continues through the vendor lifecycle
- **Questionnaires (VSQs)** — Vendor Security Questionnaires — are a standard tool used to self-assess a vendor's security posture before onboarding
- Security+ and CySA+ expect you to know that vendor risk is a **third-party risk**, distinct from internal risk, and requires its own policies separate from internal security controls
