# Regulatory Compliance

## What it is
Think of it like a building inspector who doesn't care if your house *looks* nice — they check whether it meets code: proper wiring, fire exits, load-bearing walls. Regulatory compliance is the formal process of aligning an organization's security controls, policies, and practices with legally mandated or industry-required standards. Failure isn't just a technical problem — it's a legal and financial liability.

## Why it matters
In 2017, Equifax suffered a breach exposing 147 million records. Beyond the technical failure, regulators found the company had violated FTC Act requirements and lacked adequate data protection practices — ultimately paying $575 million in settlements. Compliance frameworks like PCI DSS and HIPAA exist precisely to enforce minimum security floors that organizations might otherwise skip to cut costs.

## Key facts
- **HIPAA** governs healthcare data; violations can result in fines up to $1.9 million per violation category per year
- **PCI DSS** (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard) applies to any organization handling cardholder data; non-compliance can result in losing card processing rights entirely
- **GDPR** applies to any organization handling EU citizens' data regardless of where the company is located; fines can reach 4% of global annual revenue
- **SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley)** focuses on financial data integrity and requires IT controls supporting accurate financial reporting — relevant to auditors and CISOs alike
- Compliance ≠ Security: meeting a compliance checklist does not guarantee you're protected — it establishes a *minimum baseline*, not a ceiling
- **Right to audit clauses** in vendor contracts are a compliance mechanism that lets organizations verify third-party adherence to required standards
