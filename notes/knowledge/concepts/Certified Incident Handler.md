# Certified Incident Handler

## What it is
Like a trauma surgeon who follows a strict triage protocol rather than improvising under pressure, a Certified Incident Handler (ECIH) is a professional trained to execute a structured, repeatable methodology when an organization is actively under attack. Issued by EC-Council, the ECIH certification validates competency across the full incident response lifecycle — preparation, detection, containment, eradication, recovery, and post-incident review.

## Why it matters
During the 2021 Colonial Pipeline ransomware attack, the response team had to rapidly isolate infected OT/IT network segments, preserve forensic evidence, and coordinate with federal agencies — all simultaneously. A certified incident handler operates from a practiced playbook in exactly these high-pressure moments, preventing hasty decisions (like wiping drives) that destroy evidence needed for prosecution or root-cause analysis.

## Key facts
- Follows the **6-phase IR lifecycle**: Preparation → Identification → Containment → Eradication → Recovery → Lessons Learned (aligns with NIST SP 800-61)
- **Containment strategies** are categorized as short-term (isolate the system now) vs. long-term (patch and harden before reconnecting)
- Incident handlers must differentiate between an **event** (any observable occurrence) and an **incident** (an event that violates security policy)
- **Chain of custody** documentation is a core handler responsibility — evidence mishandled is evidence inadmissible
- ECIH maps closely to **NIST SP 800-61 Rev. 2** and **SANS PICERL** frameworks, making it directly relevant to CySA+ exam scenarios involving IR process questions
