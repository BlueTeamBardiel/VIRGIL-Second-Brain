# T1598.001

## What it is
Like a con artist calling a bank's IT help desk pretending to be a panicked new employee who "forgot" their credentials, spearphishing for information via a service means an attacker crafts targeted messages through third-party services — cloud storage, social media, or collaboration platforms — specifically to extract credentials or sensitive data through deception, without ever sending a traditional email.

## Why it matters
In the 2020 Twitter breach, attackers used phone-based social engineering against Twitter employees to harvest VPN credentials — a real-world example of how targeting service channels bypasses traditional email security controls entirely. Defenders who only monitor SMTP traffic miss these vectors completely, making detection dependent on employee training and behavioral analytics rather than perimeter tools.

## Key facts
- This is a **reconnaissance sub-technique** under T1598 (Phishing for Information), distinct from T1566 which focuses on gaining initial access rather than harvesting intel
- Attackers commonly abuse **LinkedIn messages, Slack DMs, and GitHub issues** to solicit credentials, API keys, or internal documentation
- The technique exploits **implicit trust** users place in familiar platforms — a LinkedIn message feels less suspicious than a cold email
- Defenders should implement **Data Loss Prevention (DLP)** policies and user awareness training specifically covering non-email channels
- Often precedes **credential-based initial access** (T1078) — it's the intelligence-gathering phase that enables the actual intrusion
