# CISA

## What it is
Think of CISA as the nation's immune system coordinator — it doesn't fight every infection itself, but it signals, organizes, and strengthens every other defender across the body. The Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency is a U.S. federal agency under DHS, established in 2018, responsible for protecting critical infrastructure and coordinating national cyber defense efforts between government and private sector entities.

## Why it matters
During the 2020 SolarWinds supply chain attack, CISA issued Emergency Directive 21-01 — ordering all federal civilian agencies to immediately disconnect affected SolarWinds Orion systems. This demonstrated CISA's authority to mandate defensive actions across the federal enterprise when a threat reaches systemic scale, something no private entity could compel on its own.

## Key facts
- CISA maintains the **Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog** — a living list of CVEs actively exploited in the wild; federal agencies are legally required to patch KEV entries within defined deadlines
- Issues **Alerts, Advisories, and Emergency Directives** — Emergency Directives apply specifically to Federal Civilian Executive Branch (FCEB) agencies
- Operates **#StopRansomware** campaign and publishes joint advisories co-authored with FBI and NSA
- Manages **shields up** guidance and runs **Continuous Diagnostics and Mitigation (CDM)** program for federal network visibility
- CISA is *not* a law enforcement agency — it cannot arrest, prosecute, or conduct offensive cyber operations; that authority belongs to FBI and USCYBERCOM respectively
