# regulatory fines

## What it is
Think of regulatory fines like a speeding ticket issued by a government auditor instead of a cop — you broke the posted rules, and now there's a mandatory financial penalty regardless of whether an accident actually happened. Regulatory fines are monetary penalties imposed by government or industry authorities on organizations that fail to comply with specific data protection, privacy, or security laws (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA, PCI DSS).

## Why it matters
In 2019, British Airways was fined £183 million (initially) under GDPR after attackers skimmed payment card data from 500,000 customers via a compromised script on their booking site — the fine stemmed not just from the breach, but from inadequate security controls that failed to prevent it. This illustrates that regulatory fines punish *insufficient due care*, meaning organizations must proactively implement controls, not just react to incidents.

## Key facts
- **GDPR** fines can reach €20 million or 4% of global annual turnover, whichever is higher — making large enterprises extremely exposed.
- **HIPAA** violations carry tiered civil penalties ranging from $100 to $50,000 per violation, with an annual cap of $1.9 million per violation category.
- **PCI DSS** non-compliance fines are issued by payment card brands (not governments), ranging from $5,000–$100,000/month until compliance is achieved.
- Fines can be levied even when no data was actually exfiltrated — the *failure to implement required controls* is itself the violation.
- Repeated or willful violations escalate penalties significantly; HIPAA distinguishes between "reasonable cause" and "willful neglect" tiers.
