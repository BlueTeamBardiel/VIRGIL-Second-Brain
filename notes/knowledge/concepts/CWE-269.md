# CWE-269

## What it is
Like a hospital janitor who gets handed a surgeon's master keycard "just for convenience," improper privilege management means a process or user holds more access rights than their function requires. It is the failure to correctly assign, manage, revoke, or restrict privileges across the lifecycle of users, processes, or roles within a system.

## Why it matters
In the 2020 SolarWinds breach, the compromised ORION update mechanism ran with domain-level administrative privileges — far beyond what a monitoring tool needs. This allowed attackers to move laterally across customer networks without triggering additional authentication barriers. Proper privilege scoping would have contained the blast radius significantly.

## Key facts
- CWE-269 is the parent weakness; child weaknesses include CWE-250 (Execution with Unnecessary Privileges) and CWE-732 (Incorrect Permission Assignment for Critical Resource)
- Violates the **Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP)** — every subject should operate with the minimum permissions required to complete its task
- Common in service accounts and scheduled tasks that are granted permanent admin rights instead of just-in-time (JIT) access
- Privilege mismanagement is a leading enabler of **privilege escalation attacks**, which appear in the MITRE ATT&CK framework under TA0004
- NIST SP 800-53 control **AC-6** directly addresses least privilege enforcement as a countermeasure
