# Lazarus Group

## What it is
Imagine a state-owned construction company that also moonlights as an armed robbery crew — that's Lazarus Group: a North Korean government-sponsored Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) actor (tracked as APT38) that simultaneously conducts espionage, sabotage, and financially motivated cybercrime to fund the regime. Unlike most nation-state actors who avoid financial crime, Lazarus uniquely blurs the line between intelligence operations and bank robbery.

## Why it matters
In 2016, Lazarus operatives used fraudulent SWIFT banking messages to attempt a $1 billion theft from Bangladesh's central bank, successfully stealing $81 million — demonstrating that nation-state actors can directly attack financial infrastructure for profit rather than intelligence. Defenders must treat anomalous SWIFT transactions and insider-style lateral movement as potential nation-state indicators, not just fraud.

## Key facts
- **Attribution**: Linked to North Korea's Reconnaissance General Bureau (RGB); active since at least 2009
- **WannaCry 2017**: Lazarus deployed this ransomworm affecting 200,000+ systems across 150 countries, including the UK's NHS, causing ~$4 billion in damages
- **Sony Pictures Hack (2014)**: Destroyed thousands of workstations using Destover wiper malware in retaliation for *The Interview* film — a rare destructive nation-state attack on a private company
- **Cryptocurrency theft**: Responsible for stealing over $3 billion in crypto assets between 2017–2023, including the $625 million Ronin Network bridge hack (2022)
- **TTPs**: Known for spear-phishing, supply chain compromise, custom malware families (BLINDINGCAN, HOPLIGHT), and using living-off-the-land techniques to evade detection
