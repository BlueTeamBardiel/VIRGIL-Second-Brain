# questionnaires

## What it is
Like a doctor's intake form that asks about allergies before prescribing medicine, a security questionnaire is a structured set of questions used to systematically gather information about an organization's security posture, controls, and risk exposure. It is a passive reconnaissance and assessment technique used in both ethical security engagements and vendor risk management to document the current state of security without active probing.

## Why it matters
During a third-party vendor assessment, a security team sends a standardized questionnaire (such as the SIG or CAIQ) to a cloud provider to evaluate whether they meet compliance requirements before granting access to sensitive data. Conversely, attackers conducting social engineering campaigns use informal "questionnaires" disguised as IT surveys or phishing pretexts to extract employee credentials, network details, and security tool information without ever touching a system.

## Key facts
- Questionnaires are a core component of **passive reconnaissance** in penetration testing methodology and appear in the planning/scoping phase of engagements
- The **Standardized Information Gathering (SIG) questionnaire** and **CAIQ (Consensus Assessments Initiative Questionnaire)** are widely used frameworks for cloud and vendor security assessments
- Under **NIST SP 800-30**, questionnaires are a formal risk identification technique used to collect threat and vulnerability data from stakeholders
- Questionnaires support **supply chain risk management (SCRM)** by evaluating third-party controls before onboarding — directly relevant to CySA+ Domain 4
- Responses to questionnaires are **self-reported** and unverified unless paired with evidence review or on-site audit, making them susceptible to inaccuracy or deliberate misrepresentation
