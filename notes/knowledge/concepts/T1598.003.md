# T1598.003

## What it is
Like a con artist sending a fake survey from "your bank" to harvest your account details before the real heist, spearphishing for information uses targeted, convincing emails designed not to deliver malware — but purely to trick victims into volunteering credentials or sensitive data. This sub-technique involves crafting highly personalized phishing messages aimed at reconnaissance, where the attacker's goal is intelligence gathering rather than direct system compromise.

## Why it matters
In the 2020 SolarWinds supply chain compromise, attackers conducted pre-intrusion reconnaissance by sending targeted emails impersonating trusted vendors to harvest login credentials and internal network details from IT staff. This reconnaissance phase fed directly into the broader campaign, demonstrating how information-gathering phishing can silently enable catastrophic downstream attacks without triggering endpoint detection tools looking for malicious payloads.

## Key facts
- **No malicious payload required** — the attack succeeds entirely through social engineering, making it harder for email gateways and AV tools to flag
- Attackers often impersonate HR departments, IT helpdesks, or trusted vendors to establish legitimacy before requesting sensitive data
- Collected information (credentials, org charts, VPN details) feeds into subsequent ATT&CK stages like Initial Access (T1078 — Valid Accounts)
- This technique falls under the **Reconnaissance tactic (TA0043)** in the MITRE ATT&CK framework, distinguishing it from execution-phase phishing
- Defense relies heavily on **Security Awareness Training**, **DMARC/DKIM/SPF email authentication**, and anomalous email behavior monitoring (e.g., unexpected credential request patterns)
