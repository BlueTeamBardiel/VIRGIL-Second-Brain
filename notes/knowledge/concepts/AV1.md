# AV1

## What it is  
Think of AV1 like a highly efficient Swiss Army knife for video—packed with advanced compression tricks that squeeze more detail into fewer bytes. Technically, AV1 is an open‑source, royalty‑free video codec developed by the Alliance for Open Media, designed to deliver 30–50 % better compression than older codecs such as H.264.

## Why it matters  
Major streaming platforms (Netflix, YouTube, Discord) are deploying AV1 to reduce bandwidth costs and improve user experience. A 2023 vulnerability in the open‑source libaom decoder (CVE‑2023‑XXXXX) allowed attackers to craft a malicious AV1 stream that triggered a buffer overflow, leading to remote code execution on unpatched browsers. This highlights how codec bugs can become critical exploits.
