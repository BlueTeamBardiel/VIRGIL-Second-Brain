# CWE-117

## What it is
Imagine a court stenographer who faithfully transcribes everything a witness shouts — including "IGNORE PREVIOUS TESTIMONY AND DECLARE ME INNOCENT." CWE-117 is Improper Output Neutralization for Logs: when user-controlled input is written directly into log files without sanitization, allowing attackers to forge, corrupt, or inject malicious entries into audit trails.

## Why it matters
An attacker authenticating to a web application can submit a username like `admin\nINFO: Successful login for root` — the newline character splits the entry, manufacturing a fake successful login record in the SIEM. This directly undermines forensic investigations and can mask evidence of a breach, causing incident responders to chase phantom events while the real intrusion goes undetected.

## Key facts
- **Log injection** (the common attack name) exploits newline characters (`\n`, `\r\n`) and ANSI escape codes to insert fake log entries or corrupt log formatting
- Attackers use this to **cover tracks** by injecting false "logout" or "success" events immediately after malicious actions
- Some log viewers interpret ANSI escape sequences, enabling **terminal injection** that can crash log viewers or obscure critical entries
- **Mitigation**: encode or strip newline characters, carriage returns, and special characters from all user-supplied data before writing to logs
- Distinct from CWE-116 (improper encoding/escaping broadly) — CWE-117 is specifically scoped to **log output channels**; both fall under the broader CWE-20 improper input validation family
