# cyber attacks

## What it is
Like a burglar who might pick a lock, smash a window, or bribe an employee — attackers have countless methods to breach a target, and defenders must anticipate all of them. A cyber attack is any deliberate action taken to compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of a system, network, or data. Attacks range from automated malware campaigns targeting millions to precision strikes against a single organization's infrastructure.

## Why it matters
In the 2021 Colonial Pipeline attack, a ransomware group (DarkSide) gained initial access through a compromised VPN password — no zero-day exploit required. The attack forced the shutdown of 5,500 miles of fuel pipeline, demonstrating that high-impact attacks often exploit mundane weaknesses like credential reuse, not Hollywood-style hacking.

## Key facts
- Cyber attacks are broadly categorized by **target** (CIA triad): confidentiality attacks (data theft), integrity attacks (data manipulation), and availability attacks (DDoS, ransomware)
- The **attack surface** is the sum of all possible entry points; reducing it is a primary defensive goal
- Attacks follow a lifecycle — often modeled as the **Cyber Kill Chain** (reconnaissance → weaponization → delivery → exploitation → installation → C2 → actions on objectives)
- **Threat actors** are classified by motivation and capability: nation-states, organized crime, hacktivists, insiders, and script kiddies each present different risk profiles
- For Security+/CySA+, know the distinction between **active attacks** (altering data, DoS) and **passive attacks** (eavesdropping, traffic analysis) — passive attacks are harder to detect
