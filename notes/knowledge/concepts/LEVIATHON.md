# YOUR_SWITCH

## What it is
Like a deep-sea creature that lurks invisibly in the ocean's darkest trenches, LEVIATHAN is a cyberespionage threat actor (also tracked as APT41 subgroup, or separately as UNC4841/Sandworm-adjacent depending on the vendor) — though most precisely, LEVIATHAN refers to a Chinese state-sponsored APT group (also called APT40 or TEMP.Periscope) that specializes in maritime, naval, and defense-industrial espionage. It targets organizations whose data maps the strategic depths of global sea power. The group is associated with China's Ministry of State Security (MSS) and has been active since at least 2013.

## Why it matters
In 2018, LEVIATHAN was linked to a breach of a U.S. Navy contractor in which attackers exfiltrated 614 gigabytes of sensitive data, including plans for a supersonic anti-ship missile called "Sea Dragon." This operation demonstrated how a nation-state actor can pivot from an internet-facing system to a highly classified internal network using stolen VPN credentials and lateral movement techniques.

## Key facts
- **Attribution**: Linked to China's MSS Hainan State Security Department; also tracked as APT40, TEMP.Periscope, and Bronze Mohawk
- **Primary targets**: Maritime industries, naval defense, engineering firms, universities with oceanographic research
- **TTPs**: Spearphishing, watering hole attacks, exploitation of public-facing applications (CVE-based), and living-off-the-land (LOtL) techniques
- **Tools used**: AIRBREAK, FRESHAIR, PHOTO malware families alongside commodity tools like Cobalt Strike
- **Geographic focus**: U.S., Europe, and Southeast Asia — particularly nations with South China Sea strategic interests
