# SOC 2

## What it is
Think of SOC 2 like a restaurant health inspection — except instead of checking the kitchen for rats, auditors check whether a cloud service provider handles your data with proper security controls. SOC 2 (System and Organization Controls 2) is an auditing framework developed by the AICPA that evaluates a service organization's controls across five Trust Service Criteria: Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, and Privacy. Unlike a certification, it produces an auditor's opinion report, not a pass/fail certificate.

## Why it matters
When a company like Dropbox or Salesforce stores your organization's sensitive data, you need assurance their controls actually work — not just their word for it. A SOC 2 Type II report covering a 12-month period would have revealed whether a cloud vendor was logging access events, enforcing MFA, and detecting anomalies — controls that directly limit blast radius during a credential-stuffing attack against their platform.

## Key facts
- **Two types**: SOC 2 Type I evaluates control design at a *point in time*; Type II evaluates operational effectiveness over a *period* (typically 6–12 months) — Type II carries significantly more weight
- **Five Trust Service Criteria (TSC)**: Security (Common Criteria) is the only *required* criterion; the other four are optional add-ons
- **Not a certification**: SOC 2 produces an auditor's *attestation report*, not a compliance badge — reports are typically shared under NDA
- **Auditors**: Must be licensed CPA firms — internal teams cannot self-certify SOC 2
- **Scope matters**: A vendor can have a clean SOC 2 report that doesn't cover the specific system storing your data — always verify what's in scope
