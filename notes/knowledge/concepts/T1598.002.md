# T1598.002

## What it is
Like a con artist sending a fake bank survey to harvest your account details, spear phishing for information is when attackers craft targeted, personalized emails designed not to deliver malware — but purely to trick victims into surrendering sensitive data directly. This sub-technique (under Phishing for Information) specifically uses spear phishing lures tailored to a specific individual or organization to elicit credentials, internal documents, or reconnaissance data.

## Why it matters
Before the 2016 DNC breach, attackers sent targeted emails to specific staffers asking them to "verify" their Google credentials — the replies and clicks handed over login details without a single piece of malware being deployed. This illustrates why perimeter defenses alone fail: the attack exploits human trust, not technical vulnerabilities, making detection exceptionally difficult without behavioral training and email telemetry analysis.

## Key facts
- Differs from T1598.001 (Spear Phishing Service) and T1598.003 (Spear Phishing Link) — this variant focuses on direct information elicitation, often through reply-based social engineering
- The goal is **reconnaissance and credential harvesting**, not initial system access — it supports later attack phases
- Attackers frequently impersonate HR, IT helpdesks, or executives to manufacture urgency and legitimacy
- No malicious payload means traditional AV/EDR tools generate **no alerts** — detection relies on email header analysis and user reporting
- MITRE classifies this under the **Reconnaissance** tactic (TA0043), not Initial Access — a critical distinction for exam questions
