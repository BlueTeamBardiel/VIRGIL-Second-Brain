# Charming Kitten

## What it is
Think of a con artist who spends months befriending a target on social media before ever asking for anything — that's Charming Kitten's signature move. Charming Kitten (also tracked as APT35, Phosphorus, and Mint Sandstorm) is an Iranian state-sponsored advanced persistent threat group that conducts long-term spear-phishing and social engineering campaigns, primarily targeting journalists, academics, diplomats, and human rights activists.

## Why it matters
In 2021, Charming Kitten targeted medical researchers and epidemiologists involved in COVID-19 vaccine development, using fake conference invitations and spoofed Google Meet links to harvest credentials. This illustrates how nation-state actors exploit high-value geopolitical moments to steal intellectual property and intelligence through deception rather than brute-force exploitation.

## Key facts
- **Attribution:** Linked to Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC); active since approximately 2013
- **Primary TTPs:** Spear-phishing, credential harvesting via fake login portals, and SMS-based multi-factor authentication bypass (MFA fatigue and SIM-related attacks)
- **MITRE ATT&CK alignment:** T1566 (Phishing), T1078 (Valid Accounts), T1598 (Phishing for Information) — commonly tested in CySA+ threat intelligence scenarios
- **Notable tool:** Used a custom Android malware called **POWERSTAR** (formerly CharmPower) for post-compromise surveillance on mobile devices
- **Target sectors:** Academia, media, government, defense contractors, and NGOs — consistent with Iranian geopolitical intelligence priorities
