# Job Search

## What it is
Like a fisherman casting different lures into different waters, a job search is the systematic process of identifying, targeting, and applying for employment opportunities across multiple channels simultaneously. In cybersecurity contexts, it specifically refers to the process of locating roles, building credentials, networking, and navigating hiring pipelines for security-focused positions. Understanding this process is itself a security-relevant skill, as attackers actively exploit job seekers.

## Why it matters
Threat actors use fake job postings as phishing vectors — a documented tactic used against aerospace and defense workers (Operation Dream Job by Lazarus Group) where malicious "recruiters" delivered malware disguised as job offer documents. Defenders must recognize that LinkedIn messages containing unexpected attachments or links to "job portals" requiring credential entry are high-probability social engineering attempts. Job seekers with security clearances are specifically targeted for credential harvesting.

## Key facts
- **Spear phishing via recruitment** is a recognized attack vector; attackers research targets on LinkedIn to craft highly personalized lures referencing real job titles and companies
- Legitimate employers will **never** request SSN, banking info, or credentials before a formal, verified offer — doing so is a red flag for fraud
- Security clearance holders are **high-value targets**; discussing clearance status publicly on job boards violates OPSEC best practices
- Job boards can be used for **OSINT reconnaissance** — adversaries harvest employee names, org structures, and technology stacks from job postings
- During a job search, **digital hygiene** matters: use unique email addresses per platform, enable MFA on career accounts, and treat recruiter links as untrusted URLs
