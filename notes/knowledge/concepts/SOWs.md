# SOWs

## What it is
Like a contractor's blueprint that specifies exactly which walls to knock down and which to leave standing, a Statement of Work (SOW) is a formal document that defines the precise scope, deliverables, timelines, and responsibilities for a security engagement or vendor contract. It transforms vague agreements like "test our security" into legally binding, measurable commitments.

## Why it matters
During a penetration testing engagement, a poorly written SOW that fails to explicitly exclude production databases can result in a tester legally — but disastrously — taking down a live e-commerce system during business hours. Conversely, a well-crafted SOW that specifies "external network testing only, 2AM–5AM weekdays" protects both the client and the pentester from scope creep, legal liability, and unintended outages.

## Key facts
- A SOW is a component of a Master Service Agreement (MSA) and specifies the *what*, *when*, and *how* of a particular engagement — not just the general relationship terms
- In security contexts, SOWs must define **rules of engagement (ROE)**, including authorized IP ranges, testing windows, and out-of-scope systems
- SOWs establish **acceptance criteria** — measurable conditions that confirm deliverables are complete (e.g., "final penetration test report delivered within 10 business days of testing completion")
- Missing or ambiguous SOW language is a common source of **third-party risk** — vendors may claim work outside original scope incurs extra cost, or worse, access sensitive data beyond their mandate
- For Security+/CySA+: SOWs appear in the context of **vendor management**, **risk assessments**, and **penetration testing authorization** — know that they differ from an SLA (which governs *performance standards*) and an NDA (which governs *confidentiality*)
