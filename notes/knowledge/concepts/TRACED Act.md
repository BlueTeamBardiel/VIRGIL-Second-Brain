# TRACED Act

## What it is
Like a neighborhood watch program deputizing residents to report suspicious vehicles AND requiring car manufacturers to install anti-theft locks, the TRACED Act both empowers carriers to block robocalls AND mandates technical authentication standards. Formally, the Telephone Robocall Abuse Criminal Enforcement and Deterrence Act (2019) is a U.S. federal law requiring telephone carriers to implement STIR/SHAKEN caller ID authentication and grants the FCC expanded authority to pursue robocall perpetrators.

## Why it matters
Vishing (voice phishing) attacks frequently exploit spoofed caller ID — an attacker impersonates the IRS or a bank by displaying a trusted phone number, tricking victims into surrendering credentials or wire-transferring funds. STIR/SHAKEN, mandated by TRACED, cryptographically signs calls at origination so receiving carriers can verify whether the displayed number is legitimate, directly disrupting this social engineering attack vector.

## Key facts
- **STIR/SHAKEN** = Secure Telephone Identity Revisited / Signature-based Handling of Asserted information using toKENs — the technical protocol framework TRACED mandates
- Requires major voice carriers to implement STIR/SHAKEN by **June 30, 2021**; smaller carriers had extended deadlines
- Expands the **statute of limitations** for robocall violations from 2 to **4 years**
- The FCC can issue fines up to **$10,000 per call** for intentional violations under TRACED authority
- Mandates a **Do Not Originate (DNO)** list to prevent spoofing of numbers that never make outbound calls (e.g., government agencies)
