# Sandworm Team

## What it is
Think of Sandworm like a wrecking crew hired by a landlord to destroy a building rather than rob it — their goal is destruction and disruption, not quiet theft. Sandworm Team is an Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) group attributed to Russia's GRU Unit 74455, known for deploying destructive malware against critical infrastructure, particularly power grids and industrial control systems.

## Why it matters
In December 2015 and again in 2016, Sandworm executed attacks against Ukraine's power grid using BlackEnergy and Industroyer (Crashoverride) malware, leaving hundreds of thousands of civilians without electricity in the dead of winter. These attacks demonstrated that nation-state actors could weaponize malware to physically impact industrial control systems (ICS/SCADA), forcing defenders worldwide to rethink how they segment operational technology (OT) networks from IT networks.

## Key facts
- Attributed to **GRU Unit 74455** (Russian military intelligence), active since at least 2009
- Responsible for **NotPetya (2017)**, disguised as ransomware but actually a wiper — caused over $10 billion in global damages, making it the costliest cyberattack in history
- Deployed **Industroyer/Crashoverride**, the first malware specifically designed to speak native ICS protocols (IEC 104, IEC 101, IEC 61850)
- Frequently uses **spearphishing with malicious Office documents** as initial access vector
- Targeted the **2018 Winter Olympics** with Olympic Destroyer malware, using false flag techniques to mimic North Korean TTPs
