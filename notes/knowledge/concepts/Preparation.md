# Preparation

## What it is
Like a surgeon who sterilizes instruments, reviews X-rays, and briefs the team *before* the first incision, preparation is the phase where security teams build their capability to respond *before* any incident occurs. Formally, it is the first phase of the incident response lifecycle (NIST SP 800-61), where organizations establish policies, tools, communication channels, and trained personnel to handle security events effectively.

## Why it matters
In the 2017 NotPetya attack, organizations without pre-established incident response plans, offline backups, and communication trees lost weeks of recovery time — some never fully recovered. Companies that had rehearsed tabletop exercises and maintained tested backup systems restored operations in days. The difference wasn't technical sophistication; it was preparation.

## Key facts
- NIST SP 800-61 defines six IR phases: **Preparation → Detection & Analysis → Containment → Eradication → Recovery → Post-Incident Activity** — Preparation is first
- Preparation deliverables include: an Incident Response Plan (IRP), contact lists, jump bags (forensic toolkits), communication templates, and defined roles (CISO, IR lead, legal)
- **Tabletop exercises** simulate incident scenarios without live systems; **functional exercises** test actual tools and procedures
- A **RACI matrix** (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) is a preparation artifact that prevents role confusion during live incidents
- SANS uses a slightly different model (PICERL): **Preparation, Identification, Containment, Eradication, Recovery, Lessons Learned** — both models are exam-testable
