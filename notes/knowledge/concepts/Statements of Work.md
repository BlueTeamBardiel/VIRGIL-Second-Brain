# Statements of Work

## What it is
Like a detailed recipe given to a contractor — specifying exact ingredients, quantities, and steps rather than just saying "make something edible" — a Statement of Work (SOW) is a formal document that defines the specific tasks, deliverables, timelines, and responsibilities expected from a third-party vendor or service provider. It legally binds both parties to agreed-upon scope and performance standards.

## Why it matters
During a penetration testing engagement, a poorly written SOW that fails to specify which IP ranges, systems, or testing methods are authorized can result in testers inadvertently accessing out-of-scope systems — creating legal liability and potential criminal exposure under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act. A precise SOW acts as the legal "get out of jail free" card that protects both the tester and the organization.

## Key facts
- An SOW is a component of a broader **Master Service Agreement (MSA)** and typically references it for overarching legal terms
- SOWs must explicitly define **scope boundaries** — what IS and IS NOT included — to prevent scope creep or unauthorized access
- In security engagements, SOWs should specify **rules of engagement (ROE)**, acceptable testing windows, emergency contact procedures, and escalation paths
- SOWs are critical for **supply chain risk management** — they establish accountability when third-party vendors handle sensitive data or systems
- On Security+/CySA+ exams, SOWs appear in the context of **vendor management, third-party risk**, and ensuring contractors meet security requirements before access is granted
