# Hillary Clinton

## What it is
Like finding a master key hidden under a doormat instead of locked in a safe, the Hillary Clinton email controversy is a real-world case study of shadow IT and data spillage. It refers to the 2015–2016 security incident where the U.S. Secretary of State used a privately maintained email server (clintonemail.com) for official government communications, bypassing sanctioned federal systems and classification controls.

## Why it matters
This case is a textbook data spillage and unauthorized system use scenario: classified information traversed an unclassified, privately controlled server lacking proper access controls, encryption standards, and audit logging required by federal policy. The FBI investigation (Operation Midyear Exam) demonstrated how insider risk doesn't require malicious intent — negligence and convenience-driven workarounds create the same exposure as deliberate exfiltration.

## Key facts
- The private server lacked a security certificate for the first three months of operation, leaving credentials vulnerable to interception
- Over 110 emails were identified as containing classified information at the time of transmission; 22 were retroactively classified Top Secret/SAP
- Violates FISMA (Federal Information Security Management Act) requirements mandating use of approved federal systems for official records
- Demonstrated **data spillage**: classified data commingled with unclassified systems, triggering mandatory containment and review procedures
- Foreign adversaries (Russian, Chinese, Iranian actors) were suspected of targeting the server — illustrating how shadow IT expands the attack surface against high-value targets
