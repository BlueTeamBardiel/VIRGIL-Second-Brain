# AIOps

## What it is
Think of AIOps as giving your security team superhuman speed and pattern recognition—like having thousands of experienced analysts watching logs simultaneously, 24/7, never getting tired or missing anomalies. AIOps (Artificial Intelligence for IT Operations) uses machine learning to automate detection, diagnosis, and response to infrastructure and security events by analyzing massive volumes of operational data in real-time.

## Why it matters
When a sophisticated attacker exfiltrates data, they often leave traces across thousands of logs—network flows, system calls, authentication records. A human team might take hours or days to correlate these signals; AIOps systems detect the pattern in seconds and can trigger automated containment (isolating affected systems, blocking IPs) before the breach spreads. This speed differential is the difference between a contained incident and a catastrophic one.

## Key facts
- AIOps reduces mean time to detect (MTTD) and mean time to respond (MTTR) by orders of magnitude through correlation and automation
- ML models learn your environment's "normal" baseline, making anomalies statistically obvious rather than noise-buried
- Common applications: log aggregation analysis, alert deduplication, root cause analysis, automated incident escalation
- High false-positive rates in poorly-tuned models create alert fatigue, defeating the purpose—tuning is critical
- Complements but doesn't replace human expertise; best used as a force multiplier for security teams
