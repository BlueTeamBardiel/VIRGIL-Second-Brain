# YouTube Showcase

## What it is
Like a storefront window displaying your best merchandise to passersby, a YouTube Showcase is a curated public-facing channel or playlist used to demonstrate security tools, techniques, proof-of-concept exploits, or educational content to a broad audience. In cybersecurity contexts, it refers specifically to the practice of publishing recorded demonstrations — penetration tests, tool walkthroughs, CTF solutions, or vulnerability reproductions — on YouTube for community education, professional branding, or responsible disclosure support.

## Why it matters
Security researchers frequently use YouTube showcases as part of coordinated vulnerability disclosure, publishing exploit demonstrations after a patch is released to prove the vulnerability was real and educate defenders. For example, a researcher might showcase a buffer overflow exploit against an unpatched version of software, giving blue teams a concrete visual reference for what malicious traffic or behavior looks like in practice. Conversely, threat actors study these same videos to weaponize disclosed techniques faster than organizations can patch.

## Key facts
- YouTube showcases are commonly used in **responsible disclosure timelines** — published after the CVE patch window (typically 90 days per Google Project Zero policy)
- Demonstrations often include **proof-of-concept (PoC) code** walkthroughs, which can accelerate both defensive understanding and offensive exploitation
- Blue teams use recorded attack showcases to build **detection rules** (e.g., SIEM signatures, IDS patterns) from observed behaviors
- Showcasing tools like Metasploit, Burp Suite, or Wireshark on public platforms contributes to the **security community knowledge base** but also lowers the skill barrier for attackers
- Under **CySA+ objectives**, consuming threat intelligence from open sources (including video demonstrations) falls under OSINT and threat intelligence gathering practices
