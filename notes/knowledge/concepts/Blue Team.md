# Blue Team

## What it is
Like the immune system of a living organism — constantly patrolling, detecting foreign agents, and mounting a coordinated defense — the Blue Team is the internal security force responsible for defending an organization's systems, networks, and data against attack. They operate continuously, monitoring for threats and responding when defenses are breached.

## Why it matters
During the 2020 SolarWinds supply chain attack, Blue Teams at affected agencies had to identify anomalous outbound traffic to SUNBURST command-and-control servers that had been quietly beaconing for months. Those with mature SIEM rules, network baselines, and threat hunting practices detected the compromise faster — minimizing dwell time and limiting the blast radius of the breach.

## Key facts
- Blue Team activities include **continuous monitoring, log analysis, incident response, vulnerability management, and hardening** — mapped directly to the NIST Cybersecurity Framework's Detect, Respond, and Recover functions
- **SIEM (Security Information and Event Management)** is the Blue Team's primary visibility tool — correlating logs across endpoints, firewalls, and servers to surface suspicious patterns
- Blue Teams establish **baselines of normal behavior** so that anomalies (unusual login times, unexpected data exfiltration volumes) trigger meaningful alerts rather than noise
- **Mean Time to Detect (MTTD)** and **Mean Time to Respond (MTTR)** are the key performance metrics used to evaluate Blue Team effectiveness
- In a **Purple Team** exercise, Blue and Red Teams collaborate openly — the Red Team shares their TTPs so the Blue Team can tune detections and close gaps in real time
