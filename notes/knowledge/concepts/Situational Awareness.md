# Situational Awareness

## What it is
Like a chess player who doesn't just see the current board but anticipates three moves ahead by tracking every piece's position and history, situational awareness in cybersecurity is the continuous process of perceiving, understanding, and projecting the state of your environment. It means knowing what "normal" looks like so you can instantly recognize what doesn't belong — who is connected, what is running, and where data is flowing at any given moment.

## Why it matters
During the 2020 SolarWinds supply chain attack, organizations *with* mature situational awareness — like those using behavioral baselines and network traffic analysis — detected anomalous outbound DNS beaconing from SUNBURST malware within days. Organizations *without* it went months without noticing because they had no reference point for "normal" to compare against the intrusion.

## Key facts
- Situational awareness maps directly to Endsley's three-level model: **perception** (collecting data), **comprehension** (understanding its meaning), and **projection** (predicting future state) — a framework referenced in CySA+ objectives
- **Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)** tools are the primary technology used to operationalize situational awareness by aggregating and correlating logs across the environment
- Establishing a **baseline** of normal network/user behavior is a prerequisite — without it, anomaly detection produces only noise
- **Threat intelligence feeds** extend situational awareness beyond your perimeter by providing context about external adversary TTPs (Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures)
- CISA's continuous monitoring mandates (under FISMA) formalize situational awareness as a compliance requirement for federal agencies, making it exam-relevant for governance questions
