# Emerging Threats

## What it is
Like a virus mutating faster than vaccines can be developed, emerging threats are attack techniques, tools, or vectors that outpace existing defenses and detection signatures. Precisely defined: they are newly identified or evolving attack methodologies that exploit gaps in current security controls, threat intelligence, or organizational awareness — often before vendors release patches or detection rules.

## Why it matters
In 2021, the Log4Shell vulnerability (CVE-2021-44228) emerged as a zero-day in the ubiquitous Log4j library, giving attackers remote code execution across millions of systems before most organizations even knew their exposure. Security teams scrambling to identify every asset running Log4j demonstrated exactly why threat hunting and asset inventory are critical defenses against emerging threats — reactive patching alone isn't fast enough.

## Key facts
- **AI-assisted attacks** represent a growing emerging threat category — LLMs are being weaponized to write polymorphic malware and craft highly convincing spear-phishing campaigns at scale
- **Living-off-the-land (LotL) techniques** are an emerging evasion trend where attackers use legitimate OS tools (PowerShell, WMI) to avoid triggering traditional AV signatures
- **Supply chain attacks** (e.g., SolarWinds) are classified as emerging threats because they exploit trusted vendor relationships that most security models assumed were safe
- **ISAC (Information Sharing and Analysis Centers)** are primary mechanisms for distributing emerging threat intelligence across industry sectors
- The **MITRE ATT&CK framework** is actively updated with new TTPs (Tactics, Techniques, Procedures) as emerging threats are catalogued — CySA+ expects you to know this as a living threat reference
