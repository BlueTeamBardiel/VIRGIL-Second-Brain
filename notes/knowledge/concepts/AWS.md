# AWS

## What it is  
Think of AWS as a giant, on‑demand toolkit for building, deploying, and scaling IT infrastructure—everything from servers to storage, all rented from Amazon’s data centers instead of owned on‑prem. It provides a suite of cloud services (compute, database, analytics, networking, etc.) that customers can spin up and pay for by the hour or second.

## Why it matters  
In 2020, a mis‑configured Amazon S3 bucket exposed 500+ sensitive customer records, illustrating how cloud mis‑management can turn a powerful platform into an attack vector. Conversely, defenders use AWS IAM roles and policies to enforce least‑privilege access, hardening cloud environments against insider threats and credential theft.

## Key facts  
- **Regions & Availability Zones**: Global distribution of isolated data centers ensures resilience and compliance with data‑locality laws.
