# risk assessment

## What it is
Like a ship captain surveying weather charts, reef maps, and crew experience before leaving port — risk assessment is the structured process of identifying assets, cataloging threats, analyzing vulnerabilities, and calculating the likelihood and impact of harm. It produces a prioritized picture of what could go wrong and how badly, so defenders spend effort where it actually matters.

## Why it matters
After the 2013 Target breach, post-incident analysis revealed that a formal risk assessment would have flagged third-party HVAC vendor access to the internal network as a critical attack vector. Forty million credit card records were stolen through a path that a basic threat-likelihood × impact calculation would have marked high-priority for network segmentation controls.

## Key facts
- **Risk formula**: Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Asset Value — understanding this equation is foundational for Security+ exam questions
- **Qualitative vs. quantitative**: Qualitative uses High/Medium/Low ratings; quantitative uses dollar figures like **ALE (Annualized Loss Expectancy) = SLE × ARO** (Single Loss Expectancy × Annual Rate of Occurrence)
- **Four risk responses**: Accept, Avoid, Transfer (e.g., cyber insurance), Mitigate — every control decision maps to one of these
- **Residual risk** is what remains after controls are applied; it must be explicitly accepted by management, not ignored
- NIST SP 800-30 is the authoritative U.S. government guide for conducting IT risk assessments and is directly referenced in CySA+ objectives
