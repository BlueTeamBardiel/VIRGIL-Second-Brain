# User Training

## What it is
Like teaching everyone in an office building to recognize a fire — not just the fire marshal — user training turns every employee into an active layer of defense rather than a passive vulnerability. It is the process of educating non-technical staff on security policies, threat recognition, and safe behaviors to reduce human-factor risk across an organization.

## Why it matters
In the 2016 Democratic National Committee breach, spear-phishing emails tricked staff into surrendering credentials — a technical firewall would not have stopped it, but a user who recognized the red flags might have. Effective training programs teaching employees to verify sender addresses, hover over links, and report suspicious emails directly reduce the success rate of social engineering campaigns. Security awareness training is consistently cited as one of the highest-ROI defensive investments available.

## Key facts
- **Security Awareness Training** is a formal control category in frameworks like NIST SP 800-50 and is required under compliance standards such as HIPAA and PCI-DSS.
- Simulated phishing campaigns (e.g., using GoPhish) measure training effectiveness with a **click rate metric** — a baseline above 30% typically indicates high organizational risk.
- Training must be **role-based**: finance staff need wire-fraud awareness, IT admins need privileged account hygiene, executives need whaling defense.
- **Recurring training** (at least annually, ideally quarterly with continuous micro-learning) outperforms one-time onboarding sessions in retention studies.
- The **human element** was identified as a factor in 74% of breaches (Verizon DBIR 2023), making user training a compensating control for nearly every attack category.
