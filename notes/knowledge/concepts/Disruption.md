# Disruption

## What it is
Like cutting the power cord to a hospital during surgery, disruption attacks don't steal data — they deny the ability to function. Disruption is a cyberattack objective focused on degrading, disabling, or destroying the availability of systems, services, or infrastructure. It targets the "A" in the CIA triad, making legitimate resources unreachable or inoperable.

## Why it matters
In 2021, the Colonial Pipeline ransomware attack disrupted fuel distribution across the U.S. East Coast for six days — not because data was permanently destroyed, but because operators shut down systems to contain the threat. This illustrates how disruption can cascade from a single compromised network segment into a national-scale availability crisis, even when the attacker's primary motive was financial.

## Key facts
- Disruption attacks target **availability** and include DoS/DDoS attacks, ransomware, wiper malware, and logic bombs
- **Wiper malware** (e.g., NotPetya, Shamoon) is purpose-built for disruption — it overwrites MBRs or file systems with no recovery mechanism
- Disruption differs from **destruction**: disruption is temporary denial; destruction is permanent damage to data or hardware
- Against critical infrastructure, disruption is classified under **ICS/SCADA attacks** and may trigger national security responses
- Mitigations include redundancy, failover architecture, DDoS scrubbing services, and tested **Business Continuity Plans (BCP)**
