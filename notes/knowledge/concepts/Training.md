# Training

## What it is
Like a fire drill that makes evacuation instinctive before smoke fills the hallway, security awareness training builds muscle memory for threats before they arrive. It is the structured process of educating users and security personnel on recognizing threats, following policies, and responding correctly to incidents. Training transforms policy documents from shelf-ware into practiced behavior.

## Why it matters
In the 2016 Democratic National Committee breach, spear-phishing emails bypassed technical controls entirely because users lacked training to identify suspicious OAuth permission requests. A single click by an untrained staffer handed attackers persistent access to thousands of emails. Effective phishing simulation training — where users receive fake phishing emails and immediate corrective feedback — measurably reduces click rates from ~30% to under 5% within months.

## Key facts
- **Security awareness training** is a required control under frameworks like HIPAA, PCI-DSS, and NIST SP 800-53, not optional best practice
- **Role-based training** differentiates content by job function — developers receive secure coding training, executives receive social engineering scenarios, admins receive privileged access hygiene
- **Phishing simulations** are the most empirically validated training method; click rates and report rates are measurable KPIs tracked in CySA+ environments
- **Insider threat programs** depend heavily on training employees to recognize behavioral indicators and report anomalies through proper channels without fear of retaliation
- The **human element** was identified as a factor in 74% of breaches (Verizon DBIR 2023), making training one of the highest-ROI security investments
