# CWE-250

## What it is
Like a bank teller who also has the master key to the vault, a janitor's closet, and the alarm system — just in case — execution with unnecessary privileges means a process runs with far more permissions than its actual job requires. Precisely: CWE-250 occurs when software executes with elevated privileges beyond what is needed to perform its intended function, violating the Principle of Least Privilege.

## Why it matters
In 2021, a local privilege escalation in Polkit (CVE-2021-4034, "PwnKit") exploited a SUID-root binary that ran with full root privileges even during routine operations. An unprivileged local attacker could leverage this to gain complete system control — damage that would have been contained if the binary had dropped root privileges immediately after the one action that required them.

## Key facts
- Violates the **Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP)**, a foundational security design principle tested on Security+ and CySA+
- Common in SUID/SGID binaries on Linux and services running as SYSTEM/Administrator on Windows instead of a dedicated low-privilege service account
- Attack impact is **amplified**: a buffer overflow or injection vulnerability in an over-privileged process yields attacker capabilities equal to the process's permissions
- Mitigation includes **privilege dropping** — acquiring elevated privileges briefly for a specific task, then immediately reverting to a lower-privilege context
- Distinct from **CWE-269 (Improper Privilege Management)**, which covers broader mismanagement; CWE-250 specifically targets *unnecessary* privilege at execution time
