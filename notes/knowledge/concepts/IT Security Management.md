# IT security management

## What it is
Think of it like running a hospital: you need policies for who handles medications, audits to catch errors, and incident protocols when something goes wrong — not just good doctors. IT security management is the structured discipline of governing people, processes, and technology to protect organizational information assets through policies, risk management, and continuous oversight.

## Why it matters
In 2013, Target suffered a breach exposing 40 million credit cards — not because their firewalls failed, but because management failures allowed a third-party HVAC vendor to have unchecked network access. Strong IT security management would have enforced vendor access controls, network segmentation policies, and anomaly alerting — stopping the breach at the process level before technical exploitation mattered.

## Key facts
- **CIA Triad** is the foundational management framework: Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability must be balanced in every security decision
- **ISO/IEC 27001** and **NIST SP 800-53** are the two dominant standards frameworks for building an Information Security Management System (ISMS)
- Security management operates across three layers: **administrative controls** (policies), **technical controls** (firewalls, encryption), and **physical controls** (locks, cameras)
- **Due care** means implementing reasonable safeguards; **due diligence** means continuously verifying they work — both are legal and managerial obligations
- Risk management follows: Identify → Assess → Treat (accept, mitigate, transfer, avoid) → Monitor — and this cycle never stops
