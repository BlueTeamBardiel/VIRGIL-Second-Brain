# StrangeBee

## What it is
Think of StrangeBee as the company that built the "mission control center" for incident responders — the organization behind TheHive and Cortex, two of the most widely adopted open-source security operations platforms. StrangeBee is a French cybersecurity company that commercially develops and maintains TheHive (a scalable SOAR/case management platform) and Cortex (an observable analysis and active response engine), providing both community and enterprise editions.

## Why it matters
During a ransomware incident, a SOC team using TheHive by StrangeBee can centralize all alerts from SIEMs like Splunk, assign tasks to analysts, track IOCs, and trigger automated Cortex analyzers to enrich suspicious IP addresses or file hashes — all within a single coordinated workflow. Without a platform like this, analysts work in silos, duplicate effort, and lose critical investigation threads. StrangeBee's tools directly enable the kind of structured, repeatable incident response required by frameworks like NIST SP 800-61.

## Key facts
- StrangeBee was founded by the original creators of TheHive Project after spinning it into a commercial entity, maintaining a strong open-source community commitment
- TheHive integrates natively with MISP (Malware Information Sharing Platform) for threat intelligence sharing during active investigations
- Cortex connects to 300+ analyzers and responders (e.g., VirusTotal, Shodan, abuse.ch) to automate observable enrichment
- TheHive supports multi-tenancy, making it suitable for MSSPs managing multiple client environments simultaneously
- StrangeBee operates under a dual-license model: open-source community edition and a paid enterprise edition with advanced features like RBAC and audit logs
