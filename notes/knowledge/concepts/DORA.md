# DORA

## What it is
Like a fire drill that financial regulators actually *grade you on*, DORA is a mandatory EU resilience framework — the Digital Operational Resilience Act — that legally requires financial entities to prove they can detect, withstand, and recover from ICT-related disruptions. It entered into force in January 2025 and applies to banks, insurers, investment firms, and their critical third-party ICT providers operating in the EU.

## Why it matters
In 2023, a ransomware attack on ION Trading UK disrupted derivatives clearing for dozens of banks and brokerages across Europe, exposing how a single third-party ICT provider failure cascades into systemic financial disruption. DORA directly addresses this by requiring financial entities to map, monitor, and contractually bind their ICT third-party providers — meaning a firm can now be fined not just for their own breach, but for failing to manage supplier risk.

## Key facts
- **Scope**: Covers 20+ types of financial entities plus "critical ICT third-party providers" (CTPPs) who can be directly supervised by EU authorities
- **Five pillars**: ICT risk management, ICT-related incident reporting, digital operational resilience testing, ICT third-party risk management, and information sharing
- **TLPT requirement**: Threat-Led Penetration Testing (TLPT) — analogous to TIBER-EU — is mandatory for significant entities every 3 years
- **Incident reporting timeline**: Major ICT incidents must be reported to competent authorities within 4 hours of classification, with detailed follow-up within 72 hours
- **Contractual mandates**: Contracts with ICT vendors must include audit rights, exit strategies, and security SLAs — no contract, no service
