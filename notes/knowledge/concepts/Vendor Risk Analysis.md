# vendor risk analysis

## What it is
Like a restaurant inspecting its food suppliers before trusting them to stock the kitchen, vendor risk analysis is the process of evaluating third-party organizations before granting them access to your systems, data, or infrastructure. It formally assesses a vendor's security posture, compliance standing, and operational reliability to determine whether the risk of the relationship is acceptable.

## Why it matters
The 2013 Target breach began with attackers compromising Fazio Mechanical Services, an HVAC vendor with network access to Target's environment — ultimately exposing 40 million credit card numbers. A rigorous vendor risk analysis would have caught that Fazio had inadequate security controls and should never have been granted persistent network connectivity to payment infrastructure.

## Key facts
- **Due diligence questionnaires** (DDQs) and frameworks like SOC 2 reports, ISO 27001 certifications, and NIST CSF self-assessments are primary tools for evaluating vendor controls
- Vendors are typically classified by **criticality tier** — higher data access or system integration means stricter vetting requirements and more frequent reassessment
- **Right-to-audit clauses** in vendor contracts give organizations legal standing to inspect a vendor's security practices independently
- Vendor risk is a key component of **supply chain risk management (SCRM)**, addressed directly in NIST SP 800-161 and referenced on Security+/CySA+ exams
- Risk decisions produce one of four outcomes: **accept, avoid, transfer (contractually), or mitigate** — the same risk treatment options applied to internal risks
