# T1078

## What it is
Like a thief who steals a master key instead of picking the lock, Valid Accounts abuse legitimate credentials to move through systems without triggering alarms. Precisely: T1078 describes adversaries obtaining and using existing account credentials — default, local, domain, or cloud — to authenticate to systems and maintain persistence while blending in with normal user activity.

## Why it matters
In the 2020 SolarWinds compromise, attackers used forged SAML tokens tied to valid service accounts to pivot across Microsoft 365 environments for months undetected. Because all activity appeared as legitimate authentication events, traditional signature-based detection was nearly blind to the intrusion — only behavioral analytics eventually surfaced anomalies.

## Key facts
- Covers four account sub-types: Default Accounts (T1078.001), Domain Accounts (T1078.002), Local Accounts (T1078.003), and Cloud Accounts (T1078.004)
- Commonly paired with credential dumping (T1003) or phishing — attackers harvest creds first, then use T1078 for persistence and lateral movement
- Valid account use suppresses many alerts because it bypasses authentication controls entirely rather than exploiting a vulnerability
- Detection relies on behavioral baselines: impossible travel, logon at unusual hours, first-time access to sensitive resources
- Mitigations include privileged account management, MFA enforcement, and disabling default/guest accounts before deployment
