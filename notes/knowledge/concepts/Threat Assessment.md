# Threat Assessment

## What it is
Like a chess player studying their opponent's past games, opening preferences, and resources before a match, a threat assessment is the structured process of identifying, analyzing, and prioritizing potential adversaries and their capabilities. It evaluates *who* might attack, *what* they're capable of, and *how likely* they are to act against a specific target.

## Why it matters
In 2021, Colonial Pipeline's attackers (DarkSide) were a known ransomware-as-a-service group with documented TTPs. A proper threat assessment mapping DarkSide's history of targeting critical infrastructure would have elevated defensive priorities around VPN credentials and lateral movement — the exact vectors they exploited. Organizations that had performed threat assessments against RaaS groups were better positioned to harden those specific weaknesses in advance.

## Key facts
- Threat assessments evaluate three core components: **threat actor** (who), **capability** (can they do it?), and **intent** (will they do it?) — all three must be present for a credible threat
- Outputs feed directly into **risk assessments** — threat is one variable in the risk formula: *Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Impact*
- NIST SP 800-30 provides the standard framework for conducting threat assessments within a broader risk management process
- Threat sources are categorized as **adversarial** (hackers, insiders, nation-states), **accidental** (user error), **structural** (hardware failure), and **environmental** (natural disasters)
- On CySA+, threat assessments are distinguished from **vulnerability assessments** — threat assessment asks *who and why*, vulnerability assessment asks *where are the weaknesses*
