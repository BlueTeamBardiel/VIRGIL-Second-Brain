# T1133

## What it is
Like leaving a spare key under the doormat so you can get back into your house without knocking — external remote services are legitimate access portals (VPNs, RDP, Citrix, SSH) that attackers abuse to re-enter a network without needing to break anything. Precisely, T1133 describes adversaries leveraging internet-facing remote access services to gain initial access or maintain persistence using valid or stolen credentials.

## Why it matters
During the 2021 Pulse Secure VPN attacks, threat actors exploited unpatched CVEs combined with T1133 techniques to authenticate into government and defense networks, bypass MFA in some cases, and establish persistent footholds — all while appearing as legitimate remote workers. Defenders who monitored for anomalous login times, unusual source geographies, and concurrent sessions caught the intrusion; those who didn't lost months of dwell time.

## Key facts
- T1133 sits under both **Initial Access** and **Persistence** tactics in MITRE ATT&CK — one of few techniques that spans both
- Common target services: **VPN gateways, RDP (port 3389), Citrix ADC, SSH, and VMware Horizon**
- Attackers frequently pair T1133 with **stolen credentials** from prior phishing or credential dumps — no exploit required
- Detection signatures include logins **outside business hours**, **impossible travel** (two logins from different countries within minutes), and **new device fingerprints**
- Mitigation priority: **MFA on all external-facing services**, network segmentation, and disabling unused remote access portals
