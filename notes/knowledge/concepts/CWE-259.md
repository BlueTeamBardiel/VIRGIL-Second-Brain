# CWE-259

## What it is
Like hiding your house key under the same doormat for years and writing its location in your diary — a hard-coded password is a credential permanently baked into source code or a binary, unchangeable without modifying and redeploying the application itself. CWE-259 specifically covers cases where a single, static password is embedded directly in code, configuration files, or compiled executables.

## Why it matters
In 2016, researchers discovered that Fortinet's FortiOS SSH daemon contained a hard-coded password ("FGTAbc11*xy+Zy") granting backdoor administrative access to any device running affected firmware. An attacker who extracted this credential from one firmware image could authenticate to thousands of enterprise firewalls worldwide — no brute force required, just one leaked secret multiplied at scale.

## Key facts
- Hard-coded passwords are classified under OWASP Top 10 as **A07:2021 – Identification and Authentication Failures**
- Tools like **Trufflehog**, **GitLeaks**, and **grep** can scan repositories and binaries to detect embedded credentials
- The vulnerability persists even after "deletion" — git history retains committed secrets unless history is actively scrubbed
- CVSS scores for hard-coded credential flaws frequently reach **9.8 (Critical)** due to low attack complexity and no privileges required
- Remediation requires externalizing credentials into environment variables, secrets managers (e.g., HashiCorp Vault, AWS Secrets Manager), or encrypted configuration vaults — not just obfuscation
