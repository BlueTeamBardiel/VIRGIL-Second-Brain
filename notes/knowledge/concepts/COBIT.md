# COBIT

## What it is
Think of COBIT as the air traffic control system for IT governance — it doesn't fly the planes, but it defines the routes, responsibilities, and rules that keep everything from crashing. COBIT (Control Objectives for Business and Related Technology) is a framework developed by ISACA that aligns IT processes with business goals through defined controls, metrics, and governance structures. It bridges the gap between technical operations and executive decision-making by organizing IT into domains with measurable objectives.

## Why it matters
During a post-breach audit at a financial institution, regulators discovered that no one could demonstrate who was responsible for approving privileged access changes — a classic governance failure. Had COBIT's "Manage Identity and Access" process (DSS06) been implemented, clear ownership, approval workflows, and audit trails would have existed. COBIT gives auditors and security teams a structured language to prove that controls exist, are assigned, and are monitored.

## Key facts
- COBIT 2019 organizes governance around **40 governance and management objectives** grouped into domains (e.g., Align, Plan, Organize; Build, Acquire, Implement)
- It separates **governance** (setting direction, evaluating) from **management** (planning, building, running, monitoring) — a distinction tested on exams
- COBIT maps to other frameworks including **ISO 27001, NIST CSF, and ITIL**, making it a meta-framework for compliance alignment
- Uses **Capability Maturity Models** to score process maturity on a 0–5 scale, helping organizations benchmark and improve controls
- Maintained by **ISACA**, the same body that issues the CISA and CRISC certifications — COBIT knowledge is heavily tested on both
