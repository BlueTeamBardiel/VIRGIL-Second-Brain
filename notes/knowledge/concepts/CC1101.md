# CC1101

## What it is
Think of it as a Swiss Army radio transmitter small enough to hide in a keychain — a single chip that speaks dozens of wireless dialects on demand. The CC1101 is a Texas Instruments sub-1 GHz RF transceiver IC capable of operating across 300–348 MHz, 387–464 MHz, and 779–928 MHz frequency bands, supporting modulation schemes including FSK, GFSK, ASK, OOK, and MSK. It is widely embedded in IoT sensors, garage door openers, car key fobs, smart meters, and industrial control systems.

## Why it matters
Paired with a Raspberry Pi or Arduino, the CC1101 becomes a $5 replay attack platform against fixed-code RF systems. An attacker can capture a garage door or gate opener transmission, then retransmit it verbatim to gain physical access — a classic replay attack that bypasses systems lacking rolling codes or cryptographic challenge-response. Defenders counter this by auditing RF-dependent physical access controls and enforcing rolling code protocols (like KeeLoq) instead of static codes.

## Key facts
- Operates in unlicensed ISM bands (315 MHz, 433 MHz, 868 MHz, 915 MHz) commonly used by alarm systems, TPMS, and smart home devices
- Supports data rates from 0.6 to 600 kBaud, making it capable of both slow sensor polling and faster telemetry capture
- SPI interface allows direct integration with microcontrollers, lowering the barrier for DIY RF attack tooling significantly
- Used in platforms like the Flipper Zero as the core sub-GHz radio module enabling signal capture and replay
- Fixed-code RF systems (no rolling codes) are trivially compromised with CC1101-based capture-replay setups
