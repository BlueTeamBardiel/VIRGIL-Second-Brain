# CWE-290

## What it is
Like a bouncer who checks your face but not your ID — and a attacker just holds up a photo — Authentication Bypass by Spoofing occurs when a system grants access based on easily forged identity signals (IP addresses, hostnames, referrer headers) without verifying their authenticity. The system trusts *what you claim to be* rather than *proving who you are*.

## Why it matters
In 2012, attackers exploited IP-based authentication in MongoDB deployments — many DBs trusted connections from `127.0.0.1` (localhost), so attackers who could spoof or route traffic through the local interface bypassed authentication entirely and exfiltrated millions of records. Defense requires cryptographic proof of identity (mutual TLS, signed tokens), not address-based trust.

## Key facts
- **Root cause**: Trusting network-layer attributes (source IP, MAC address, HTTP headers like `X-Forwarded-For`) that can be forged or manipulated by an adversary
- **Common vulnerable patterns**: `if request.remote_addr == "10.0.0.1": grant_admin_access()` — IP allowlisting without cryptographic authentication
- **Attack vectors**: ARP spoofing, IP spoofing, header injection, DNS spoofing — all allow impersonation of a "trusted" identity signal
- **Differs from CWE-287** (Improper Authentication): CWE-290 is the *specific mechanism* of bypassing auth via spoofed identity, not just a general authentication failure
- **Mitigation**: Use mutual TLS certificates, signed JWTs, or challenge-response mechanisms — anything requiring possession of a secret, not just knowledge of an address
