# Onboarding

## What it is
Like issuing a new employee a keycard, parking pass, and network login all on their first day — onboarding is the formal process of provisioning access, accounts, and permissions to a user (or system) entering an organization's environment. Precisely, it encompasses identity creation, role-based access assignment, device enrollment, and security awareness training for new personnel or integrated systems.

## Why it matters
Poor onboarding controls are a common entry point for privilege creep and insider threats. In a real-world scenario, a contractor onboarded with full-time employee permissions — because no one scoped their role correctly — may retain access to sensitive HR data long after their project ends. This is exactly the failure pattern that least-privilege and formal provisioning workflows are designed to prevent.

## Key facts
- Onboarding should follow the **principle of least privilege**: grant only the minimum access required for the user's specific role from day one.
- **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)** is the standard framework used during onboarding to map job functions to permission sets, reducing manual error.
- Security awareness training is considered a mandatory onboarding component under frameworks like **NIST SP 800-53** and **ISO/IEC 27001**.
- Onboarding must be paired with a formal **offboarding** process; unrevoked accounts from missed offboarding steps are a leading cause of unauthorized access incidents.
- Device enrollment during onboarding typically involves **Mobile Device Management (MDM)** or endpoint registration to ensure devices meet security baselines before network access is granted.
