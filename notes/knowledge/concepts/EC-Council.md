# EC-Council

## What it is
Think of EC-Council like a driving school that specifically trains people to think like car thieves — so they can better secure parking lots. EC-Council (International Council of E-Commerce Consultants) is a US-based cybersecurity certification body founded in 2001, best known for creating the Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH) certification and formalizing offensive security training for defensive purposes.

## Why it matters
When a financial institution hires a penetration tester to validate their web application firewall before a PCI-DSS audit, that tester often holds a CEH — demonstrating they understand attack methodologies like SQL injection, session hijacking, and malware deployment within a structured, legal framework. EC-Council certifications provide a verifiable credential that organizations use to vet whether a security professional can think offensively to defend proactively.

## Key facts
- **CEH (Certified Ethical Hacker)** is EC-Council's flagship certification, covering 20 attack domains including reconnaissance, scanning, exploitation, and covering tracks
- **CHFI (Computer Hacking Forensic Investigator)** is their digital forensics cert, relevant when preserving evidence chains for legal proceedings
- **CPENT (Certified Penetration Testing Professional)** replaced the older Licensed Penetration Tester (LPT) as their advanced hands-on pentest credential
- EC-Council certifications are **DoD 8570/8140 approved**, meaning U.S. military and federal contractors can use CEH to satisfy IAT and IAM baseline requirements
- CEH emphasizes a **structured 5-phase hacking methodology**: Reconnaissance → Scanning → Gaining Access → Maintaining Access → Covering Tracks
