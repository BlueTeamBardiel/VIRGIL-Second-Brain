# Decentralized Governance

## What it is
Like a medieval guild system where no single king dictates rules — each guild enforces its own standards, and power is distributed across many independent nodes. Decentralized governance is a model where security policies, decision-making authority, and controls are distributed across multiple business units, geographic regions, or autonomous entities rather than managed from a single central authority. Each unit owns responsibility for its own security posture within a broader framework of agreed-upon standards.

## Why it matters
In 2020, the SolarWinds supply chain attack succeeded partly because thousands of organizations had siloed, decentralized security teams that independently trusted the same vendor update — with no centralized threat intelligence coordination to flag the anomaly. Conversely, decentralized governance in cloud environments enables individual development teams to manage their own IAM policies, which accelerates delivery but creates shadow IT risks and policy drift that attackers exploit through misconfigured S3 buckets or overprivileged service accounts.

## Key facts
- **Policy fragmentation** is the primary risk: decentralized units may implement inconsistent controls, creating compliance gaps across frameworks like NIST CSF or ISO 27001
- **Federated identity** is a common technical implementation — organizations share authentication standards (SAML, OIDC) while each entity manages its own identity provider
- **Risk ownership** shifts to business unit leaders rather than a central CISO, which can improve accountability or create blind spots depending on security maturity
- **Blockchain-based DAOs** (Decentralized Autonomous Organizations) use smart contracts to encode governance rules, making policy changes tamper-evident but potentially slow to patch when vulnerabilities emerge
- On **CySA+ exams**, decentralized governance appears in questions about organizational security architecture, third-party risk management, and data sovereignty challenges
