# Mobile Device Management

## What it is
Think of MDM like a remote control for every company-issued phone and tablet — IT can mute, wipe, or reconfigure any device from a central dashboard without touching it physically. Mobile Device Management (MDM) is a centralized platform that enforces security policies, manages configurations, and controls access on mobile endpoints across an organization. It operates through an agent installed on the device that communicates with a management server.

## Why it matters
In 2020, a healthcare worker lost an unencrypted company phone containing thousands of patient records. With MDM in place, the security team could have issued a remote wipe within minutes, rendering the data inaccessible before anyone could extract it. Without MDM, that device became a HIPAA violation waiting to happen.

## Key facts
- **Remote wipe** removes all data from a lost or stolen device; a **selective wipe** removes only corporate data, leaving personal data intact — critical distinction for BYOD environments
- MDM can enforce **containerization**, separating corporate apps and data from personal apps to prevent data leakage between the two
- **Geofencing** capabilities allow MDM to trigger policy changes (like disabling the camera) when a device enters or leaves a defined geographic boundary
- MDM is a component of the broader **Unified Endpoint Management (UEM)** framework, which also covers desktops, IoT, and wearables
- Common MDM enrollment methods include **DEP (Device Enrollment Program)** for Apple or **zero-touch enrollment** for Android — both allow out-of-box corporate provisioning
