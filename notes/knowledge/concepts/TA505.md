# TA505

## What it is
Think of TA505 like a professional logistics company that ships malware for hire — they don't care what's in the package, only that it gets delivered at scale. TA505 is a financially motivated, prolific threat actor group (active since at least 2014) known for operating large-scale spam campaigns and distributing commodity malware including banking trojans, ransomware, and remote access tools.

## Why it matters
In 2019, TA505 targeted hundreds of financial institutions and retail organizations globally using Clop ransomware delivered via malicious email attachments containing FlawedAmmyy RAT as a precursor — establishing persistence before deploying ransomware to maximize damage. Defenders who monitor for unusual email volume spikes and macro-enabled document downloads can catch TA505's delivery infrastructure before the payload executes.

## Key facts
- TA505 is credited with distributing **Dridex** banking trojan, **Locky** ransomware, **FlawedAmmyy RAT**, **ServHelper** backdoor, and **Clop** ransomware across their operational history
- Their primary initial access vector is **malicious spam (malspam)** using macro-laced Office documents — often impersonating shipping invoices or financial statements
- Classified under **MITRE ATT&CK** with techniques including T1566.001 (Spearphishing Attachment) and T1204.002 (Malicious File execution)
- TA505 frequently uses **legitimate remote administration tools** (like AnyDesk and Ammyy Admin) to blend into normal traffic — a living-off-the-land style approach
- They are believed to operate out of **Russia** and are tracked separately from but associated with the FIN11 cluster by some threat intelligence vendors
