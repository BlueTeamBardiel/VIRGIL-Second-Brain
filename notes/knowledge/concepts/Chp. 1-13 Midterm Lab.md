# Chp. 1-13 Midterm Lab

## What it is
Think of this like a flight simulator that tests whether a pilot can actually land the plane — not just recite the checklist. A midterm lab in cybersecurity courses covering Chapters 1–13 is a cumulative, hands-on practical assessment that validates applied competency across foundational domains including threats, vulnerabilities, network security, cryptography, identity management, and security architecture.

## Why it matters
In a real SOC environment, an analyst who memorizes definitions but cannot correlate a port scan with a reconnaissance phase will miss an active intrusion. Midterm labs simulate exactly this pressure — requiring students to perform tasks like configuring access controls, analyzing packet captures, or identifying attack indicators — mirroring the workflow of an actual incident response or security audit.

## Key facts
- Chapters 1–13 of Security+ curricula typically span: threat actors, attack types, social engineering, cryptography basics, PKI, network protocols, wireless security, cloud concepts, identity/access management, and risk management
- Lab assessments often require live tool usage: Wireshark for traffic analysis, Nmap for network scanning, or OpenSSL for certificate inspection
- The CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability) serves as the evaluative framework underlying most scenario-based lab questions
- Expect scenario-to-concept mapping: given a symptom (e.g., ARP cache poisoning output), identify the attack, the layer it operates on, and the appropriate countermeasure
- Time management is a tested skill — labs simulate real triage conditions where incomplete analysis has consequences
