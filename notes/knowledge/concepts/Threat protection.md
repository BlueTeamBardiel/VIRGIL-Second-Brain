# Threat protection

## What it is
Like a castle with a moat, drawbridge, archers, and interior guards — no single layer is trusted to hold alone — threat protection is the layered, coordinated set of technologies and processes designed to detect, block, and respond to malicious activity across an organization's attack surface. It spans prevention, detection, and response across endpoints, networks, email, and cloud environments working in concert.

## Why it matters
In the 2021 Colonial Pipeline ransomware attack, attackers entered through a compromised VPN credential — bypassing perimeter defenses entirely. A mature threat protection strategy with endpoint detection and response (EDR), behavioral analytics, and lateral movement detection could have flagged the anomalous activity before ransomware deployed, limiting the blast radius.

## Key facts
- **Defense-in-depth** is the architectural principle behind threat protection — no single control is sufficient; controls overlap intentionally
- **EDR** (Endpoint Detection and Response) provides continuous endpoint telemetry and automated containment, going beyond traditional antivirus signature matching
- **SIEM platforms** aggregate logs and apply correlation rules to surface threats that individual controls miss — central to threat protection at scale
- **Threat intelligence feeds** (IOCs, TTPs mapped to MITRE ATT&CK) allow threat protection systems to proactively block known-bad infrastructure and behaviors
- **Zero Trust architecture** reinforces threat protection by assuming breach — enforcing least privilege and continuous verification rather than trusting internal network position
