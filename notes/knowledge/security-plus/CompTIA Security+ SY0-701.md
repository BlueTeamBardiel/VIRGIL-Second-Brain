# CompTIA Security+ SY0-701

## What it is
Think of it as a driver's license for cybersecurity professionals — it doesn't make you a Formula 1 racer, but it proves you understand the rules of the road and can operate safely. CompTIA Security+ SY0-701 is a vendor-neutral, entry-level certification that validates foundational knowledge across threat management, cryptography, identity, network security, and compliance, updated in 2023 to emphasize hybrid cloud environments and zero trust architecture.

## Why it matters
A hiring manager at a defense contractor receives 200 resumes for a junior SOC analyst role — Security+ is often the baseline filter, and DoD Directive 8140 mandates it for certain government IT positions. In practice, a certified analyst who understands the exam's coverage of incident response phases will correctly prioritize containment before eradication during a ransomware event, rather than immediately wiping infected systems and destroying forensic evidence.

## Key facts
- **Five exam domains (2023):** General Security Concepts (12%), Threats/Vulnerabilities/Mitigations (22%), Security Architecture (18%), Security Operations (28%), Security Program Management & Oversight (20%)
- **Exam format:** 90 questions maximum, 90-minute time limit, passing score of 750 on a 100–900 scale; includes performance-based questions (PBQs) requiring hands-on simulations
- **SY0-701 vs SY0-601:** New version added AI/ML threat vectors, emphasized zero trust and software supply chain attacks (e.g., SolarWinds-style compromises)
- **DoD 8140 approved:** Fulfills IAT Level II requirements, making it mandatory for many federal and military IT roles
- **Renewal:** Valid for 3 years; maintained via 50 Continuing Education Units (CEUs) or by passing a higher-level exam
