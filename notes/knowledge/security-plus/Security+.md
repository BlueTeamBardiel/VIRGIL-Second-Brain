# Security+

## What it is
Think of Security+ like a driver's license for cybersecurity professionals — it proves you understand the rules of the road before anyone hands you the keys to a production network. Formally, it is a vendor-neutral, entry-level certification from CompTIA that validates foundational knowledge across domains including threats, cryptography, identity management, network security, and incident response. It is often the baseline requirement employers use to filter job candidates for roles like SOC analyst, sysadmin, or IT auditor.

## Why it matters
A hiring manager at a defense contractor receives 200 applications for a junior security analyst position; Security+ immediately filters the pool because DoD 8570/8140 mandates it for personnel handling classified information systems at IAT Level II. Without it, a candidate cannot legally be placed in that role regardless of their practical skills — making the certification a literal gate to employment in government and federal contracting work.

## Key facts
- **DoD 8570/8140 compliance**: Security+ satisfies IAT Level II and IAM Level I requirements, making it mandatory for many government and military IT positions
- **Exam code**: SY0-701 is the current version (released November 2023), covering five domains: General Security Concepts, Threats/Vulnerabilities/Mitigations, Security Architecture, Security Operations, and Program Management
- **No prerequisites required**, but CompTIA recommends Network+ and two years of IT experience before attempting
- **Passing score**: 750 out of 900 on a scaled scoring model; exam includes up to 90 questions with multiple-choice and performance-based question (PBQ) formats
- **Certification validity**: 3 years, renewable via CompTIA's Continuing Education (CE) program — 50 CEUs required per renewal cycle
