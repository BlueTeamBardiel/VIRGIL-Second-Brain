# CompTIA A+

## What it is
Like a mechanic's license proving you can diagnose and repair engines before you tune race cars, CompTIA A+ certifies foundational IT knowledge — hardware, operating systems, networking basics, and troubleshooting — before you specialize in security. It is a vendor-neutral, entry-level certification from CompTIA covering two exams (Core 1: 220-1101 and Core 2: 220-1102) that validates competency in supporting and maintaining end-user technology environments.

## Why it matters
A help desk technician without A+ fundamentals might mishandle a ransomware-infected endpoint — failing to isolate the machine from the network before attempting removal, allowing lateral spread. A+ training explicitly covers proper incident response basics: recognizing symptoms of malware, quarantining systems, and escalating appropriately. These ground-floor skills prevent catastrophic mistakes that security tools alone cannot compensate for.

## Key facts
- **Two exam structure**: Core 1 (220-1101) focuses on hardware, mobile devices, networking, and virtualization; Core 2 (220-1102) focuses on OS, security, software troubleshooting, and operational procedures
- **DoD 8570 approved**: A+ Core 2 satisfies IAT Level I requirements, meaning U.S. military and federal contractors can use it for baseline privileged-user roles
- **Exam format**: Each exam has up to 90 questions (multiple choice + performance-based), 90-minute time limit, passing score of 700/900
- **Security domain in Core 2**: Covers physical security, malware removal best practices, browser security, and basic access controls — directly feeding into Security+ concepts
- **Renewal**: Certification expires after 3 years; renew via CEUs, higher-cert exams, or retaking the exam
