# CySA+ Problem Areas

## What it is
Like a pilot's pre-flight checklist that reveals which instruments are broken before takeoff, CySA+ problem areas are the specific domains where candidates consistently lose points — revealing gaps between surface-level familiarity and operational depth. These include threat and vulnerability management, software and systems security, security operations and monitoring, incident response, and compliance/assessment — the five core domains where conceptual confusion causes exam failure.

## Why it matters
A SOC analyst preparing for CySA+ who memorizes tool names but doesn't understand *when* to use SIEM correlation rules versus behavioral analytics will fail scenario-based questions — the same gap that causes real analysts to miss lateral movement during an actual intrusion. The exam deliberately tests decision-making under ambiguous conditions, not just vocabulary recall.

## Key facts
- **Incident response sequencing** is a top stumbling block — candidates confuse "containment before eradication" order and misidentify when to escalate versus remediate independently
- **Threat intelligence** questions require distinguishing STIX/TAXII (structured sharing formats) from sources like ISACs — knowing *what* they do, not just their names
- **Vulnerability prioritization** trips candidates who apply CVSS scores in isolation without factoring in asset criticality or exploitability in context (CVSS ≠ remediation priority)
- **Log analysis and correlation** questions require recognizing attack patterns (beaconing, failed auth spikes) from raw log snippets — not just knowing that SIEMs exist
- **Compliance frameworks** — candidates confuse NIST CSF (voluntary, risk-based) with RMF (federal mandate) and misapply controls-versus-controls-framework distinctions
