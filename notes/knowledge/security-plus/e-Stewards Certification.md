# e-Stewards Certification

## What it is
Like a chain-of-custody seal on evidence bags that proves no one tampered with the contents, e-Stewards is an audited certification proving electronics recyclers handle e-waste without dumping toxics or exposing data. Specifically, it is a third-party certification program created by the Basel Action Network (BAN) that holds recyclers to strict environmental and data security standards when disposing of end-of-life electronics.

## Why it matters
When an organization retires old servers without verifying their recycler's credentials, hard drives containing customer PII can end up in informal scrap markets in Southeast Asia — where they are recovered and mined for credentials, trade secrets, or PHI. Choosing an e-Stewards certified recycler provides documented assurance that storage media is either securely wiped to NIST 800-88 standards or physically destroyed before the hardware leaves the chain of custody.

## Key facts
- e-Stewards certification requires third-party audits and prohibits export of hazardous e-waste to developing countries, directly addressing both environmental law and data breach risk
- Certified recyclers must ensure data destruction meets recognized standards (e.g., NIST SP 800-88 or physical shredding) — making it relevant to media sanitization policy
- It is distinct from R2 (Responsible Recycling) certification; e-Stewards is generally considered the stricter standard for data security requirements
- Under frameworks like HIPAA and PCI-DSS, organizations must demonstrate due diligence in media disposal — using an e-Stewards recycler provides documented evidence for audits
- Failure to use certified recyclers has resulted in real breaches; recovered hard drives from recyclers have been sources of data exposure incidents documented by investigative journalists
