# Shadow Brokers

## What it is
Imagine someone broke into a master locksmith's workshop, stole every skeleton key ever made, then auctioned them on the internet — that's essentially what happened here. The Shadow Brokers were an anonymous threat actor group that publicly leaked NSA-developed hacking tools and zero-day exploits between 2016 and 2017, exposing some of the most sophisticated offensive cyberweapons ever created by a nation-state.

## Why it matters
The Shadow Brokers leak directly enabled the WannaCry ransomware attack of May 2017, which infected over 200,000 systems across 150 countries — including UK National Health Service hospitals that had to turn away patients. The leaked exploit **EternalBlue** (targeting SMBv1 via MS17-010) was weaponized within weeks of the leak, demonstrating how stolen nation-state tools can cascade into global civilian infrastructure damage.

## Key facts
- The group leaked tools attributed to the NSA's **Equation Group**, considered one of the most advanced APT actors ever documented
- **EternalBlue** exploited a buffer overflow in Windows SMBv1 (CVE-2017-0144); Microsoft patched it in MS17-010 before the full leak, but millions of unpatched systems remained vulnerable
- EternalBlue was used in both **WannaCry** (North Korea/Lazarus Group) and **NotPetya** (Russia/Sandworm) — two of the most destructive cyberattacks in history
- The Shadow Brokers offered tools for sale initially, then released them freely — suggesting motivations beyond simple financial gain (possibly geopolitical signaling)
- This event triggered a broader policy debate about **vulnerability equities process (VEP)** — whether governments should stockpile or disclose discovered zero-days
