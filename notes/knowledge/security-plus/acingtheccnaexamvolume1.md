# acingtheccnaexamvolume1

## What it is
Think of this like a flight simulator manual for network engineers — it doesn't just tell you what buttons do, it trains you to fly under pressure. *Acing the CCNA Exam, Volume 1* is a structured study resource targeting Cisco's CCNA certification, covering foundational networking concepts including IP addressing, subnetting, routing protocols, and switching fundamentals essential for building and defending network infrastructure.

## Why it matters
A penetration tester exploiting a misconfigured OSPF neighbor relationship or performing VLAN hopping attacks is leveraging the exact topology knowledge this material teaches — understanding trunk ports, STP, and routing tables is prerequisite knowledge for both attackers and defenders. Network defenders who lack this foundational literacy often misconfigure ACLs, leaving lateral movement paths wide open inside a corporate network.

## Key facts
- Covers OSI and TCP/IP model layers — Security+ expects you to map attacks (e.g., ARP spoofing = Layer 2, IP spoofing = Layer 3) to the correct layer
- Subnetting mastery is directly applicable to network segmentation, a core defense strategy for limiting breach blast radius
- Switching concepts like VLANs and STP are prerequisites for understanding VLAN hopping and STP manipulation attacks (relevant to CySA+ network analysis scenarios)
- IPv4 addressing, CIDR notation, and NAT/PAT concepts appear in firewall rule analysis questions on both Security+ and CySA+
- Understanding routing protocols (RIP, OSPF, EIGRP) helps identify route poisoning and BGP hijacking threats in real network defense contexts
