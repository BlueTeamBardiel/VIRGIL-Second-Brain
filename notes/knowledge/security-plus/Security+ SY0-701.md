# Security+ SY0-701

## What it is
Think of it as a driver's license for cybersecurity professionals — it proves you know the rules of the road before you're trusted behind the wheel. Security+ SY0-701 is CompTIA's vendor-neutral, entry-level cybersecurity certification covering threat detection, network security, cryptography, identity management, and risk frameworks, updated in 2023 to reflect the current threat landscape.

## Why it matters
A hiring manager at a defense contractor must verify that a new SOC analyst understands baseline security concepts before granting access to sensitive systems — SY0-701 serves as that verified baseline. The exam's inclusion of zero-trust architecture and cloud security topics directly reflects gaps that attackers exploited in breaches like SolarWinds, where insufficient network segmentation and privileged access controls were root causes.

## Key facts
- **Five exam domains:** General Security Concepts (12%), Threats/Vulnerabilities/Mitigations (22%), Security Architecture (18%), Security Operations (28%), and Security Program Management & Oversight (20%)
- **Exam format:** Maximum 90 questions, mix of multiple-choice and performance-based questions (PBQs), 90-minute time limit, passing score of 750/900
- **DoD 8570 approved:** Satisfies IAT Level II and IAM Level I requirements, making it mandatory for many U.S. federal and military IT roles
- **New in 701 vs 601:** Heavier emphasis on zero-trust, hybrid cloud environments, automation/orchestration, and AI/ML threat detection — reflects post-pandemic infrastructure shifts
- **Recommended prerequisite:** CompTIA Network+ and roughly two years of IT/security experience, though it is not formally required to sit the exam
