# CompTIA

## What it is
Think of CompTIA like the DMV for cybersecurity professionals — it doesn't build the roads or drive the cars, but it certifies that you're qualified to operate in the field. CompTIA (Computing Technology Industry Association) is a vendor-neutral, nonprofit trade association that issues globally recognized IT and cybersecurity certifications. Unlike vendor-specific certs (Cisco's CCNA, Microsoft's AZ-900), CompTIA credentials validate foundational skills applicable across any platform or environment.

## Why it matters
The U.S. Department of Defense Directive 8570/8140 mandates that personnel in Information Assurance roles hold approved certifications — and CompTIA Security+ is explicitly listed as a baseline requirement. This means a misconfigured system managed by an uncertified contractor could trigger compliance violations, and during a breach investigation, credential gaps in staff can expose an organization to regulatory liability. Security+ also signals to hiring managers that a candidate understands foundational attack vectors, risk management, and cryptography concepts without needing platform-specific retraining.

## Key facts
- **Security+** (SY0-701) is the most widely held entry-level cybersecurity cert and DoD 8570 IAT Level II compliant
- **CySA+** (CS0-003) targets intermediate blue-team analysts, focusing on threat detection, SIEM analysis, and vulnerability management
- **PenTest+** covers offensive techniques including reconnaissance, exploitation, and reporting — sits between Security+ and OSCP in difficulty
- CompTIA certs are **vendor-neutral**, meaning skills apply to AWS, Azure, on-prem, and hybrid environments equally
- Certifications are valid for **3 years** and require Continuing Education Units (CEUs) or retesting for renewal via the CertMaster CE program
